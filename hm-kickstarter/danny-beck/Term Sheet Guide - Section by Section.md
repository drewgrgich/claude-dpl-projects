# Simplified Term Sheet — Section-by-Section Guide

**What this document covers:** The "Hamsters & Monsters — KS1 Only" simplified term sheet. Each section is explained in plain language — what it does, why it's there, and what it means for you.

---

## Section 0: Parties

**Purpose:** Identifies who's involved in the deal.

- **Publisher/Licensee:** Design Play Labs, LLC — the company making and selling the card game.
- **Artist/IP Owner/Licensor:** Danny Beck — that's you. You own the Hamsters & Monsters universe and you're licensing it to them.

This section establishes the two sides of the agreement so there's no ambiguity about who has what obligations.

---

## Section 1: Purpose

**Purpose:** Explains what this term sheet actually is and what deal it's leading toward.

It's a summary of business terms for two things bundled together: (1) a commission agreement where you create art for the game, and (2) an exclusive license letting the Publisher use your Hamsters & Monsters IP for a tabletop card game. This simplified version covers **KS1 only** — any future products would be handled through a separate negotiation (Section 8).

Importantly, this is a term sheet, not the final contract. The final "Definitive Agreement" comes later and will be the legally binding version.

---

## Section 2: Definitions

**Purpose:** Sets the precise meaning of key terms used throughout the document so both sides are on the same page.

The important ones to know:

- **Licensed Property** — your pre-existing Hamsters & Monsters universe IP (what's listed in Exhibit B). This is what you're licensing to them.
- **Product** — the base card game. That's it. Not expansions, not merch, not a video game. Just the one card game.
- **Field of Use** — tabletop game products and related marketing. Everything else (apparel, apps, film/TV, etc.) is off-limits unless separately agreed.
- **KS1** — the first Kickstarter campaign, including pledge manager and late pledges.
- **Net Receipts** — the money the Publisher actually takes in from the Product after subtracting platform fees, processing fees, and refunds. This is the number your royalty is calculated on.
- **Captured Gross Funding** — the total money raised through Kickstarter before platform/processing fees are deducted. This is the number that triggers your profit-share kicker tiers.
- **Franchise Marks** — your "Hamsters & Monsters" brand (name, logos, trade dress). You own these.
- **Product Marks** — the game-specific branding the Publisher creates (game title, game logo). They own these.

## Section 3: Commissioned Works

**Purpose:** Covers the art and creative deliverables you're being hired to produce.

This section says you'll create the deliverables listed in Exhibit A (faction art, card backs, box art, etc.) for the fees stated there. Key protections for you:

- **3.1 Revisions** — You owe them two rounds of revisions per deliverable, not unlimited. If they change creative direction entirely, that's new scope at additional cost.
- **3.2 Delivery/Acceptance** — They have 7 business days to review your work. If they don't respond, it's automatically accepted. This prevents them from sitting on your deliverables indefinitely.
- **3.3 Payment** — Milestones are in Exhibit A (40/30/30 split).
- **3.4 Hourly Add-Ons** — Any work beyond the fixed-fee deliverables is $50/hour, capped at 15 hours/month, and requires prior written approval. This keeps scope creep in check.

## Section 4: Independent Counsel Stipend

**Purpose:** The Publisher pays you $750 when the final agreement is signed so you can have your own lawyer review it.

This is non-refundable and doesn't reduce any other payment owed to you. It's there to make sure you can afford independent legal advice before locking in the deal.

## Section 5: License Grant

**Purpose:** This is the core of the IP deal — it defines exactly what rights the Publisher gets to use your Hamsters & Monsters universe, and what they don't.

- **5.1 Grant** — The Publisher gets an exclusive, worldwide license to use your IP, but *only* for the Product (the card game) and *only* in the Field of Use (tabletop games and marketing). They can't use it for anything else.
- **5.2 Trademark License** — They can use the "Hamsters & Monsters" name and branding on the game, but only for this product. No apparel, toys, apps, film, or anything else without a separate agreement.
- **5.2.1 Quality Control** — You get approval rights over how your brand first appears on the box front, rulebook cover, and Kickstarter page. They have 10 business days to get your sign-off; if they don't hear back, it's deemed approved. After that initial approval, anything consistent with what you already approved doesn't need a second round.
- **5.2.2 Registration & Filing** — You keep control over registering your Franchise Marks. They handle their own Product Marks. Neither side steps on the other's trademarks.
- **5.3 Permitted Modifications** — The Publisher can make production adjustments (cropping, color correction, adding layout) as long as they stay consistent with brand integrity. This is standard for print production.
- **5.4 Sublicensing/Assignment** — The Publisher can bring in manufacturers, distributors, etc. They can also transfer the deal if the company is sold, but the buyer has to honor all the same terms.
- **5.5 Sell-Off** — If the deal ends, the Publisher gets 12 months to sell through existing inventory. They still owe you royalties on those sales.

## Section 6: License Economics (KS1)

**Purpose:** This is the money section — how you get paid beyond the commission work.

- **6.1 Royalty** — 5% of Net Receipts on the Product. No minimum guarantee for KS1 (meaning they don't owe you a floor payment, but you earn on every dollar that comes in).
- **6.2 Profit-Share Kicker** — Bonus payments triggered by how much the Kickstarter raises (Captured Gross Funding):
  - $50K–$74,999 raised → you get $1,000
  - $75K–$149,999 raised → you get $3,000
  - $150K+ raised → you get $6,000
  - Highest tier only, no stacking. Paid within 90 days of KS1 ending. This is on top of royalties.
- **6.3 Net Receipts** — Clarifies the royalty math: cash in, minus platform fees, processing fees, and refunds. Shipping, taxes, and pass-through charges don't count. Bundles get allocated by standalone pricing.
- **6.4 Reporting, Payment, Audit** — They report quarterly (45 days after quarter end), pay within 30 days of that, and you can audit once per year. If they underpaid by more than 10%, they cover audit costs.
- **6.5 Assignment/Sublicensing Consideration** — If the Publisher ever gets paid specifically for sublicensing or assigning your IP rights (not just normal product sales), that money counts as Net Receipts and you get your 5%.

## Section 7: Term and Reversion

**Purpose:** Defines how long the deal lasts and what triggers your IP rights coming back to you.

- **7.1 KS1 Phase** — The license starts when the deal is signed and runs through KS1 fulfillment plus 12 months. "Fulfillment Completion" means either 95% of backer rewards shipped or a backstop date (TBD), whichever comes first.
- **7.2 Reversion Triggers** — Your IP rights come back if:
  1. They fail to launch KS1 by a set deadline (after notice and cure period)
  2. The Product is out of print for 12 straight months after fulfillment (with 90-day cure)
  3. They stop reporting or paying and don't fix it within 45 days of notice
  4. They go bankrupt

This section is a key protection — it means your IP can't be locked up indefinitely if the Publisher stops actively using it.

## Section 8: Right of First Negotiation (Future Products)

**Purpose:** If the Publisher wants to do more with Hamsters & Monsters (expansions, reprints, new games), they have to come to you first and negotiate in good faith for 60 days before talking to anyone else.

This is **not** an option or a commitment. Neither side is obligated to agree on terms. If 60 days pass without a deal, both sides are free. This replaces the original term sheet's built-in KS2 structure — instead of locking in future terms now, you'd negotiate them later with the benefit of knowing how KS1 actually performed.

## Section 9: IG/TikTok Story Campaign

**Purpose:** If included, you post a minimum of 10 stories over the 10 weeks leading up to KS1, at $50 per story.

Schedule gets locked in advance. You provide proof of posting within 24 hours. If a post doesn't happen, you repost within 7 days or refund that placement. This is a straightforward social media deliverable with clear accountability on both sides.

## Section 10: Credits & Portfolio

**Purpose:** You get credited as artist/IP owner wherever standard, and you can use the commissioned work in your portfolio after it's been publicly revealed.

Before public reveal, you keep it confidential. This is standard creative industry practice — the Publisher gets to control the timing of their marketing, and you get to show off your work once it's out there.

---

## Section 11: Ownership; Logo; Warranties

**Purpose:** Clarifies who owns what, and provides a basic warranty.

- **Your ownership:** You keep all rights to the Hamsters & Monsters universe IP and the Franchise Marks. The license doesn't transfer ownership.
- **Publisher's ownership:** They own the game rules/mechanics, their own content, and the Product Marks (game title branding, etc.). They do **not** own your Franchise Marks.
- **Game Logo:** This one deliverable is treated as "work made for hire" — the Publisher owns it. If that legal classification doesn't hold, you assign the rights to them. This is the one piece of commissioned art that belongs to them outright.
- **Warranties/Indemnity:** You warrant your work is original and properly licensed (no stolen assets). If a third party sues over your work, you indemnify the Publisher, but the liability is capped at what you've been paid under the deal (except for fraud).

---

## Section 12: Publisher Termination for Convenience

**Purpose:** The Publisher can walk away from the license with 120 days' written notice, but not before KS1 fulfillment is complete.

If they terminate, the sell-off period still applies (12 months to clear inventory), and they still owe you royalties on anything sold during sell-off. This gives the Publisher an exit ramp while making sure you're not left hanging mid-campaign.

---

## Section 13: Non-Binding; Governing Law

**Purpose:** This term sheet is for negotiation purposes only — it's not the final contract. The binding version will be the Definitive Agreement.

Governing law is Arizona, meaning any disputes would be handled under Arizona law.
