# Hamsters & Monsters Term Sheet — Plain English Guide

**For:** Danny Beck
**Document:** HM-Term-Sheet-KS1.md (KS1 Commission + Exclusive License)

## Section 0 — Parties

This deal is between you (Danny Beck, called "Licensor" because you own the IP) and Design Play Labs, LLC (called "Publisher" because they're publishing the game). Straightforward.

## Section 1 — Purpose

This term sheet lays out the business terms for two things bundled together: (1) you creating artwork for the game, and (2) you licensing the Hamsters & Monsters universe so the Publisher can make a tabletop card game. This only covers the first Kickstarter campaign (KS1). If either side wants to do more products after that, you'd negotiate fresh terms (see Section 8).

## Section 2 — Definitions

This section defines all the key terms used throughout the document so there's no ambiguity. The important ones:

- **Licensed Property** — your Hamsters & Monsters universe (characters, settings, factions, visual elements).
- **Product** — the specific game: *Hamsters & Monsters: Contests of Chaos* (base game).
- **Field of Use** — tabletop card games only. Everything else (board games, video games, merch, film/TV, etc.) stays with you unless separately agreed.
- **KS1** — the first Kickstarter campaign, including pledge manager and late pledges.
- **KS1 Pledge Price** — whatever backers pay per unit during KS1 (this is what royalties are based on).
- **Franchise Marks** — your H&M trademarks and logos.
- **Product Marks** — trademarks the Publisher creates for the game itself (like the game title logo).
- **Captured Gross Funding** — total money received from Kickstarter minus refunds/chargebacks (used for the profit-share kicker).
- All dollar amounts are in U.S. dollars.

## Section 3 — Commissioned Works

This covers the actual artwork you'll create.

- **What you're making:** Everything listed in Exhibit A (faction pieces, card back, box art, marketing pack, game logo, stories).
- **Revisions:** You get two rounds of revisions per piece. If the Publisher changes direction entirely, that's new work billed separately.
- **Delivery:** You deliver to a shared folder. Publisher has 7 business days to accept or request revisions. No response = accepted.
- **Payment:** Paid per the milestone schedule in Exhibit A5.
- **Hourly add-ons:** $50/hour with prior written approval, capped at 15 hours/month.
- **Delays:** If either side causes delays, all deadlines shift day-for-day. If Publisher delays cause you to miss two consecutive Kickstarter launch windows, you can terminate with 30 days' notice.

## Section 4 — Independent Counsel Stipend

The Publisher pays you $750 so you can hire your own lawyer to review the deal before signing. Non-refundable, doesn't come out of any other payments.

## Section 5 — License Grant

This is the core IP licensing section. Here's what it means:

- **5.1 — The Grant:** You're giving the Publisher an exclusive, worldwide license to use your H&M universe to make, market, and sell the card game. "Exclusive" means no one else (including you) can make a tabletop card game with this IP during the term.
- **5.2 — Trademark License:** They can use your H&M logos and branding on the game. You review and approve the first public uses (box front, rulebook cover, Kickstarter page header). After that, consistent uses are auto-approved.
- **5.3 — Production Modifications:** They can crop, resize, and adjust color for printing purposes. Normal production stuff.
- **5.4 — Sublicensing/Assignment:** They can sublicense to manufacturers, distributors, etc. If they sell the company, the new owner takes on all obligations (with your written consent, which you can't unreasonably refuse). You also can't transfer the IP during the term without ensuring any successor is bound by the deal.
- **5.5 — Sell-Off:** When the deal ends, they get 12 months to sell remaining inventory (still paying royalties). After that, you can buy leftover inventory at cost, or they destroy it.
- **5.6 — Creative Control:** This is a big one for you. You have approval rights over major narrative additions (new characters, new lore, new factions). For card flavor text and marketing copy, they consult you and give good-faith consideration to your feedback. You'll work together to build an IP Style Guide within 45 days of signing.
- **5.6.2 — Tiered Approvals:** Three tiers — (1) big lore decisions need your approval, (2) flavor text and marketing copy you get consultation rights, (3) rules language and mechanical text is Publisher's call.
- **5.7 — Marketing Assets:** You provide existing marketing art for them to use until the commissioned artwork is done. Then they transition to the new stuff.

## Section 6 — License Economics (KS1)

How you get paid beyond the commission work:

- **6.1 — Royalty:** 5% of the KS1 pledge price per unit, on every unit ordered from the manufacturer — whether it's sold yet or not. No minimum guarantee for KS1.
- **6.2 — Profit-Share Kicker:** Bonus payments based on how much KS1 raises: $1,000 at $50K, $3,000 at $75K, or $6,000 at $150K+. Highest tier only (they don't stack). Paid within 90 days after KS1 ends.
- **6.3 — How royalties are calculated:** Pledge price × units ordered × 5%, calculated separately for each SKU.
- **6.4 — Reporting and payment:** Quarterly reports due 45 days after quarter end. KS1 unit royalties due within 30 days of receiving KS1 funds. Additional unit royalties due when Publisher receives the units. Late payments accrue 1.5% monthly interest. You can audit their books once per year.
- **6.5 — Assignment/Sublicensing consideration:** If they sell or sublicense the rights (not just sell product), you get 5% of what they receive.

## Section 7 — Term and Reversion

- **7.1 — How long it lasts:** From signing through KS1 fulfillment plus 12 months after. Then the license ends unless you negotiate new terms.
- **7.2 — When rights revert to you:** If they miss the KS1 launch deadline (with 30-day cure), if the product goes out of print for 12 months, if they stop paying/reporting for 45 days, or if they go bankrupt.
- **7.3 — If KS1 fails:** If the campaign doesn't fund or gets cancelled, the license terminates automatically. You keep payments for work already delivered.

## Section 8 — Right of First Negotiation

If either side wants to do more H&M card game products after KS1, you give each other 60 days of exclusive negotiation before talking to anyone else. Neither side is obligated to make a deal.

## Section 9 — IG/TikTok Story Campaign

If included, you post at least 10 stories over 10 weeks leading up to KS1 at $50/story. Schedule gets locked per the milestone timeline. If you miss a post, you repost within 7 days or refund that placement.

## Section 10 — Credits & Portfolio

You get standard artist/IP credit on the game. You can show the commissioned work in your portfolio after it's publicly revealed.

## Section 11 — Ownership; Logo; Warranties

- **Your stuff stays yours:** You own the H&M IP, the Franchise Marks, and the Licensed Property.
- **Their stuff stays theirs:** They own the game rules/mechanics and Publisher-created content.
- **Game logo:** This is work-for-hire — the Publisher owns it. But if the deal ends before KS1 launches, the logo reverts to you (see Section 12).
- **Your warranties:** You're confirming you own the IP, it doesn't infringe anyone else's rights, and the commissioned work is original.

## Section 12 — Publisher Termination for Convenience

The Publisher can end the deal with 120 days' notice, but not before fulfillment is complete. Sell-off rights and royalty obligations continue.

**Pre-Launch Termination:** If the deal ends before KS1 launches for any reason, all artwork (including the game logo) reverts to you. You keep payments for delivered work; you refund payments for undelivered work.

**Termination for Material Breach:** Either side can terminate if the other materially breaches and doesn't fix it within 30 days of written notice.

## Section 13 — Independent Contractor

You're a contractor, not an employee. You handle your own taxes and insurance. Publisher issues a 1099.

## Section 14 — Confidentiality

Both sides keep the deal terms and each other's non-public information confidential. Standard exceptions apply (public info, prior knowledge, independent development, legal compulsion). Survives for 2 years after the deal ends.

## Section 15 — Notices and Written Acceptance

Everything important (approvals, notices, changes) has to be in writing via email. Nothing is official until confirmed in writing.

## Section 16 — Non-Binding; Governing Law

This term sheet itself is non-binding — it's a negotiation document. The binding stuff comes in the Definitive Agreement. Arizona law governs. Disputes go to binding arbitration in Maricopa County, Arizona.
