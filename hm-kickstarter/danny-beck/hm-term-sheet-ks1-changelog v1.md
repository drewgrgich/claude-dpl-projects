# Hamsters & Monsters — KS1 Term Sheet Changes

**From:** Drew (Design Play Labs)
**Date:** February 26, 2026
**Re:** Summary of revisions to the KS1 term sheet

Danny — here's a walkthrough of everything I changed from the last draft you saw, along with my reasoning. Nothing here is meant to be adversarial; most of it is about removing hard dates that would break if our timeline shifts, tightening up ambiguities before they become arguments, and making sure the agreement works cleanly for both of us no matter when we actually sign.

---

## 1. Product Name (Section 2)

**Changed:** "Untitled Card Game" → "The Chaos Games"

We have a name now, so the term sheet should reflect it.

---

## 2. Licensor Assignment Restriction (Section 5.4 — new paragraph)

**Added:** If you assign or transfer the Licensed Property during the Term, the successor has to be bound by this agreement, and you give me 30 days' written notice before any transfer.

**Why:** The original only addressed Publisher assignment. This is the mirror image — if you ever sold or transferred the H&M IP while our deal is active, I need to know about it, and the new owner needs to honor the license. Standard provision; protects both sides' expectations.

---

## 3. No-Double-Royalty Clarification (Section 6.1)

**Added:** "For the avoidance of doubt, units produced as part of the KS1 production run for which royalties have already been paid on Net Receipts are not subject to additional royalties upon subsequent resale by Publisher (e.g., convention sales, direct-to-consumer, or retail)."

**Why:** Without this, there's a read where leftover KS1 inventory sold at a convention could trigger a second royalty on the same units. That was never the intent. You get 5% of Net Receipts when the money comes in from KS1; I don't pay again when I sell remaining copies through other channels. This just makes the intent explicit.

---

## 4. Fulfillment Hard Stop (Section 7.1)

**Changed:** "August 30, 2027" → "eighteen (18) months after the KS1 campaign close date"

**Why:** The original hard date assumed a fall 2026 launch. If we launch in spring 2027 instead, August 30 only gives us five months for fulfillment — that's tight for a card game with international shipping. Making it relative to the actual campaign close date gives us the same 18-month runway regardless of when we launch. Same protection for you (there's still a backstop), it just floats with reality.

---

## 5. Reversion Trigger — Launch Deadline (Section 7.2(1))

**Changed:** "October 1, 2026" → "the target KS1 launch date (as determined under Exhibit A4, including any automatic adjustment for late execution)"

**Why:** Same principle as the fulfillment fix. The old version was a fixed date that didn't account for the auto-shift provision we built into Exhibit A4. If we sign late and the launch moves to spring 2027, the reversion trigger needs to move with it — otherwise I'm technically in breach the day after October 1 even though we both agreed the timeline shifted.

---

## 6. Right of First Negotiation — Trigger Mechanism (Section 8)

**Added:** Written notice requirement specifying that the party wishing to proceed describes the proposed product in reasonable detail, and the 60-day clock starts on receipt.

**Why:** The original said we'd negotiate for 60 days but didn't say how the clock starts. That's the kind of ambiguity that causes problems when the game is successful and we both want to do an expansion — "I mentioned it at PAX" shouldn't count as triggering a formal negotiation window. Now it's clean: written notice in, clock starts, 60 days to talk.

---

## 7. Story Schedule Lock Dates (Section 9 and Exhibit A6)

**Changed:** "June 30, 2026" → "per the story schedule lock milestone in Exhibit A4"

**Why:** Another hard date that doesn't flex with the timeline. Exhibit A4 now has the full milestone schedule built on relative dates from the Effective Date. The story schedule lock should reference that same system rather than sitting on a fixed date that might not make sense if we sign later than planned.

---

## 8. Warranties and Indemnity (Section 11)

**Changed:** Removed the specific limited indemnity language (capped at amounts paid, except fraud/willful misconduct). Replaced with: "Indemnification terms to be addressed in the Definitive Agreement."

**Why:** Indemnification is one of those provisions that really needs to be worked out by attorneys in the definitive agreement, not locked in at the term sheet stage. The warranty itself stays — you're still warranting your work is original and properly licensed. I just didn't want us to commit to a specific indemnity cap and structure before our lawyers have had a chance to negotiate it properly. This keeps the door open for a fair outcome on both sides.

---

## 9. Pre-Launch Termination Refinement (Section 12)

**Changed:** Two additions: (a) commission payments are non-refundable only for work that's been "delivered and accepted" per Section 3.2, and (b) payments for deliverables not yet delivered and accepted get refunded within 30 days.

**Why:** The original said commission payments for "delivered work" were non-refundable, but didn't address money paid for work that hadn't been delivered yet. If we terminate pre-launch after paying you a milestone for work you haven't started, it's reasonable that money comes back. And tying "delivered" to the acceptance process in Section 3.2 prevents ambiguity about what counts as delivered. This protects you too — once I've accepted the work, I can't claw back the payment.

---

## 10. Dispute Resolution (Section 14)

**Added:** "Disputes to be resolved by binding arbitration in Maricopa County, Arizona; details to be set forth in the Definitive Agreement."

**Why:** The original specified Arizona law but didn't address how disputes would actually be resolved. Arbitration is faster and cheaper than litigation for a deal this size, and Maricopa County is the logical venue since that's where I'm based. The specifics (arbitration rules, number of arbitrators, etc.) will be worked out in the definitive agreement.

---

## 11. Exhibit A4 — Full Milestone Schedule

**Changed:** Expanded from a placeholder ("Target dates and dependencies to be set in the Definitive Agreement") to a complete milestone schedule with relative deadlines tied to the Effective Date.

**Why:** This was the biggest structural gap in the original. Without a concrete schedule, we had no shared understanding of the production timeline, and the day-for-day delay provisions in Section 3.5 had nothing to anchor to. The schedule is now built on relative dates (ED + 1 week, ED + 4 weeks, etc.) so it works regardless of when we sign. It also includes the auto-shift provision: if we don't sign until after March 31, 2026, everything recalculates to a spring 2027 launch automatically.

---

## 12. Game Logo — Added as Line Item (Exhibit A1 and A5)

**Added:** Game logo as a deliverable in Exhibit A1 at a proposed rate of \$350, with a 50/50 payment split (50% on scope confirmation, 50% on delivery and acceptance) in Exhibit A5.

**Why:** The logo was already referenced in Section 11 (ownership, work-for-hire) and A5 had a conditional note about it, but it wasn't actually listed as a deliverable with a price. Adding it to A1 makes the scope explicit. The \$350 rate matches the faction pieces and card back; the final price is subject to written confirmation once we align on scope. This way it's on the table from the start rather than being an afterthought we have to paper separately.

---

## What I Deliberately Left Alone

A few things I reviewed and decided not to touch:

- **Section 3.5** (cross-references to Section 9 and Exhibit A6 for schedule lock dates) — technically fine since both now reference A4, so the chain works.
- **Section 5.5 sell-off royalties vs. Section 6.1 no-double-royalty** — these are consistent as written. Sell-off royalties apply to future production; KS1 units already paid aren't double-dipped.
- **Section 12, no Licensor termination for convenience** — your protections are adequate through reversion triggers, the two-missed-windows termination right, and Pre-Launch Termination.

---

Happy to talk through any of this. None of it changes the economics or the core deal structure — it's all about making the agreement work cleanly regardless of when we sign and removing ambiguities before they matter.
