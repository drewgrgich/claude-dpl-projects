# What Changed: Original (2/12) vs. Simplified (KS1-Only) Term Sheet

This is a plain-language walkthrough of every difference between the original 2/12/26 term sheet and the new simplified version. Organized so you can walk Danny through it section by section.

---

## The Bottom Line for Danny

**Nothing changes about how Danny gets paid for KS1.** Every dollar amount, every trigger, every timeline that applies to the first Kickstarter campaign is identical. The commission fees, the royalty rate, the profit-share kicker tiers, the counsel stipend — all the same. What changed is that we removed everything related to a hypothetical second campaign (KS2) and replaced it with a simple "let's talk first" clause if we ever want to do more together.

---

## Section-by-Section Comparison

### 1. Definitions — Simplified

**What was there before:** The original defined "KS2," "Licensed Products" (which included expansions, deluxe editions, promo packs, print-and-play, etc.), and a bunch of terms needed to support the KS2 economics (like "MG" for minimum guarantee).

**What's there now:** We dropped the KS2 definition entirely, dropped "Licensed Products" as a separate concept (since there's just the one Product now), and removed the MG definition. Everything is scoped to the base game and KS1.

**Why it matters to Danny:** Less to read, less to negotiate, and less to get his lawyer to review. The definitions that remain are the ones that actually affect what's happening right now.

### 2. Commissioned Works (Art & Deliverables) — No Change

All the deliverables, fees, revision rounds, delivery/acceptance timelines, hourly rate, and payment milestones are **identical**:

- Faction pieces: 6 × $350 = $2,100
- Card back: $350
- Box art: $750
- Marketing pack: $500
- IG/TikTok stories: 10 × $50 = $500
- Landing page + logo: TBD (~$450)
- Hourly add-ons: $50/hr, 15 hr/month cap
- Payment schedule: 40% / 30% / 30%
- 2 revision rounds per deliverable
- 7 business days to accept or revise

**Danny's take-home: Identical.**

### 3. Independent Counsel Stipend — No Change

$750 upon signing, non-refundable, non-recoupable. Same in both versions.

### 4. License Grant — Streamlined but Substantively the Same

**What was there before:** The original granted an exclusive license for the "Licensed Products" (plural — the base game plus expansions, reprints, etc.).

**What's there now:** The grant is for the "Product" (singular — just the base game). Same exclusive, worldwide, field-limited structure. Same sublicensing/assignment rights, same sell-off period (12 months).

**New in the simplified version:** The 2/22 draft had already added detailed trademark provisions (Franchise Marks vs. Product Marks, quality control, registration rights) that weren't in the 2/12 original. The simplified version keeps those — they're good protections for Danny's brand. The original 2/12 version flagged trademark as an "open item to decide."

**What was removed:** The original had an open item about whether Danny's consent should be required for assignment to a successor publisher. The simplified version dropped that open item and just says the successor must assume all obligations. This is a minor Publisher-favorable simplification, but Danny's core protection (the successor has to honor the deal) is still there.

**Danny's take-home: His IP protections are the same or better (the trademark provisions are more detailed now). The license scope is just narrower — one product instead of a whole product line.**

### 5. KS1 Economics — No Change

This is the section Danny will care most about, and it's **completely unchanged**:

- **Royalty rate:** 5% of Net Receipts. Same.
- **Minimum guarantee for KS1:** None. Same in both versions.
- **Profit-share kicker tiers (highest tier only):**
  - $50K–$74,999 captured → $1,000
  - $75K–$149,999 captured → $3,000
  - $150K+ captured → $6,000
- **Kicker timing:** Within 90 days after KS1 ends (the original said "[__] days" — the simplified version filled in 90).
- **Net Receipts definition:** Same formula — cash received minus platform fees, processing fees, and refunds. Same exclusions (shipping, taxes). Same bundle allocation rule.
- **Reporting:** Quarterly, due 45 days after quarter end (the original said "[__] days" — now filled in).
- **Payment:** Due 30 days after report (original said "[__] days" — now filled in).
- **Minimum payout:** $50 (original said "$[__]" — now filled in).
- **Audit:** 1×/year, cost-shifting if underpayment exceeds 10% (original said "cost-shifting rules to be specified" — now specified).

**Danny's take-home: Every dollar, every percentage, every trigger is the same. The only differences are that blanks got filled in with specific numbers, which is progress, not a change in terms.**

### 6. KS2 Economics — Removed Entirely

**What was there before:** This was the most complex part of the original. It included:

- A $5,000 minimum guarantee (split into two $2,500 installments) triggered by KS2 launch
- The MG was recoupable against the 5% royalty
- A separate set of hit-sharing kicker tiers for KS2 ($3K / $7.5K / $15K at higher thresholds than KS1)
- Detailed mechanics for when installments were due, what happens if KS2 doesn't fund, etc.

**What's there now:** None of this. KS2 doesn't exist in the simplified agreement.

**Danny's take-home: He's not losing guaranteed money — the KS2 MG was only ever triggered if you actually ran KS2. Since this simplified deal doesn't commit to KS2, there's nothing to "lose." If you do KS2 later, you'd negotiate those terms then through the right-of-first-negotiation clause.**

### 7. Term and Reversion — Simplified

**What was there before:** A three-phase structure:
1. KS1 Phase (through fulfillment + 12 months)
2. KS2 Option Window (24 months in the original, expanded to 36 in the 2/22 draft, during which Publisher could exercise the option to do KS2)
3. KS2 Phase (3 years from KS2 launch, then auto-renew annually while in print)

Plus five reversion triggers, two of which were KS2-specific.

**What's there now:** Just the KS1 Phase — the license runs from signing through KS1 fulfillment plus 12 months, then it's done (unless otherwise negotiated). Four reversion triggers instead of five:
1. Failure to launch KS1 by a deadline
2. Product not "In Print" for 12 months after fulfillment (with 90-day cure)
3. Material nonreporting/nonpayment (45-day cure)
4. Insolvency

The dropped trigger was "No KS2 within the Option Window" — which no longer applies since there's no KS2 option.

**Danny's take-home: This is actually cleaner for him. The original could have tied up his IP for years through the KS2 Option Window (24–36 months just for Publisher to decide whether to do KS2, plus 3 more years if they did). Now, the license has a clear endpoint: KS1 fulfillment + 12 months. His IP isn't locked up waiting for a second campaign that may never happen.**

### 8. Right of First Negotiation — New

**What was there before:** Nothing like this. The original assumed KS2 was baked into the deal.

**What's there now:** If Publisher wants to do expansions, reprints, or new games in the Hamsters & Monsters universe, Publisher and Danny will negotiate in good faith for 60 days before either party can talk to third parties about those products.

**What this is NOT:** It's not an option. It's not an exclusive right beyond the 60-day window. It doesn't commit either party to any price, any royalty rate, or any deal structure. If they can't agree in 60 days, both sides walk away free.

**Danny's take-home: This is a handshake signal that you want to keep working together. It costs Danny nothing, commits him to nothing, and gives him 60 days of guaranteed attention before you could go find another artist/IP for a follow-up product. It's arguably better for Danny than the original — under the old structure, the KS2 economics were already locked in at terms that might not reflect reality after KS1. Under this approach, he gets to negotiate KS2 terms with the benefit of knowing how KS1 actually performed.**

### 9. Perpetual Buyout Option — Removed

**What was there before:** The original included an open-item provision where Publisher could buy a perpetual license to the IP for 4× trailing-twelve-month royalties (with a $25K floor), which would eliminate or reduce ongoing royalties.

**What's there now:** Gone. Not in the simplified version.

**Danny's take-home: This was an "open item" in the original anyway (never finalized), but removing it means there's no mechanism for Publisher to permanently acquire rights to his universe IP. His IP stays his, period. If perpetual rights ever become relevant, it would be a fresh negotiation.**

### 10. IG/TikTok, Credits, Ownership, Warranties, Termination, Governing Law — No Change

All of these are substantively the same across both versions. The social media campaign terms, credit/portfolio rights, IP ownership split, logo assignment, warranty/indemnity cap, Publisher's 120-day termination-for-convenience right, and Arizona governing law are all carried over unchanged.

### 11. Exhibits — Streamlined

**Exhibit A (Deliverables):** Same content, just reformatted. All fees and milestones identical.

**Exhibit B:** Simplified. The original had a "Licensed Products" sub-section listing expansions, deluxe editions, promo packs, etc. The simplified version just has the canon list and trademark note, since the only licensed product is the base game.

**Exhibit C (Royalty Report):** Same fields, minus the "MG recoupment balance" line (since there's no MG).

## Quick Reference: What Danny Might Ask

**"Am I getting paid less?"**
No. Every payment for KS1 — commissions, royalties, kickers, counsel stipend — is identical.

**"What about KS2 money?"**
The KS2 MG ($5K) and KS2 kickers ($3K–$15K) were only ever triggered if KS2 happened. Since this deal doesn't include KS2, those aren't applicable. But if you do a KS2 later, you'd negotiate fresh terms — possibly better ones, since you'll know how KS1 actually performed.

**"Is my IP less protected?"**
No. The trademark provisions are actually more detailed than the 2/12 original. The license scope is narrower (just the base game), and the term is shorter and cleaner. His IP isn't locked up behind a multi-year option window anymore.

**"What's this 'right of first negotiation' thing?"**
It means if you want to do more Hamsters & Monsters products, you have to talk to Danny first for 60 days before going elsewhere. It doesn't lock him into any price or commitment. It's a courtesy clause that says "we want to keep working together."

**"Why simplify?"**
Because negotiating KS2 terms before KS1 even launches means guessing at numbers neither side can validate yet. Better to get KS1 right, see how it performs, and then have an informed conversation about what comes next.
