## THE THREE GAMES

### 🎪 CONTESTS OF CHAOS
**The Main Event**

**Players:** 2-4 · **Time:** 30-45 min · **Complexity:** ★★★☆☆

> Draft monsters from a sliding market where cards get cheaper the longer they wait. Complete athletic events on the Jumbotron for victory points. Watch your rivals — they're drafting from the same pool.
>
> Contests of Chaos is the centerpiece of the box. It's the game you play when everyone's settled in and ready for a full evening.

**[Gameplay GIF: drafting from the lineup, completing an event]**

**What makes it click:**
- The sliding market creates agonizing "wait or grab?" decisions
- Free Agents (0s and 10s) work for any faction — but everyone wants them
- The Playbook expansion module adds hidden objectives for experienced players

---

### 🎉 GET STUFFED
**The Warm-Up**

**Players:** 2-6 · **Time:** 10-15 min · **Complexity:** ★☆☆☆☆

> Play a matching card. Use a faction power. Try to empty your hand before everyone else. Get stuck with cards and hear the table roar.
>
> Get Stuffed is the game that starts the evening. It's the game you play while waiting for the last person to arrive. It's the game your non-gamer friend asks to play again.

**[Gameplay GIF: rapid card play, someone getting stuck, laughter]**

**What makes it click:**
- Teach it in 60 seconds to anyone who's played UNO
- Faction powers (ranks 6-10) add just enough strategy to keep gamers engaged
- The "Get Stuffed!" moment when someone gets stuck is genuinely funny every time

---

### 🏔️ SUMMIT SCRAMBLE
**The Sprint**

**Players:** 3-5 · **Time:** 20 min · **Complexity:** ★★☆☆☆

> Play card formations to climb the mountain. Singles, pairs, runs, bombs. Beat what's on the table or pass and fall behind. First to empty their hand reaches the Summit.
>
> Summit Scramble is the game between games. Quick, competitive, and full of moments where a well-timed Trip-Up ruins someone's entire strategy.

**[Gameplay GIF: formations being played, a Trip-Up moment]**

**What makes it click:**
- The Trip-Up: a lowly 0 beats a mighty 10 played solo. The table goes silent. Then erupts.
- Confetti Cannons (four-of-a-kind) interrupt anything. Pure chaos.
- Faction powers trigger at rank 6+ — knowing when to activate is the skill gap

## FAQ

**Q: Is this really three different games from one deck?**
Yes. Same 66 cards. Completely different mechanics, strategies, and table energy. Get Stuffed is a party game. Summit Scramble is a racing game. Contests of Chaos is a strategy game. One purchase.

**Q: What about the stretch goal games?**
Stretch goal games add rulebook inserts to the box — no extra cards or components needed. The deck you're already getting plays them all.

**Q: Is this good for families?**
Get Stuffed and Summit Scramble are accessible to ages 10+. If we unlock the Junior Field Guide stretch goal, Fuzzy Gang and Fuzzy Faceoff bring the entry age down to 5+.

**Q: What's the replay value?**
Three games minimum. Up to six with stretch goals. Each game has different faction interactions, so drafting different combinations creates different experiences every session.

**Q: Who is Danny Beck?**
Danny illustrated Mechs vs Minions, the cooperative tabletop game published by Riot Games. His style brings warmth, personality, and detail to every card in the deck.
