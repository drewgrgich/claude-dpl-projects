## ACT 2: THE PRODUCT (0:10–0:30)

| Time | Visual | Audio |
|------|--------|-------|
| 0:10 | **Hand fans the 66-card deck.** Slow spread showing all six factions. | Drew (VO): *"66 cards. Six factions."* |
| 0:13 | **Deck gets shuffled.** | *"Three completely different games."* |
| 0:15 | **GET STUFFED.** Fast, chaotic energy. Someone yelling. Cards flying. | *"Get Stuffed — ten minutes of chaos."* |
| 0:19 | **Same deck, shuffled again.** 2-second insert. Same card backs. | *(no VO — just the sound of shuffling)* |
| 0:21 | **SUMMIT SCRAMBLE.** Formations climbing. Trip-Up moment. Table erupts. | *"Summit Scramble — a twenty-minute race to the top."* |
| 0:25 | **Same deck, shuffled again.** Same cards. Different layout on table. | *(shuffle sound)* |
| 0:27 | **CONTESTS OF CHAOS.** Drafting. Strategy. Quiet intensity, then a big play. | *"Contests of Chaos — a full evening showdown."* |

**Key:** The shuffle between each game IS the unique selling proposition made visual. The backer sees one deck becoming three games.

**No text overlays during montage.** VO names the games. Visuals show the feeling. One input channel per sense.

## Production Notes

- **Film gameplay sessions FIRST.** The best moments will tell you what to say.
- **Danny's art:** Use Ken Burns effect (slow pan/zoom) on static illustrations. Looks cinematic at zero cost.
- **The deck tap at 1:15** is physical punctuation — small gesture, big moment.
- **"More to unlock" is a mistake.** Don't mention stretch goals in the video. Sell what's in the box.
