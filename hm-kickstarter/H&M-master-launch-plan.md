## The Math: Can You Hit 1,500 Backers?

Your budget spreadsheet models this with email list size of 2,000, 1% email-to-backer conversion, 500 KS followers at 50% conversion, plus 100 from other sources = **~370 expected backers initially, growing to ~592 over the campaign**. That's well short of 1,500.

**To hit 1,500 backers, you need to either:** grow the email list well beyond 2,000, improve the conversion rate above 1%, build a much larger KS follower base, or some combination of all three.

**Mitigating factors in your favor:** Danny's 39K followers provide instant credibility, the 66-card system is a low-cost accessible entry point, art-forward games convert higher, and multiple games from one deck ("one deck, infinite play") is a strong value prop.

**Realistic path:** 5,000–8,000 engaged emails → 500–1,000 backers → $17,500–$45,000 funding.

## Smoke Test: Danny's Audience (Post-Contract Signing)

As soon as Danny signs, run a smoke test: Danny posts about H&M on Instagram with a link to the landing page. This is the first real signal of whether his audience converts.

### What to Measure

| Metric | How to Track |
|--------|-------------|
| Impressions | Danny's IG insights |
| Link clicks | Landing page analytics (UTM tagged) |
| Landing page → email signup rate | Email platform |
| Engagement (likes, comments, saves, shares) | Danny's IG insights |

### Decision Thresholds

| Signal | Click-Through Rate | LP Signup Rate | What It Means | Action |
|--------|-------------------|----------------|---------------|--------|
| **Green — Full speed ahead** | >3% of impressions click | >20% of visitors sign up | Danny's audience is interested and converting. This is your primary acquisition channel. | Execute plan as-is. Lean hard into Danny content funnel. |
| **Yellow — Adjust strategy** | 1–3% click | 10–20% signup | Some interest, but Danny's audience alone won't get you there. Art fans ≠ board gamers. | Shift more budget to paid ads targeting board game communities directly. Increase Reddit/BGG organic efforts. Danny content becomes brand credibility, not primary funnel. |
| **Red — Serious reassessment** | <1% click | <10% signup | Danny's audience isn't your market. | Pause and reassess. Consider: Is the landing page/offer the problem, or is the audience mismatch fundamental? Run a second test with different messaging before making major decisions. Increase paid ad testing budget. If second test also fails, significantly restructure acquisition plan around paid channels and organic board game community building. |

### Important Context

A weak smoke test does NOT mean the project is dead. It means Danny's art followers aren't your primary customer acquisition channel. Board gamers are — and you reach them through BGG, Reddit, paid Meta ads targeting board game interests, podcast appearances, convention demos, and influencer reviews. Danny's art still provides credibility and production value; it just may not drive direct signups.

**Before the smoke test:** lock your thresholds, agree on them with Danny, and commit to interpreting the data objectively. Don't move the goalposts after you see results.

**Run a second test if Red:** change the CTA, offer, or landing page before concluding the audience doesn't work. It might be a messaging problem, not an audience problem.

## Playtesting Strategy

### Weekly Cadence
- **Wednesday nights:** Silver Key Lounge playtest group — consistent weekly attendance starting ASAP
- **Weekends (1–2x/month):** Blind playtests at local game stores — rotate locations for fresh audiences

### Playtest Types

**Guided playtests (early phase):** You teach the rules, observe, and take notes. Good for catching mechanical issues and balance problems.

**Blind playtests (critical):** Hand someone the rulebook and prototype, walk away. This is the real test — if people can't learn it without you, the rulebook needs work. Prioritize these at game stores where you'll get strangers with no context.

### What to Track
- [ ] Create standardized feedback form covering: ease of learning, game length, fun factor, confusing moments, favorite/least favorite mechanics
- [ ] Separate blind playtest form: add questions about rulebook clarity, setup confusion, and where they got stuck
- [ ] Track session data: date, location, player count, game variant played, session length
