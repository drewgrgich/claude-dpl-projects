Seat 0 wins 52% — worse than baseline (46%). With only 4 rooms and 3 players, the first player's initial placements dominate. Fewer rooms = less recovery time for late-seat players.

Games also get very fast (15 turns, ~5 min). That might feel too quick for the bluffing to develop.

Not recommended as-is. The completion fix is essential, but 4 rooms at 3 players creates a different problem. A better approach: keep 5 rooms but deal 6 cards instead of 5 at 3 players. This gives each player one extra placement to compensate for the tight card economy without cutting the game short.

Combining 4 rooms + partial bust scoring at 3 players gives near-perfect completion (99.6%) and scoring that actually means something (12.0 avg vs 8.6 with 4 rooms alone). The seat imbalance (22.8%) is better than 4-rooms-only (34.4%) but still not great.

The 3-player first-player advantage is structural across all variants. Even the best patch here has Seat 0 at 47%. This is inherent to the clockwise draft + clockwise play + room-filling dynamics. A snake draft (1→2→3→3→2→1...) would help but isn't testable in this sim without a bigger AI rework.

My Take — Responding to Morgan

The 3-player issue is real but fixable. The game's core is remarkably solid.

Morgan asked about the 77% completion rate at 3 players. Here's what the data says:

The root cause is card economy math. At 3 players with 5 rooms: 3 × 5 = 15 cards, and 5 × 3 = 15 cards needed to fill every room. There's zero slack — any turn spent on a power or accusation means a room can't fill. At 4 and 5 players, the math has built-in margin (20 cards for 18 slots, 25 for 21), so those counts work perfectly.

My recommended fix for 3 players:

  1. Keep 5 rooms (preserves game length and bluffing space)
  2. Deal 8 cards, draft 6 (instead of deal 7, keep 5) — gives one extra placement that creates the slack the card economy needs
  3. Consider snake draft (1→2→3→3→2→1) to reduce first-player advantage

For the bust rate (all player counts):

Partial bust scoring is a clean win — +34% more total scoring, same game feel, no new rules complexity. The rule is simple: "In a bust, each faction scores half its rank sum (rounded down)." This makes every room resolution feel meaningful without eliminating the sting of a bust.

What I'd leave alone:

Everything else. The draft tension is real (Q2 confirmed). The deduction system works (Q3). The skill-luck balance is in the sweet spot (Q6). No dominant strategy exists (Q11). The ascending tension curve is beautiful (Q5). These are the signs of a game that's already well-designed — it just needs the 3-player economy fixed and the bust sting softened.
