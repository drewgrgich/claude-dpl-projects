# 🎭 MYSTERY MASCOTS

### A Hidden-Fandom Bluffing Game for 3–5 Players

**System:** Hamsters & Monsters Core Deck (66 cards) · **Time:** 15 Minutes · **Ages:** 12+

------

## The Pitch

The big game is tonight. The mascots are everywhere. The chants are loud. The hallways are chaos.

Everyone says they're here to support the whole event. They are lying.

Each player is secretly rooting for one faction and trying to sneak the best gear into the right Locker Rooms before the doors close. You'll draft your hand while watching what everyone else wants, hide your plays face-down, and try to score points for your team without making it too obvious.

Because if you get too bold, your cover is blown.

From that point on, every card you play is face-up. Everyone sees exactly who you're helping. And suddenly this stops being a subtle little bluffing game and becomes a public incident.

Sneak the gear. Cheer for your team. Don't get caught.

------

## What You Need

Just the **Hamsters & Monsters deck** — 66 cards, six factions, ranks **0–10**. The faction identities and taglines come straight from your broader H&M guide, so this still reads like part of the same family.

**The Six Factions:**

| Color    | Faction            | Tagline                          |
| -------- | ------------------ | -------------------------------- |
| 🔴 Red    | Super-Dupes        | **Strength and Let Me Help**     |
| 🟠 Orange | Finders-Keepers    | **Just Dibs Mine**               |
| 🟡 Yellow | Tinkerers          | **Too slow!**                    |
| 🟢 Green  | Prognosticationers | **I Knew That!**                 |
| 🔵 Blue   | Magicians          | **Sleight of Paw**               |
| 🟣 Purple | Time Travelers     | **I've already done this 1000x** |

You’ll also want:

- **2 Accusation Tokens per player**
- A little table space for **Locker Rooms**
- A way to track score

Coins, cubes, scraps of paper, ominous eye contact — whatever works.

------

## Setup

### 1) Deal Secret Allegiances

Shuffle the full deck. Deal **1 face-down card** to each player. This is your **Secret Allegiance**. Peek at it. Keep it hidden. You may check it again anytime.

That card stays out of your hand for the whole game.

### 2) Draft Your Hand

Deal **7 cards** face-down to each player.

Now draft:

- Pick **1 card** to keep
- Pass the rest **clockwise**
- Repeat until each player has kept **5 cards**

Discard the final **2 cards per player** face-down, unseen.

Everyone now has a **5-card hand**.

### 3) Build the Locker Rooms

Create a row of empty Locker Rooms equal to **players + 2**.

| Players | Locker Rooms |
| ------- | ------------ |
| 3       | 5            |
| 4       | 6            |
| 5       | 7            |

Each Locker Room holds **up to 3 cards**. When the 3rd card enters a room, that room resolves immediately.

### 4) Give Out Accusation Tokens

Each player gets **2 Accusation Tokens**.

That’s it. No event deck. No setup sub-table. No “wait, how many cards at 4 again?” The v2.2 draft had more setup granularity, but this flattening keeps the pregame cleaner while preserving the same room-count logic that fixed the 3-player math.

------

## The Core Idea

You are secretly trying to score points for **your own faction**.

You do that by helping your faction win Locker Rooms.

But you are **not** trying to look like you’re helping your faction.

That tension is the whole game.

Low cards are sneaky. High cards are powerful. High cards also get you noticed.

------

## On Your Turn

Choose **one**:

- **Place 1 card** into a Locker Room
- **Use your one-time faction power**
- **Make an accusation**

Then play passes clockwise.

------

## Placing Cards

Choose any Locker Room with fewer than 3 cards and place **1 card** into it.

If you are still **Hidden**, place it **face-down**.

If you are **Exposed**, place it **face-up**.

That’s the basic action you’ll take most of the game.

If your card becomes the **3rd card** in that Locker Room, the room resolves immediately.

------

## Locker Room Resolution

When a Locker Room gets its 3rd card, resolve it in this order:

### Step 1: Reveal the Room

Flip all 3 cards face-up.

### Step 2: Declare Wilds

Ranks **0** and **10** are **Wilds** in Mystery Mascots. The factions guide already frames 0s and 10s as the natural candidates for special / joker behavior in H&M, so this still fits the deck physics.

For each Wild in the room:

- the player who played it declares which faction it counts as **for this room**
- the Wild keeps its printed rank
- if multiple Wilds are present, declare them in the order they were played into the room

A 0 can become any faction and still counts as **0 points**.
A 10 can become any faction and still counts as **10 points**.

### Step 3: Check Majority

Count how many cards belong to each faction in the room.

- The faction with the **most cards** wins the room
- That faction scores points equal to the **sum of its card ranks**
- If there is a tie for most cards, the room is a **bust** and no one scores

> Example: a room reveals Red 3, Red 8, Blue 10.
> If the Blue 10 is declared Red, Red has all 3 cards and scores **21**.
> If the Blue 10 stays Blue, Red has 2 cards and scores **11**.

### Step 4: Check Exposure

Whoever played the **highest-ranked card in the room** becomes **Exposed**.

- Ties are broken by **most recently played**
- If Yellow’s power changes a card for the Exposure check, use that adjusted value here
- Exposure happens whether the room scored or busted

### Step 5: Clear the Room

Discard all 3 cards. That Locker Room is empty again.

------

## Hidden vs. Exposed

This is the part that gives the game teeth. In v2.2, Exposure became meaningful because it stopped being “they know my color” and started being “they can now see what I do.” That stays here.

### Hidden

While Hidden:

- your allegiance stays secret
- the cards you place go in **face-down**

### Exposed

Once Exposed:

- reveal your **Secret Allegiance**
- from now on, every card you place goes in **face-up**

Exposure is permanent. No recovering your innocence. No putting the mascot head back on.

------

## Accusations

Twice per game, you may spend an Accusation Token to call your shot.

### Make an Accusation

Choose another player and guess their faction.

That player must answer **truthfully** — but only to **you**.

Do **not** reveal the answer to the table.

At end of game:

- **Correct accusation:** +5 points
- **Incorrect accusation:** −2 points

That’s it. No public trial. No vote. No table-wide lock-in. Just one player taking a private swing and living with the consequences.

------

## Scoring

You only care about points scored by **your faction**.

When a Locker Room resolves, the winning faction earns those points. Every player whose Secret Allegiance matches that faction scores them.

At game end, add:

- all points your faction earned from Locker Rooms
- your accusation bonuses and penalties

Highest total wins.

### Tiebreakers

1. **Fewest times Exposed**
2. **Most correct accusations**
3. Shared victory

That tie structure comes straight from your v2.2 file and still works fine here.

------

## Game End

The game ends **immediately after the last Locker Room resolves**.

That means:

- **5th** room at 3 players
- **6th** room at 4 players
- **7th** room at 5 players

Finish that resolution, total scores, and declare the most suspiciously enthusiastic fan the winner.

------

## Faction Powers

Each faction has **one** power. You may use yours **once per game**.

All powers now use the same timing window:

> **On your turn, instead of placing a card or making an accusation, you may use your faction power.**

That unified timing is one of the main simplifications from v2.2, where Yellow resolved during room resolution and Green had an “anytime on your turn” wording that added overhead without adding much play value.

Using your power does **not** automatically Expose you.
Using it badly might.

------

### 🔴 Super-Dupes — Forced Assist

*"Strength and Let Me Help"*

Flip **any face-down card** in any Locker Room face-up.

It stays face-up for the rest of its time in that room.

This is the blunt instrument. Sometimes you use it for information. Sometimes you use it to ruin somebody’s whole afternoon.

------

### 🟠 Finders-Keepers — Just Dibs Mine

*"Just Dibs Mine"*

Secretly peek at **any one face-down card** in a Locker Room.

Do not reveal it to the table.

Simple, useful, rude.

------

### 🟡 Tinkerers — Scoreboard Mod

*"Too slow!"*

Choose a Locker Room containing **one of your cards**. Until that room resolves, your card there counts as either **0** or **11** for the **Exposure check only**.

Its printed rank still counts normally for scoring.

So yes:

- your 8 can pretend to be a 0 and duck Exposure
- your 2 can pretend to be an 11 and take the hit on purpose
- none of that changes who scores the room

This keeps Yellow’s identity as the fast little problem-solver while moving it into the same timing window as every other power. The old v2.2 version was strong but awkward because it lived inside room resolution.

------

### 🟢 Prognosticationers — Smug Foreknowledge

*"I Knew That!"*

Secretly look at **one other player’s** Secret Allegiance.

Then that player secretly looks at **yours**.

Neither of you becomes publicly Exposed. The rest of the table learns nothing.

You now have a tiny private cold war inside the larger game.

------

### 🔵 Magicians — Sleight of Paw

*"Sleight of Paw"*

Move **one of your previously played cards** from its current Locker Room to a different Locker Room with space.

If it was face-down, it stays face-down.
If it was face-up, it stays face-up.

If this creates the 3rd card in the destination room, that room resolves immediately.

Blue should feel tricky and disruptive in the H&M line, and this version keeps that role while removing some of the old wording clutter around exposure-state edge cases.

------

### 🟣 Time Travelers — The Rewind

*"I've already done this 1000x."*

Return **one of your previously played cards** from a Locker Room to your hand.

Then immediately play **that same card** to a different Locker Room, following normal Hidden / Exposed placement rules.

This counts as **one action**.

If the new room becomes full, it resolves immediately.

Purple still gets the “take it back and do it right this time” identity from v2.2, but in a cleaner rules shell.

------

## The Tailgate Draft

This happens before the game starts, but it matters enough to call out on its own because it’s the signature move.

You are not just drafting for card quality.

You are drafting while:

- protecting your own allegiance
- tracking what colors disappear
- noticing what keeps getting passed
- deciding whether to hate-draft dangerous cards
- trying not to look too interested in your own team

That is the game’s first act of deception.

This was one of the strongest parts of the v2.2 rules and absolutely worth preserving.

------

## GREGOR’S CLIPBOARD

*Stop arguing and read this.*

**Hidden allegiance stays hidden until Exposure.** You do not reveal your allegiance when you score points. Points are tracked secretly or on paper if needed.

**Wilds are declared at resolution, not when played.** A 0 or 10 does nothing special until the room reveals.

**Wild rank never changes.** A declared 0 is still worth 0 points. A declared 10 is still worth 10.

**Multiple Wilds are legal.** If two or three Wilds appear in one room, each player declares their own Wild’s faction in play order.

**All-Wild rooms are legal.** Declare them like normal, count majority like normal, score like normal. No special collapse rule.

**Exposure cares about rank, not faction size.** The highest-ranked card causes Exposure even if that card’s faction lost the room.

**Tied high cards use most recent play.** If two 9s are tied for highest, the one played later causes Exposure.

**Yellow only changes Exposure.** It does not change scoring. It does not change majority. It only changes who gets caught.

**You can use your power while Hidden or Exposed.** Exposure changes how you place cards, not whether you still have a power.

**Blue can move any card you played.** Not just face-down ones. If it’s yours, Blue can pull the trick.

**Purple replays the same card.** Not a different card. You are rewinding that exact play.

**Moved cards can trigger resolution.** If Blue or Purple causes a Locker Room to become the 3rd card, resolve it immediately.

**Accusation answers are private and truthful.** You may lie about what you learned afterward, obviously. This is still a social deduction game. The truthful part only applies to the accused’s answer.

**No one is required to place all 5 hand cards.** Some cards will stay unplayed. That’s normal. The room count, not hand exhaustion, ends the game.

**3 players is still the sharpest version.** It works. It is just more exact, less foggy, and meaner. Four players is probably still the cleanest first experience, same as your earlier analysis suggested.

## Quick Reference

**Setup:**
Deal 1 Secret Allegiance each → deal 7, draft 5 → set Locker Rooms to players + 2 → 2 Accusation Tokens each

**On Your Turn:**
Place 1 card **or** use your one-time faction power **or** make an accusation

**Placing:**
Hidden = face-down
Exposed = face-up

**Locker Room Resolution:**
Reveal → declare Wilds (0s and 10s) → check majority → score winning faction by rank sum → highest rank becomes Exposed → clear room

**Accusations:**
Spend 1 token → guess a player’s faction → private truthful answer → endgame: +5 if correct, −2 if wrong

**Game End:**
After the last Locker Room resolves

**Faction Powers:**
