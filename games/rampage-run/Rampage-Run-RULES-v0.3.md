# RAMPAGE RUN — Game Rules v0.3

*Designer Corps — Phase 2 Draft*
*Iteration 3 of ~4*

---

## Concept

**Rampage Run** is a press-your-luck card game where players build a shared ascending streak of cards. On each turn, you either play a card equal or higher than the last — or you fold to bank your points and pass the risk to the next player. The tension builds with every card: bank now, or push your luck and force someone else to crash?

---

## Object of the Game

Be the first player to reach **50 banked points**, or hold the most points when the deck is exhausted.

---

## The Deck

- **66 cards** total: ranks **0–10** across **6 factions**
- **Rank 0** cards are **wild** — play as any rank 1–10 to satisfy any target. Adds 0 to the streak total.
- No jokers; the 0 is the wild.

---

## Setup

1. Shuffle the deck
2. Deal **5 cards** face-down to each player
3. Place the remaining deck face-down as the **draw pile**
4. Flip the top card of the draw pile — this starts the **Streak Pile** and becomes the **Current Target**

**Starting player:** The player who holds the lowest-ranked card in hand reveals it. Ties broken clockwise.

---

## Card Anatomy

Each card has:
- **Rank** (0–10): used to determine playability against the Current Target
- **Point value** = rank (0 = 0 points toward streak total)
- **Faction** (color): thematic only; no abilities in the base game

---

## Core Loop — Your Turn

Look at the **Current Target** (top card of the Streak Pile) and the **Streak Total** (sum of ranks in the Streak Pile).

Choose ONE:

### Option A: PLAY
Play one card from your hand whose rank is **≥ the Current Target's rank**. Place it on top of the Streak Pile — it becomes the new Current Target. The Streak Total increases by the card's rank.

> **The Post-Play Fold:** After playing, you may immediately **FOLD** (see Option B) instead of ending your turn. This lets you bank the Streak Total *before* the next player's turn begins — securing your points at the cost of passing the risk.

- Rank **0** is wild: announce any rank 1–10 when played. It satisfies any target and adds 0 to the Streak Total.

### Option B: FOLD
Bank your points and reset the streak:
1. Add the **Streak Total** to your banked score
2. Discard the entire Streak Pile
3. Draw back up to **5 cards** from the draw pile
4. Flip the top card of the draw pile to start a new Streak (new Current Target; Streak Total = that card's rank)
5. Your turn ends

*Folding is always available — even if you have a playable card.*

### Option C: CRASH
If you **cannot play** any card ≥ the Current Target, you must choose between Folding (banking zero) and **CRASHING**:
1. The **Streak Pile is discarded** — no one banks those points
2. The player who played the card **right before you** scores a **Crash Bonus** equal to the Streak Total
3. You draw back up to **5 cards**
4. Flip the top card of the draw pile to start a new Streak
5. Your turn ends

> **Crash Bonus Example:** Streak Total = 23. You cannot play and choose to crash. The player who played the previous card scores **23 bonus points** added directly to their bank.

> **Key rule — you can always fold:** If you cannot play, you may *always* choose to Fold instead of Crashing. Crashing is only possible when you have at least one legal play and *choose* to play it knowing you cannot continue. You can never be forced to crash.

---

## How Scoring Works

| Event | Who Scores | What They Score |
|-------|-----------|-----------------|
| You FOLD | You | The full Streak Total |
| You CRASH | The previous player | The full Streak Total as a Crash Bonus |
| You play and end your turn | No one (yet) | — |

**The fundamental tension:** When you play a card, you are raising the Streak Total — making it more rewarding for whoever eventually banks it (by folding) or whoever causes a crash (by forcing the next player to fail). Every card you play is a gift to your opponent. Every card you don't play is a missed opportunity.

---

## Reading the Table

The **Streak Pile** sits in the center. Everyone can see:
- The **Current Target** (top card's rank)
- The **Streak Total** (announced after each play)
- Whose card is whose (turn order is public)

---

## Win Condition

The game ends when **either:**
1. A player reaches **50+ banked points** after any banking action — that player wins immediately
2. The draw pile is **exhausted** and the last card has been used to start a Streak — complete the round, then highest score wins

*Ties: most cards in hand > fewest crashes > sudden death.*

---

## End-of-Game (Deck Exhausted)

- Complete the round (everyone takes one turn in order)
- If the draw pile is empty when you need to start a new Streak, the Streak simply continues as-is (no reset)
- Crashes during an empty deck award **no Crash Bonus** (streak is simply discarded)
- Highest total banked wins

---

## The 0 Card (Wild)

Rank **0** cards are wild:
- Playable as any rank 1–10 to satisfy the ≥ target requirement
- Add **0** to the Streak Total — useful for extending without raising the stakes
- Can be played to "reset" the pressure without increasing the reward for the next player

---

## Crash Shield (Recommended — Start of Game)

Each player has **one Crash Shield** for the entire game.

- **First crash:** Discard the Streak. No Crash Bonus is awarded to anyone. Your Shield is consumed (flip your marker face-up).
- **Second and subsequent crashes:** Normal Crash rules apply — you lose the Streak; the previous player scores the Crash Bonus.

Mark your Shield by keeping one of your face-down starting cards in front of you.

*Why it matters:* Without the Shield, a player who crashes twice with 0 banked points has almost no path back. The Shield gives every player one genuine comeback opportunity without softening subsequent crashes.

---

## 2-Player Game

In 2-player, the Crash Bonus is **not awarded** — there is no distinct "previous player" different from the crasher.

- When you crash: the Streak is simply discarded. No points to anyone. You draw back up to 5.
- The Crash Shield still works normally.

This makes crashing **more costly** in 2-player (you lose your streak AND give nothing to your opponent), raising the stakes.

---

## Player Scaling

| Players | Notes |
|---------|-------|
| **2** | Crash Bonus disabled. Higher stakes. |
| **3** | Standard rules. Natural cycling of Crash Bonuses. |
| **4** | Standard rules. More players = more chances to extend streaks = higher totals = faster games. |
| **5** | Standard rules. With 5 players and only 5 cards each, streaks cycle quickly. Tension spikes in late game. |

## Designer Notes

**Why the Crash Bonus works:**
The Crash Bonus ensures extending the streak is always meaningful. If you play a card, you're raising the stakes for the next player — and if they crash, you get rewarded. This turns the passive act of "building on someone else's streak" into an active investment. Without it, the optimal strategy when you can't play is simply to fold, making the game passive and defensive.

**Why the post-play fold matters:**
The ability to play a card and *then* immediately fold is the game's most important strategic depth. It means you can contribute to the streak (raising the Crash Bonus for the previous player) while still banking your own points. You can also play a 0 to extend the streak without raising the total, then fold — a zero-cost contribution.

**Why the Crash Shield matters:**
Without it, a player who crashes twice from 0 has essentially no chance in a 4–5 player game. The Shield keeps all players competitive and creates memorable comeback rounds.

**Why 50 points:**
Average card rank ≈ 5. Each successfully banked streak of 3–4 cards is worth ~15–20 points. Most games finish in 2–4 successful banks per player. 50 is reachable but not guaranteed — exact timing matters.

## Comparison to Existing H&M Titles

| Feature | Rampage Run | Bid Brawl | Merch Stand Mob | Tunnel Brawl |
|---------|------------|-----------|-----------------|--------------|
| **Core mechanic** | Press-your-luck ascending streak | Blind commitment bidding | Simultaneous reveal | Two-front deployment |
| **Hidden info** | Hand only | Bid amount | Hands + roles | Hands |
| **Tension type** | "Bank or push?" | "How much to commit?" | "Will they flip?" | "Who owns the tunnel?" |
| **Scoring** | Additive streaks | Winner-take-most | Cumulative | Threshold |
| **Crash/rollback** | Yes — lose streak | Yes — lose bid | Partial (0-vs-all) | No |
| **Physical play** | Single card line | Card stack | Simultaneous | Cross-body layout |

**Mechanical gap filled:** Press-your-luck with ascending streak. No other H&M title uses "stay or go" as its primary tension loop.

## Manufacturing Notes

- **Components:** 66 cards only (no tokens, no dice)
- **Card size:** Standard poker (2.5" × 3.5")
- **Crash Shield marker:** Each player repurposes one of their own face-down starting cards — no additional components
- **Rulebook:** Single double-sided sheet or trifold — teachable in 60 seconds
- **Box:** 67 cards + rulebook fits standard card box

- **Pig** — the "hold or push" risk/reward loop
- **Bid Brawl** (H&M) — escalating commitment tension

## Intended Audience

- **Age:** 10+
- **Player count:** 2–5
- **Playtime:** 15–25 minutes
- **Vibe:** Clean, snappy, immediately readable. Excellent for mixed-skill groups. Works as a filler or main event.
