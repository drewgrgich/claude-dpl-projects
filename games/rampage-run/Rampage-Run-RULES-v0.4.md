# RAMPAGE RUN — Game Rules v0.4

*Designer Corps — Phase 2 Draft*
*Iteration 4 of ~5*

---

## Concept

**Rampage Run** is a fast press-your-luck card game about monster crews tearing through a city block. Players build one shared **Streak** in the center of the table. On your turn, you either:

- **Play** to keep the rampage going
- **Fold** to bank the damage already done
- **Crash** to wipe the whole scene and deny the next player a clean payout

The tension is simple: **bank now, or make the run bigger and risk losing it.**

---

## Goal

Win by reaching **50+ banked points**, or by having the highest banked score when the deck runs out and the final round ends.

---

## The Deck

- **66 cards** total
- **6 factions** x **11 ranks** each
- Ranks are **0–10**
- Card value = its rank
- **0** is wild: it counts as any rank **1–10** for matching the current target, but still adds **0 points** to the Streak Total

### Factions

The six factions are just flavor in the base game: six different monster crews causing the damage. They matter for theme and table readability, not for powers.

---

## Setup

1. Shuffle the deck.
2. Deal **5 cards** to each player.
3. Place the rest face-down as the **draw pile**.
4. Flip the top card of the draw pile to begin the **Streak Pile**.
   - Its rank becomes the first **Current Target**.
   - Its value becomes the first **Streak Total**.
5. Starting player: whoever holds the lowest rank in hand. Break ties clockwise.

---

## Read the Table

At all times, players should know:

- the **Current Target** = top rank in the Streak Pile
- the **Streak Total** = total points in the Streak Pile
- who played the most recent card

---

## Turn Options

On your turn, choose **one** of these:

### A. PLAY
Play 1 card from your hand that matches or beats the Current Target.

- A card is legal if its rank is **equal to or higher than** the Current Target.
- A **0** may be declared as any rank **1–10** so it is always legal.
- After you play, your card becomes the new **Current Target**.
- Add the card's printed rank to the **Streak Total**.

### Post-Play Fold
After playing, you may immediately **Fold** instead of passing the turn. This lets you add one more card to the run and then bank the whole Streak yourself.

### B. FOLD
Take the current Streak for yourself.

1. Add the **Streak Total** to your banked score.
2. Discard the whole Streak Pile.
3. Draw back up to **5 cards**.
4. If cards remain in the draw pile, flip a new card to begin the next Streak.
5. End your turn.

**Important:** You may Fold even if you have a legal play.

### C. CRASH
If you have at least one legal play, you may choose to **Crash** instead of playing or folding.

1. Discard the whole Streak Pile.
2. If your personal shield is already gone, the player who played immediately before you scores the Streak Total as a **Crash Bonus**.
3. If your shield is still available, you spend it and **no Crash Bonus** is awarded.
4. Draw back up to **5 cards**.
5. If cards remain in the draw pile, flip a new card to begin the next Streak.
6. End your turn.

**No legal play?** Then you cannot Crash. Just Fold and reset with **0 points banked**.

---

## Personal Shield

Each player begins the game with **one personal shield**.

- The **first time you Crash**, the shield is spent.
- That first Crash clears the Streak but awards **no Crash Bonus**.
- Any later Crash follows the normal bonus rule.

Use any easy reminder at the table: a marker, a coin, or one face-down card.

---

## Scoring Summary

| Event | Who scores? | Points |
|---|---|---:|
| Fold | Active player | Full Streak Total |
| Crash with shield available | Nobody | 0 |
| Crash after shield is gone | Previous player | Full Streak Total |
| Play and pass | Nobody yet | 0 |

---

## End of Game

### Instant Win
If any player reaches **50 or more banked points** after scoring, that player wins immediately.

### Empty Deck
If the draw pile runs out:

- finish the current round so every player gets one final turn
- if a reset would normally start a new Streak but no draw cards remain, the existing Streak simply continues if one exists
- once the deck is empty, a Crash clears the Streak but awards **no Crash Bonus**
- highest banked score wins

### Ties
Break ties in this order:
1. most cards left in hand
2. fewest Crashes
3. sudden-death extra round

---

## 2-Player Rule

In a 2-player game, a Crash never gives a Crash Bonus.

- Clear the Streak
- Draw back up to 5
- Start a new Streak if possible

The personal shield still works the same way.

---

## Quick Teaching Script

**On your turn:**
1. **Play** to keep the run alive,
2. **Fold** to bank the pile,
3. or **Crash** to blow up the pile on purpose.

**Zeros are wild.**
**First Crash is protected by your shield.**
**First to 50 wins.**

---

## Why This Version Is Better

- The turn structure is shorter and easier to teach.
- Crash timing is now explicit: you choose it only when you could legally play.
- The shield rule is separated and easier to remember.
- The monster-crew framing gives the factions a cleaner thematic hook without adding extra rules.

---

## Intended Audience

- **Age:** 10+
- **Players:** 2–5
- **Playtime:** 15–25 minutes
- **Feel:** loud, mean, quick, readable

---

## Playtest Checklist

- [ ] Do players understand Play / Fold / Crash after one example?
- [ ] Does the Post-Play Fold create interesting choices?
- [ ] Does the personal shield feel fair?
- [ ] Are monster-crew factions flavorful enough without adding complexity?
- [ ] Does 50 points still end most games in the target time?
