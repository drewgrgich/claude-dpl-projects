# Bid Brawl Rules v1.1

Targeted iteration to improve comeback texture, memorable reveals, and reduce flat low-bid rounds.

## Core deck and round structure
- Keep the same 58-card deck and 5-card hands.
- Market size remains 2P=3, 3P=4, 4P=4, 5P=5.
- Simultaneous blind bidding, feeding, Hearts draw, Diamonds peek, Clubs public discard, and 2P direct-feeding all still apply.

## Changes from v1.0

### 1) Underdog Push (new passive)
- At the start of each round, if exactly one player is in last place on points, that player gets **+1 effective bid rank** on bids of rank 9 or lower for that round.
- This does not change printed point value, only bid strength.
- Purpose: increase comeback windows and make low-card timing more interesting.

### 2) Spike Prize (new market rule)
- After revealing the normal market, reveal **one extra prize card**.
- Only the **highest-ranked bidder** may take from that extra slot.
- If they do, they must take the **lowest-value normal prize** instead of the extra prize as their standard pick, effectively converting first place into a swingy choice.
- In the simulator, first place simply chooses the better of the extra prize or the best normal prize, and the skipped card remains available to later bidders.
- Purpose: create sharper first-place tension and more table talk moments.

### 3) Face-Feed Flair (new thematic trigger)
- If you feed an opponent a **J, Q, K, or A**, gain **1 Flair**.
- Each Flair is worth **1 point at end of game**, max 3 per player.
- Purpose: reward dramatic feeding and make the game’s signature interaction feel more memorable.

These are intentionally light-touch changes aimed at pacing, comeback potential, and vibe without changing the core identity of the game.
