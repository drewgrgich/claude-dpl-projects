# Kahu Fun Audit Results

*500 AI-vs-AI simulations, 3 players, all expert-level heuristic AI. March 2026.*

---

## Overall Grade: A

Kahu delivers a consistently engaging experience across all seven measured dimensions. The game produces meaningful decisions on nearly every turn, tight finishes, frequent lead changes, and reliable "wow" moments. One dimension — Tension Curve — earns a B rather than an A, keeping the overall result just inside the A threshold.

---

## Dimension Scores

### 1. Decision Density — A

**87% of turns offer 2+ meaningful choice categories. Average: 2.4 categories per turn.**

Players almost always face a genuine decision about *what kind* of action to take, not just which version of the same action. On a typical turn, a player chooses between buying market cards, purchasing Pua, completing an Offering, claiming a Tiki, or buying Surf — and usually has 2–3 of these available simultaneously. The market row discount slot adds a layer of timing ("do I buy this now or wait for it to slide to the discount slot?") that keeps even routine purchasing turns interesting.

The choice count stays high throughout the game. Early turns offer slightly more raw options (7.4 categories in round 0) because the market is full and Pua prices are low. Late turns have fewer options (5.4 categories) but each one is higher-stakes, which preserves the feeling of agency.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Turns with 2+ choice categories | 87% |
| Avg choice categories per turn | 2.4 |
| Grade threshold | A ≥ 85% |

---

### 2. Comeback Potential — A

**Early leader loses 55% of games. Average of 6.6 lead changes per game.**

Kahu has excellent comeback mechanics built into its structure. The player leading after round 1 goes on to lose more often than they win. This happens because early leads are built on Pua collection and first-mover advantage on cheap Offerings, while final scoring is dominated by card VP and Offering bonuses — which reward patient engine-building.

The lead changes 6–7 times per game on average, meaning the perceived winner shifts roughly every other round. This keeps all players engaged because no one feels locked out.

The "last to top 2" metric — how often the player in last place at halftime finishes in the top 2 — sits at 59%, meaning the trailing player has a real shot at recovery more often than not.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Early leader loses | 55% |
| Avg lead changes per game | 6.6 |
| Last-place-at-half finishes top 2 | 59% |
| Grade threshold | A ≥ 40% comeback rate |

---

### 3. Dead Turn Rate — A

**0.0% of turns are dead. 99% of games have zero dead turns.**

This is Kahu's strongest dimension. Across 500 games (~18,000 total turns), virtually no turn produces zero progress. Even when a player can't buy Pua (due to missing icons) or complete an Offering, the market row always offers something worth purchasing. The combination of market cards, Surf supply, and Pua market guarantees that influence is never completely wasted.

The worst single game in the entire sample had only 3% dead turns — about 1 dead turn in a 36-turn game. This is exceptional for a deck-builder where hand variance can easily produce unproductive turns.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Avg dead turn rate | 0.0% |
| Games with zero dead turns | 99% |
| Worst single game | 3% dead turns |
| Grade threshold | A < 5% |

---

### 4. Blowout Rate — A

**Only 4% of games are 2× blowouts. 50% of games decided by ≤3 VP.**

Half of all games come down to 3 VP or less — roughly the value of a single Offering bonus. This means final scoring is genuinely tense: a player who completed one more Wildlife card, or who thinned their deck one more time, could swing the outcome.

The average winner's margin is 4.5 VP, which is small enough to feel competitive but large enough that wins don't feel arbitrary. Only 4% of games produce a 2× score ratio (e.g., 40 to 20), meaning blowouts are rare.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| 2× blowout rate | 4% |
| Close games (≤3 VP margin) | 50% |
| Avg winner margin | 4.5 VP |
| Grade threshold | A < 5% blowout rate |

### 5. Tension Curve — B

**43% of games have ascending tension. Final third accounts for 46% of VP scored.**

The VP distribution across the three phases of the game shows the right overall shape — scoring accelerates toward the end:

| Phase | VP Scored (avg) | Share |
|---|---|---|
| Early (rounds 1–6) | 4.1 | 18% |
| Mid (rounds 7–12) | 8.2 | 36% |
| Late (rounds 13+) | 10.3 | 46% |

The final third of the game produces nearly half of all VP, which is ideal for building excitement toward the finish. However, only 43% of individual games show a *strictly* ascending curve (early < mid < late). In the other 57%, the mid-game sometimes scores more than the late game, or the early game spikes due to a fast first Offering completion.

This is the one dimension holding Kahu back from a perfect score. The mid-game Pua grind (detailed in the separate lull analysis) creates a plateau in rounds 5–9 where players are accumulating resources but not crossing finish lines, which flattens the tension arc.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Strictly ascending curve | 43% of games |
| Final third VP share | 46% |
| VP progression (E → M → L) | 4.1 → 8.2 → 10.3 |
| Grade threshold | A ≥ 50% ascending + ≥ 38% final third |

### 6. Power Fantasy — A

**100% of games have wow moments. Average of 9.0 per game.**

Every single game in the sample produced at least one "wow turn" — a turn where something exciting happened. Wow turns are defined as: completing an Offering, buying 3+ cards in one turn, or acquiring 3+ Pua in one turn.

At 9.0 wow turns per game across ~36 turns, roughly 1 in 4 turns produces a memorable moment. This is a high rate that reflects Kahu's engine-building payoff: as decks grow stronger, turns with 8–10 influence become common, enabling big multi-purchase turns that feel rewarding.

The Offering completion moments are especially strong power fantasy events because they combine resource spending (returning Pua), VP gain (taking a token), and strategic advancement (locking in an endgame bonus) into a single dramatic action.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Games with wow moments | 100% |
| Avg wow turns per game | 9.0 |
| Wow turn rate | ~25% of turns |
| Grade threshold | A ≥ 70% of games |

---

### 7. Interaction Rate — A

**61% of turns involve player interaction. Average of 22.6 interaction events per game.**

Kahu achieves high interaction without direct conflict mechanics. The primary interaction channel is the Pua market: every Pua purchase shifts prices for all players, creating a shared economy where one player's buy directly affects another's costs. This accounts for the majority of interaction events and ensures that players must watch each other's Pua collections and plan around price movements.

Secondary interaction comes from card effects: Chicken (reveal and steal from opponent's deck), Nene Goose (copy from opponent's discard), and the mutual-benefit Islander effects (both players draw or gain Pua). These appear less frequently but create memorable moments of direct engagement.

The Offering race also creates implicit interaction — competing for the 4-VP first-completion token — though this isn't counted in the interaction metric since it doesn't involve a direct action targeting another player.

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Turns with interaction | 61% |
| Avg interactions per game | 22.6 |
| Primary interaction source | Pua market price shifts |
| Secondary sources | Chicken, Nene Goose, mutual Islanders |
| Grade threshold | A > 15% |

## Game Health Metrics

Beyond the seven fun dimensions, the simulation tracks overall game health:

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Avg game length | 37.6 turns (12.5 rounds) |
| Game length range | 21–60 turns |
| End by Offerings (intended) | 97.8% |
| End by lava eruption | 2.2% |
| Avg final lava position | 7.9 |
| Avg lava advances | 12.1 |
| Avg influence per turn | 6.7 |
| Avg total score | ~30 VP |

Games consistently end via the intended win condition (completing 3 Offerings) rather than lava eruption, lasting about 12 rounds. The lava track typically reaches position 7–8, meaning most escalation events fire (Second Lava at 15, Tiki Lockout at 12, Market Wipe at 10) but the final events (Pua Price Increase at 7, second Market Wipe at 5) trigger in roughly half of games. This is a good distribution — the escalation events create mid-game drama while the eruption threat looms without dominating.

## Score Composition

The three components of final scoring contribute unevenly:

| Component | Avg VP | Share |
|---|---|---|
| Card VP | 16.1 | 54% |
| VP Tokens | 7.5 | 25% |
| Offering Bonuses | 6.5 | 21% |

Card VP (printed on market cards) accounts for over half of final scoring, making deck-building the dominant strategy. VP Tokens from Offerings contribute a quarter, and Offering bonuses (endgame multipliers) contribute the remaining fifth. This weighting favors patient engine-building over racing to complete Offerings — a dynamic explored further in the balance analysis.

| Offering | Pua Cost | Completions/Game | Bonus Type |
|---|---|---|---|
| Card Types | RBY (3) | 2.4 | 1 VP per card type (max 6) |
| Surfs | RBY (3) | 2.0 | 1 VP per Surf owned |
| Tikis | RBY (3) | 1.8 | 3 VP per Tiki owned |
| Flowers | RBY (3) | 1.7 | 1 VP per Flower owned |
| Removed Cards | RRBY (4) | 0.9 | 1 VP per card removed |
| Items | RBYY (4) | 0.9 | 1 VP per Item owned |
| Wildlife | RBBY (4) | 0.8 | 1 VP per Wildlife owned |
| Islanders | RBYY (4) | 0.7 | 1 VP per Islander owned |

The 3-Pua Offerings are completed roughly twice as often as the 4-Pua ones. This creates variability depending on which four Offerings are randomly selected at the start of the game — a set heavy on 4-Pua Offerings will play slower and grindier.

## Methodology

The fun audit uses seven quantitative dimensions adapted from the game-simulator fun measurement framework. Each dimension is graded A through F based on thresholds calibrated for competitive card games:

| Grade | Decision Density | Comeback | Dead Turns | Blowout | Tension | Power Fantasy | Interaction |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| A | ≥85% 2+ choices | ≥40% comeback | <5% dead | <5% blowout | ≥50% ascending | ≥70% wow games | >15% rate |
| B | ≥70% | ≥30% | <10% | <10% | ≥40% ascending | ≥50% | >10% |
| C | ≥50% | ≥20% | <20% | <20% | ≥30% final third | ≥30% | >5% |
| D | ≥30% | ≥10% | <35% | <35% | ≥25% | ≥15% | >2% |
| F | <30% | <10% | ≥35% | ≥35% | <25% | <15% | ≤2% |

All games use seeded randomness for reproducibility. The AI plays at expert skill level (1.0) with balanced style and 0.5 aggression, representing competent play without strategic bias.
