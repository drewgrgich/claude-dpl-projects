# Contests of Chaos — Official Rules

*A card-drafting competition for 2–4 players. Build your roster, complete events, and trigger the Standing Ovation.*

---

## Components

- **66 Recruit Cards** — 6 factions, ranks 0–10 each (11 cards per faction)
- **37 Event Cards** — Tiers 1–6, the challenges you're competing to complete
- **24 Playbook Cards** — Secret objectives for bonus VP
- **Shiny tokens** — the game's currency
- **4 Player Aid Cards**

### The Six Factions

| Code | Name |
|------|------|
| RED | Super-Dupes |
| ORG | Finders-Keepers |
| YLW | Tinkerers |
| GRN | Prognosticationers |
| BLU | Magicians |
| PUR | Time Travelers |

### Free Agents

Rank 0 and Rank 10 cards are **Free Agents**. A Free Agent can count as any faction when completing events — but it needs a **Buddy**. For every faction you claim using a Free Agent, at least one card in your submission must be a signed player (ranks 1–9) of that faction. One Buddy covers all Free Agents claiming that faction.

*Example: You need 3 Magicians. You play BLU-7 (Buddy), BLU-0 (Free Agent counting as Magician — has Buddy BLU-7), and YLW-10 (Free Agent counting as Magician — same Buddy covers it). Legal play.*

---

## Setup

### For 3 Players

1. **Shuffle** the Recruit Deck and place it face-down as **The Stands**.
2. **Deal 4 cards** face-up in a row to form **The Lineup** (the draft market). Place 1 Shiny token on each card in the Lineup.
3. **Shuffle** the Event Deck. Deal 4 events face-up in a row to form **The Jumbotron**.
4. **Shuffle** the Playbook Deck.
5. **Starting Shinies:** Player 1 receives 3, Player 2 receives 4, Player 3 receives 4.
6. **Starting Hand:** Deal 5 Recruit Cards to each player. Each player keeps 3 and discards 2 face-down to the bottom of The Stands.
7. **Playbook Draft:** Deal 2 Playbook Cards face-down to each player. Each player keeps 1 (secret) and returns the other to the bottom of the Playbook Deck.

### For 2 Players

Same as above, except: Player 1 receives 3 Shinies, Player 2 receives 4. Victory threshold is 25 Event VP (instead of 30). **Coach's Toll** is active: whenever a Recruit Card is drafted from Lineup Slot 1 for free, place 1 Shiny from the bank onto the new card that slides into Slot 1.

### For 4 Players

Same as 3 players, except: starting Shinies are 3, 4, 4, 5 (by seat order).

---

## The Lineup

The Lineup is a sliding market of 4 face-up Recruit Cards. Each slot has a price:

| Slot | Cost |
|------|------|
| Slot 1 (leftmost) | Free |
| Slot 2 | 1 Shiny |
| Slot 3 | 2 Shinies |
| Slot 4 (rightmost) | 3 Shinies |

When a card is drafted from any slot, all cards to the right slide left to fill the gap. A new card is drawn from The Stands to fill the rightmost empty slot. Any Shinies sitting on a drafted card are collected by the player who drafts it.

At the start of each round, place 1 Shiny from the bank onto each Lineup card that already has a card (Shinies accumulate on undrafted cards, making them more attractive over time).

---

## The Jumbotron

The Jumbotron holds 4 face-up Event Cards. Events slide left the same way as the Lineup — when one is completed, cards shift left and a new event is drawn from the Event Deck to fill the rightmost slot.

---

## On Your Turn

On your turn, take **one** of the following actions:

### 1. Recruit from the Lineup

Draft one card from the Lineup by paying its slot cost. Collect any Shinies sitting on that card. The Lineup slides and refills.

### 2. Scramble

Pay 2 Shinies (1 in a 2-player game). Discard the card in Lineup Slot 1 to the bottom of The Stands. Draw the top card of The Stands directly into your hand (a "mystery draw"). The Lineup does not slide — the Slot 1 position stays empty until refill.

### 3. Complete an Event

Choose one face-up event from the Jumbotron. Commit cards from your hand that satisfy its requirements. Those cards are discarded to the bottom of The Stands. You score the event's VP, gain its reward (if any), and the Jumbotron slides and refills.

Cards committed to an event are gone from your hand permanently. Choose carefully.

### 4. Timeout

Gain 1 Shiny from the bank. You may optionally **discard** up to 2 cards from your hand (to the bottom of The Stands).

Timeout is your reset button. Use it to build up Shinies and shed dead-weight cards when nothing else is worth doing.

### 5. The Agent

Pay 5 Shinies. Draw 2 Playbook Cards, keep 1, return the other to the bottom of the Playbook Deck. This is the only way to gain additional Playbooks mid-game — use it when your current Playbook doesn't match your board position or when you want a second scoring objective.

### 6. Wipe the Jumbotron

Pay **5 Shinies**. You must have **at least 5 cards in your hand**. Discard all 4 Jumbotron events to the bottom of the Event Deck and deal 4 new events.

**Post-Wipe Event Completion:** After wiping, you may immediately complete one of the new events on the same turn — but you must pay an additional **2-Shiny surcharge** on top of any normal costs.

*The Wipe is a high-risk, high-reward play. You're betting 5 Shinies that the new events will be better for you than the old ones. The hand size requirement prevents abuse — you can't wipe after dumping your hand into an event. The surcharge ensures that wiping into an immediate score is expensive enough to be a real decision, not an automatic combo.*

---

## Hand Limit

Your hand limit is **8 cards**. At the end of your turn, if you exceed 8, discard down to 8 (your choice of which to discard).

---

## Event Requirements

Events have requirements written on them. Common patterns:

- **Faction counts:** "3x Magicians" — commit 3 cards of that faction (Free Agents can substitute with a Buddy).
- **Multi-faction:** "2x Super-Dupes + 1x Tinkerer" — commit cards from multiple factions.
- **Sum requirements:** "Sum ≥ 12" — the ranks of your committed cards must total at least 12.
- **Runs:** "3-card Run" — commit 3 cards with consecutive ranks (e.g., 4-5-6).
- **Sets:** "3 cards with the same number" — commit 3 cards sharing a rank.
- **Different factions:** "4 cards showing 4 different faction symbols" — faction diversity.
- **Free Agent specific:** "4x Free Agents" — commit 4 rank-0 or rank-10 cards.
- **Low-sum (Limbo):** "4 cards with Sum ≤ 8" — commit low-rank cards.

Some events combine multiple requirements (e.g., faction count + sum minimum).

---

## Event Rewards

When you complete an event, resolve its reward immediately. Common rewards:

- "Take 2 Shinies from the bank."
- "Perform one free Scramble."
- "Draw top 3 from The Stands, keep 1."
- "Draft Lineup Slot 2 for free."
- "Look at top 5 of The Stands, take 1."

---

## Playbooks

Each player has one secret Playbook card. Playbooks are bonus VP scored at specific moments:

- **Combo** timing — triggers when you complete an event that meets the Playbook's condition (e.g., "Complete an Event using 3 or fewer cards").
- **Finish** timing — triggers at end of turn if a condition is met (e.g., "End of turn: 2 or fewer cards in hand and you completed an Event this turn").
- **Immediate** timing — triggers when you take a specific action (e.g., "Recruit a card from Lineup Slot 4").

When a Playbook triggers, reveal it and score its VP immediately. You keep the Playbook face-up as a scored card.

---

## Scoring and Victory

### Standing Ovation

The game enters its final phase when any player reaches **30 Event VP** (25 in a 2-player game). This triggers a **Standing Ovation** — every other player gets exactly one more turn, then the game ends.

### Final Tally

Each player's total VP is:

**Event VP + Playbook VP + Shiny Bonus**

**Shiny Bonus:** At game end, every 3 Shinies you have converts to 1 VP, up to a maximum of 3 bonus VP.

| Shinies | Bonus VP |
|---------|----------|
| 0–2 | 0 |
| 3–5 | 1 |
| 6–8 | 2 |
| 9+ | 3 |

The player with the highest total VP wins. Ties are broken by most events completed, then most Shinies remaining.

## Event Tier Reference

Events are organized into 6 tiers of escalating difficulty and reward:

| Tier | Cards Needed | VP Range | Description |
|------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | 3 | 3 | Single-faction basics — one event per faction |
| 2 | 2–3 | 4–6 | Mixed factions with sum requirements |
| 3 | 3–4 | 5–8 | Mechanical variety: runs, sets, sums, multi-faction |
| 4 | 5 | 8–10 | Faction + 2 Free Agents, high sum thresholds |
| 5 | 5 | 9–13 | Deep faction commitment (4x + FA limit) |
| 6 | 3–6 | 12–16 | Special challenges: big runs, sets, Group Photo |

Lower tiers appear earlier in the game and are easier to complete. Higher tiers demand more cards but offer dramatically more VP. A typical winning strategy completes 4–5 events mixing Tier 1–3 volume with one or two high-tier payoffs.

## Strategy Notes for New Players

**Start with your factions.** Look at the Jumbotron and your starting hand. Which factions overlap? Draft toward those.

**Free Agents are gold.** A rank 0 or 10 card fits into almost any event as long as you have a Buddy. Prioritize drafting them early.

**Don't hoard.** Sitting on 8 cards hoping for the perfect event means you're burning turns. Complete Tier 1–2 events to stay on pace, even if the VP seems small.

**Watch the Lineup Shinies.** Cards in Slot 1 accumulate Shinies over time. A free card with 3+ Shinies on it is almost always worth drafting.

**Wipe strategically.** If the Jumbotron has nothing you can complete and nothing close, spending 5 Shinies to reset it can save you from a long stagnation. But make sure you have 5 cards in hand first.

**Your Playbook shapes your game.** If your Playbook rewards completing events with 3 or fewer cards, lean into Tier 1–2 events. If it rewards big events (9+ VP), invest in building toward Tier 4–6.

## Design Notes (Simulation-Backed)

*These notes explain why specific values were chosen, based on hundreds of AI-simulated games.*

**Wipe cost of 5** was selected over costs of 3, 4, and 6. At cost 3–4, wipes happened nearly 3 times per game and players could wipe-and-score too freely, distorting the economy. At cost 6, wipes almost never happened and stagnation increased. Cost 5 produces ~2.1 wipes per game — enough to be a real strategic option without dominating play. The win rate spread across players at cost 5 is the tightest observed at 8 percentage points.

**Hand minimum of 5** for wiping prevents the "score then wipe" degenerate pattern where a player completes a big event (dumping 5 cards), then immediately wipes the Jumbotron with their remaining resources. Counterintuitively, this requirement makes games *shorter* and more eventful — it forces players to maintain hand size, which means they're more likely to have completable events in hand.

**Post-wipe surcharge of 2 Shinies** prevents the wipe from being a guaranteed combo play. Without it, wiping into an immediate score is too consistent. With it, the total cost of a wipe-and-score is 7 Shinies — a serious investment that's only worth it for high-VP events. The surcharge barely changes average game length but measurably tightens VP spreads across players.

**Playbooks add ~2.4 VP per player** (~9% of total), which is meaningful without distorting core strategy. Games with and without playbooks show nearly identical game length and event completion patterns — playbooks reward sharp play rather than warping it.

**Lineup slot pricing at 0/1/2/3** results in ~76% of drafts coming from Slot 1 (free), with diminishing usage at higher slots. This is intended — expensive slots should be reserved for high-value picks, not routine drafting. The Shiny accumulation on undrafted cards creates a natural pressure release: unpopular cards eventually become valuable for their Shinies alone.

**30 VP threshold** triggers Standing Ovation in 98% of games before the turn limit, confirming the event VP curve is well-calibrated. A typical winner completes 4–5 events over ~60 turns.
