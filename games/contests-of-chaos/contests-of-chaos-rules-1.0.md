# 🏆 Contests of Chaos

### A Card-Drafting Showdown for 2–4 Coaches

**System:** Hamsters and Monsters Core Deck (66 cards) · **Time:** 30–45 Minutes · **Ages:** 10+

------

## What's Going On Here?

The **Stadium** is packed. The confetti cannons are loaded. Somewhere in the bleachers, a hamster is eating popcorn that definitely belongs to someone else.

Welcome to the **Contests of Chaos** — the most ridiculous athletic spectacle the fuzzy multiverse has ever witnessed. Every season, the six factions send their finest competitors to battle it out in events ranging from *Sack Races Through the Fourth Dimension* to *Staring Contests Judged on Aerodynamic Creativity* to the legendary *Free-For-All Dodgeball Battle Royale*.

You're a **Head Coach**, assembling a squad of elite hamsters and monsters to compete. The draft board shifts constantly. Your rivals are scouting the same talent. The snack table is running low. And somewhere out there, a Time Traveler insists they've already won this tournament a thousand times.

Every coach arrives with a pouch of **Shinies** — those glittering coins that make hamster eyes go wide and monster hearts beat faster. Talent doesn't come cheap. The best athletes want to see the glint of gold before they'll even look at your roster. You'll spend Shinies to recruit, Shinies to score, and if you're clever, you'll walk away with more than you started.

Complete events. Earn points. Cross the victory threshold before anyone else — and claim the **Championship Trophy** for another glorious year.

Everyone else? Face-down in the Stands. Forgotten. Fuel for the next cycle.

*Okay, everyone squeeze in. Tall monsters in the back. Stop making faces. This is for posterity.*

------

## What You're Trying to Do

**Win the game** by being the first coach to reach **30 Victory Points** from Events (or **25 VP** in a two-player grudge match). Playbooks and Shinies add to your Final Tally — they can swing who wins, but only Events can end the game.

### How do you earn points?

**Complete Events.** The Jumbotron always has four events on display, worth anywhere from 3 to 16 VP each. Draft the right cards, play them to the table, score the points. Simple. (It is not simple. Your rivals are watching the same screen.)

**Score Playbooks.** *(Advanced Module)* Secret objectives only you know about. Complete the condition, reveal the Playbook, score bonus VP.

**Hoard Shinies.** At game end, every 3 Shinies you've hoarded converts to 1 bonus VP (maximum 3 VP). Not game-changing, but occasionally the difference between *Champion* and *Also Ran*.

### What are Shinies?

Shinies are your currency. Your leverage. Small creatures love shiny things — it's instinct — and in the Stadium, that instinct is economics.

**You earn Shinies by:**

- Calling a Timeout (reliable, strategic, effective)
- Swiping Shinies that are sitting on Lineup cards you draft
- Certain event rewards

**You spend Shinies to:**

- Draft better cards from the Lineup (good cards cost more — that's capitalism, baby)
- Scramble the board when the Lineup is garbage
- Call in The Agent for secret Playbooks *(Advanced Module)*

## What You Need

**66 Recruit Cards** — That's your full Hamsters & Monsters deck. Six factions, numbered 0 through 10 each. These are your athletes.

**37 Event Cards** — Athletic competitions to enter and complete. Each shows what cards you need, how many points it's worth, and what reward you get.

**Shinies (30–40 tokens)** — Any shiny metal coins, chips, or tokens will do. Hamsters and monsters aren't picky about *which* Shinies — just that they glitter.

**Playbook Cards (Optional)** — Secret scoring objectives. Include these when you want hidden information and long-term scheming.

**The Six Factions:**

## The Board (Such As It Is)

You'll set up two rows of four cards each:

**The Lineup** — Four face-up Recruit cards. This is where you draft athletes for your team.

**The Jumbotron** — Four face-up Event cards. The big screen shows what competitions are running. This is what you're trying to win.

Both rows slide and refill the same way:

- **Slot 1** (leftmost) is the oldest
- **Slot 4** (rightmost) is the newest
- When a card leaves, everything slides left to fill the gap
- New cards always enter at Slot 4

**Lineup Shinies:** When you draft from the Lineup, you pay by placing Shinies on cards to the left of your pick (see Recruit action). Those Shinies sit on the cards until someone drafts them. This means the Lineup's leftmost cards accumulate Shinies over time — sometimes the free card in Slot 1 comes with a pile of gold on it.

> **Why this matters:** A card in Slot 4 costs 3 Shinies to draft (you drop 1 Shiny on each card to its left). But if someone drafts Slot 2 first, that Slot 4 card slides to Slot 3 — now it costs only 2 Shinies. The board is alive. Timing is everything.

**The Recruit Deck** — Face-down pile of Recruit cards. If the Recruit Deck runs out, shuffle The Stands to form a new one. (The benched athletes get another shot. Don't tell them it's because everyone else is tired.)

**The Event Deck** — Face-down pile of Event cards.

**The Stands** — Face-down discard pile. During normal play, no peeking — those athletes had their shot. But some event rewards let you dig through The Stands and rescue someone. When an effect says "take a card from The Stands," look through the entire pile and choose one. Then shuffle The Stands back together. (The rescued athlete is grateful. Probably.)

## Free Agents (Rank 0 and 10)

Most cards are exactly what they say: a Tinkerer-7 is a Tinkerer with value 7. End of story.

"But **0s and 10s are Free Agents** — versatile athletes who *do* belong to a faction (shown on the card) but may also play for any other faction when needed. Magician-10 is a Magician who can also fill in elsewhere. They're not unsigned — they're just not *exclusive*."

Free Agents can count as **any faction** when completing events. Need three Magicians but only have two? That Tinkerer-0 can put on a top hat and pretend.

**The Buddy System:** Free Agents are divas. They won't take the field unless a friend from the team is already there. For each faction you're claiming, at least one card must be a signed player (ranks 1–9) of that faction. Free Agents fill in the gaps, not the whole roster.

> **Example:** An event needs 3 Finders-Keepers. You play Finders-Keepers-7 (the Buddy), Finders-Keepers-10 (Free Agent), and Tinkerer-0 (Free Agent counting as Finders-Keepers). This works — the 7 is the Buddy that lets the Free Agents join the team. Three Free Agents with no Buddy? That's just a pile of confused hamsters in street clothes. Doesn't count.

**Important:** Free Agents keep their printed number (0 or 10) for sum requirements. They're flexible about *who* they play for, not *how good* they are.

## Setup (Pre-Season Training Camp)

1. **Shuffle** the 66 Recruit cards. Deal **4 face-up** in a row — this is **The Lineup**. Stack the rest as **the Recruit Deck**.

2. **Shuffle** the 37 Event cards. Deal **4 face-up** in a row — this is **The Jumbotron**. Stack the rest as **the Event Deck**.

3. **Draft your starting roster:** Deal **5 Recruit cards** to each player. Look at them. Keep **3**. Discard **2** face-down to The Stands. (Those hamsters didn't make the cut. Don't think about it too hard.)

4. **Starting Shinies:**

   - 2 players: Player 1 gets 3, Player 2 gets 4
   - 3 players: 3, 4, 4
   - 4 players: 3, 4, 4, 5

   (Going later is a disadvantage. Shinies compensate. The system works.)

5. **Pick a starting player.** Whoever looks most like a hamster. Play proceeds clockwise.

6. **Playbook Setup (Optional — Advanced Module):**

   - Shuffle the Playbook deck
   - Deal **2 Playbooks** face-down to each player
   - Each player looks at their Playbooks, **keeps 1**, places the other face-down on the **bottom of the Playbook deck**
   - Keep your Playbook secret and in hand — it counts toward your hand limit
   - **Playbook Limits:** Maximum **2 Playbooks** in hand at any time (all player counts)

The draft is open. The games begin.

## Your Turn: Pick ONE Action

On your turn, you do exactly **one** thing. Not two things. Not zero things. One thing. Choose wisely.

------

### 🏃 ACTION A: RECRUIT (Draft a Card)

You want better athletes? You're going to have to pay for them.

#### Option 1: The Lineup Draft

Pick a card from the Lineup. Pay the Slot Price by placing **1 Shiny from your supply onto each card to the left** of your choice.

| Slot   | Cost                          |
| ------ | ----------------------------- |
| Slot 1 | Free! (Nothing to the left)   |
| Slot 2 | 1 Shiny (on Slot 1)           |
| Slot 3 | 2 Shinies (on Slots 1 & 2)    |
| Slot 4 | 3 Shinies (on Slots 1, 2 & 3) |

Take all Shinies sitting on your chosen card — those are yours now. Some other coach sweetened the pot for you. (Thanks, some other coach.)

Slide remaining cards left. Deal a new card to Slot 4 from the Recruit Deck.

> *"The chalkboard is full of diagrams. The plan is perfect. Who drew a cat on the map?"*

#### Option 2: The Scramble

Sometimes the Lineup is garbage. Shake it up.

**Cost:** 2 Shinies (or **1 Shiny** in a 2-player game).

Here's the sequence:

1. Discard Slot 1 to The Stands (goodbye, unwanted hamster)
2. Slide remaining cards left
3. Deal a new card to Slot 4 from the Recruit Deck
4. Place the Shinies you paid onto the new Slot 4 card
5. Take the top card of the Recruit Deck into your hand (mystery box!)

Any Shinies that were sitting on Slot 1 **also push** to the new Slot 4 card. That card is now a goldmine — and the next player is staring at it.

The Scramble costs more and you don't know what you're getting. But sometimes you need to shake up the board. *Brakes are optional. Ethics are flexible. Speed is mandatory.*

------

### ⏱️ ACTION B: TIMEOUT (Gather Resources)

Sometimes you need to step back, catch your breath, and regroup.

- Take **1 Shiny** from the supply
- **Optional:** Discard up to **2 cards** from your hand to The Stands
- **Optional:** Discard the **Slot 1 Event** from the Jumbotron to the bottom of the Event Deck. Slide remaining events left and deal a new event to Slot 4.

That last part is how the board stays fresh. Events enter at Slot 4, drift leftward as others get completed, and the stale ones eventually reach Slot 1 where a Timeout flushes them out. No extra cost — just stadium maintenance.

> *"I was just reaching for the popcorn and my elbow hit the scoreboard. My bad."*

> **2-Player Variant — The Coach's Toll:** When you call a Timeout in a 2-player game, also place **1 Shiny from the supply** onto Lineup Slot 1. This keeps the board from stagnating when there are only two of you circling each other.

------

### 🏅 ACTION C: COMPLETE EVENT (Score Points!)

This is why you're here. This is the trophy moment.

1. **Select** a face-up Event from the Jumbotron
2. **Reveal** cards from your hand that meet all requirements:
   - Match the faction icons (remember the Buddy System!)
   - Hit any sum, set, or run requirements shown
3. **Discard** used cards face-down to The Stands (opponents see them during the reveal, then they're filed away forever)
4. **Score** the Event — place it in your Victory Pile
5. **Resolve** the Event's Reward text immediately
6. **Slide** and refill the Jumbotron

> *"Link paws. Hold the line. The cookie jar will be defended."*

**Event Rewards** range from "take Shinies from the supply" to "steal a card from an opponent" to "take another free Scramble" to the deeply satisfying "opponent discards cards to the Stands." Each event tells you exactly what you get. Read the card. Do the thing.

------

### 🕵️ ACTION D: THE AGENT (Advanced Module)

*Only available when playing with Playbooks.*

Sometimes the draft isn't giving you what you need. Sometimes you need to call in a favor from someone who knows where the *real* opportunities are hiding.

**Cost:** Pay **5 Shinies** to the supply.

**Effect:**

1. Draw **2 Playbooks** from the Playbook deck
2. Keep **1**, place the other on the bottom of the Playbook deck
3. You may not exceed your Playbook limit (2 Playbooks). If you would, immediately place the excess on the bottom of the deck.

That's it. Expensive, but now you have options.

> *"I know a guy who knows a guy who definitely didn't steal that roster analysis from the other team's locker room."*

**When to use this:** When your current Playbook is impossible or nearly complete and you want insurance. When you're flush with Shinies and starving for direction. When your rival looks too comfortable.

------

## End of Your Turn

After your action resolves:

1. **Hand Limit:** If you have more than **8 cards**, discard down to 8. (Playbooks count toward this limit. Too much scheming, not enough roster space.)
2. **Playbook Check (Advanced Module):** You may reveal and score **one** completed Playbook. Show that the condition is met, add its VP to your score, and place it in your Victory Pile. Maximum one Playbook scored per turn, even if multiple are complete.
3. **Victory Check:** If your **Event VP** (not counting Playbooks) is **30+** (or **25+** in a 2-player game), trigger **The Standing Ovation**. Playbooks are scored at Final Tally — they can change who wins, but they can't end the game early.

------

## The Standing Ovation (Endgame)

When someone's **Event VP** crosses the victory threshold (30+ in multiplayer, 25+ in 2-player):

- **Finish the round** so all players have equal turns
- **Final Tally:**
  - Event VP + Playbook VP + Shiny Bonus (1 VP per 3 Shinies held, maximum 3 VP)
- **Tiebreaker:** Most completed Events worth 9+ VP individually (the big wins matter)

Note: Playbooks don't trigger the endgame — only Events do. But Playbook VP absolutely counts in the Final Tally. A coach who's behind on Events can still steal the championship with clever Playbook scoring.

Crown your champion. The confetti cannons fire. Somewhere, a Prognosticationer insists they knew this would happen all along.

------

## Deck Exhaustion

**The Recruit Deck** will run out. When it does, shuffle The Stands to form a new Recruit Deck. The benched athletes are back — refreshed, confused, and ready to be disappointed again.

**The Event Deck** does not refill. If the Event Deck runs out and the Jumbotron can't be refilled, finish the current round (equal turns), then proceed directly to Final Tally. The season is over.

## Understanding Event Requirements

Events come in tiers, from easy 3-pointers to brutal 16-point glory runs. Here's how to read them:

**Faction Requirements:** Icons showing which factions you need. Each icon needs at least one signed player (ranks 1–9) of that faction as a Buddy, plus Free Agents to fill the gaps.

> *Tug of War (3 VP): 3× Super-Dupes* "Pull hard, dig in, and pretend the rope isn't tearing your paws off."

**Sum Requirements:** Your cards' ranks must add up to a minimum (or exact) total.

> *Wheelbarrow Race (5 VP): 2× Super-Dupes + 1× Tinkerer, Sum ≥ 12* "One provides the muscle. One provides the wheels. Physics provides the humiliation."

**Sets and Runs:**

- **Set:** Multiple cards of the same rank (e.g., three 4s from any factions)
- **Run:** Consecutive ranks (e.g., 3-4-5). Mixed factions allowed — it's about the numbers, not the colors.

> *Convoy (6 VP): 3-card Run (consecutive ranks)* "Left, right, left! Stop stepping on my tail! Maintain formation!"

**Mixed Requirements:** Some events get creative. Read carefully.

> *Limbo Contest (8 VP): 4 cards with Sum ≤ 8* "We have flattened ourselves like pancakes. The bar is a suggestion, not a limit."

**Free Agent Events:** A few events specifically want Free Agents.

> *Sprinkler Ambush (12 VP): 3× Free Agents with Rank 0* "The timer clicked! The ground is erupting! Abandon the field! Save the snacks!"

## Strategy Notes (From the Coaching Manual)

**The Slide Is Your Friend:** When you can't afford Slot 4, sometimes the right play is waiting. Let someone else draft Slot 2, and suddenly Slot 4 becomes Slot 3. Patience is a weapon.

**Shinies Accumulate:** Those Shinies sitting on Lineup cards add up. Sometimes drafting a card you don't even need is worth it just to grab the 4 Shinies sitting on it.

**Watch the Jumbotron:** If your rival has two Magicians in hand and a 3× Magicians event is in Slot 2... maybe you draft those Magicians before they can. Or maybe you sprint to complete *your* event first. Tension is the game.

**Timeouts Clear the Board:** When the Jumbotron is clogged with events nobody can complete, Timeouts naturally flush Slot 1 and bring in fresh competitions. Don't wait for someone else to fix the board — take your Shinies *and* clear the junk.

**Free Agents Are Gold:** 0s and 10s are the utility players of your roster. They make impossible events possible. Hoard them.

**The Agent Is Insurance:** *(Advanced Module)* 5 Shinies is steep, but a second Playbook means a backup plan. Call The Agent when your current Playbook is blocked or when you're about to close it out and want another scoring opportunity.

*"I've already done this a thousand times. Trust me."*

## 2-Player Variant: The Grudge Match

Two-player Contests of Chaos is a knife fight. No hiding behind multiplayer chaos — every move is aimed directly at your rival. These adjustments keep the duel tight, interactive, and fair.

**Victory Threshold:** 25 Event VP (not 30).

**Starting Shinies:** Player 1 gets 3, Player 2 gets 4.

**The Coach's Toll:** When you call a Timeout, also place 1 Shiny from the supply onto Lineup Slot 1. This keeps the board moving when there's no third player to churn it.

**Scramble Discount:** The Scramble costs **1 Shiny** (not 2). That single Shiny goes onto the new Slot 4 card. Cheaper board churn prevents stagnation and creates tension: take the mediocre card, or gamble on the blind draw?

**Playbook Limit:** Maximum 2 Playbooks (same as multiplayer). This makes The Agent worth calling — you're adding options, not just swapping your only card.

> *Two coaches. One trophy. Zero mercy.*

## Quick Reference

**Victory:** First to 30 Event VP (25 VP in 2-player) triggers The Standing Ovation. Playbooks count in Final Tally only.

| Action             | What It Does                                                 |
| ------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Recruit**        | Draft from Lineup (pay Slot Price) or Scramble (2 Shinies / 1 in 2P) |

| **Complete Event** | Play cards matching requirements; score VP; resolve Reward   |
| **The Agent**      | Pay 5 Shinies; draw 2 Playbooks, keep 1 *(Advanced Module only)* |

**Slot Pricing (Lineup):**

- Slot 1: Free
- Slot 2: 1 Shiny on Slot 1
- Slot 3: 2 Shinies on Slots 1 & 2
- Slot 4: 3 Shinies on Slots 1, 2 & 3

**Free Agents (Rank 0 and 10):**

- Count as any faction (but need a Buddy)
- Keep their printed number for sums

1. Hand limit: discard to 8 (Playbooks count)
2. Playbook check: score up to 1 completed Playbook *(Advanced Module)*

- Event VP + Playbook VP + Shiny Bonus (1 VP per 3 Shinies, max 3 VP)
- Tiebreaker: Most events worth 9+ VP

**2-Player Adjustments:**

- 25 VP threshold, 1 Shiny Scramble
- Coach's Toll: Timeout adds 1 Shiny to Lineup Slot 1

**Playbook Limit:** 2 (all player counts)

## The Six Factions (Personality Guide)

**🔴 Super-Dupes** — The jocks. The protectors. They pull hard, dig in, and absolutely will not let go of the tug-of-war rope even if their paws are on fire. *"I've got your back! ...and also your front, and your sides."*

**🟠 Finders-Keepers** — The opportunists. They called dibs. On everything. The orange ones hide behind the tire swing during Easter Egg Hunts, and they *will* find your stash before you do. *"That's mine now. I touched it first."*

**🟡 Tinkerers** — The chaos engineers. Their soapbox derby car is a toaster on skateboard wheels with no brakes. It is glorious. They move fast, break things, and somehow make it work. *"Too slow!"*

**🟢 Prognosticationers** — The know-it-alls. They predicted you'd pick the blue shell. They also predicted you'd regret it. Their crystal balls are mostly decorative, but their smugness is 100% real. *"I knew that!"*

**🔵 Magicians** — The tricksters. The pebble isn't under any cup. The pebble is in their pocket. The cups are a metaphor. They'll distract the goalie with a card trick while their teammate grabs the flag. *"Sleight of paw."*

**🟣 Time Travelers** — The do-over specialists. They weren't moving — they were simply rewinding their position to three seconds ago. The judge is biased. They've already done this a thousand times, and they'll do it a thousand more until they win. *"I've already done this 1000x."*

## A Note on Sportsmanship

All six factions are friends. Really! Contests of Chaos are competitive — ruthlessly so — but at the end of the day, everyone's going home to the same Stadium. Win with grace. Lose with style. And if a Tinkerer's soapbox derby crashes into the scoring table, well... *mistakes were made*.

## Designer's Note

Contests of Chaos is intended as the main event of this collection — the game you pull out when Get Stuffed has warmed everyone up and Summit Scramble has sharpened their competitive edge. It's a drafting game at heart, but the sliding market means you're never just building a tableau. You're reading the table, predicting rival moves, and occasionally hate-drafting a card just to keep it out of someone else's hands.

Where Get Stuffed is chaos and Summit Scramble is a sprint, Contests of Chaos is calculated. You can see what everyone's building. You can see the events on the Jumbotron they're chasing. The question isn't *if* you can stop them — it's whether stopping them is worth more than finishing your own play first.

**On Playbooks:** The base game works beautifully as a pure tactical race where everyone can see everyone's plans. That transparency creates tension. Playbooks add a different kind of tension: *uncertainty*. Why is your rival hoarding Shinies? Why did they just pay 5 for The Agent? What are they building toward that you can't see?

Use Playbooks when your group wants more replayability, hidden information, and a reason to care about *how* you win. Skip Playbooks when teaching new players or when you want a cleaner, faster game. The base game is complete without them. Playbooks are dessert, not the main course.

The Event Deck provides the variation. Some games, the Tinkerers dominate. Some games, someone pulls off a 16-point Group Photo and everyone applauds while quietly seething. That's the Stadium. That's the Games.

Now shuffle up. The Coach is waiting.
