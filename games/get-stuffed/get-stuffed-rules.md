# 🐹 GET STUFFED

### A High-Octane Shedding Game for 2–6 Players
**System:** Hamsters & Monsters Core Deck (66 cards) · **Time:** 10–15 Minutes · **Ages:** 10+

---

## What's Going On Here?

The Coach is picking the final roster for the **Contests of Chaos**, and the bench is *way* too crowded. Every hamster and monster wants a spot on the field — but there's only so much room.

Your job? Get your entire squad off the bench and onto the field before anyone else. Play your cards, dodge disasters, and if you're lucky, make your rivals' lives just a *little* bit harder along the way.

The first player to play their last card **makes the cut**. Everyone else? Still doing warm-up laps.

---

## What You Need

Just the **Hamsters & Monsters deck** (66 cards — six factions, numbered 0 through 10 each).

**The Six Factions:**

| Color | Faction | Symbol |
|-------|---------|--------|
| 🔴 Red | Super-Dupes | Shield |
| 🟠 Orange | Finders-Keepers | Magnifying Glass |
| 🟡 Yellow | Tinkerers | Spanner |
| 🟢 Green | Prognosticationers | Crystal Ball |
| 🔵 Blue | Magicians | Top Hat |
| 🟣 Purple | Time Travelers | Backward Clock |

---

## Setup

1. **Shuffle** all 66 cards and place them face-down. This is **the Stash** (your draw pile).
2. **Flip** the top card of the Stash face-up next to it. This starts **the Pit** (your discard pile). If it's a 🟣 Time Traveler, tuck it back into the middle of the Stash and flip again — you need a real faction showing to kick things off.
3. **Deal** starting hands based on player count:

| Players | Cards Each |
|---------|-----------|
| 2–3 | 7 |
| 4–5 | 6 |
| 6 | 5 |

Pick someone to go first. (Youngest player, loudest player, whoever lost last — dealer's choice.) Play goes clockwise.

---

## The One Rule That Runs Everything

Every card has two things on it: a **Faction** (its color) and a **Rank** (its number). On your turn, you play one card from your hand onto the Pit. To be a legal play, your card must match the top card of the Pit by **either** Faction **or** Rank.

That's it. That's the core of the whole game.

> **Example:** The Pit shows a 🟡 Yellow 7. You can play *any* Yellow card (faction match) or *any* 7 (rank match).

### 🟣 The One Exception: Time Travelers Are Wild

Purple cards (Time Travelers) **always match the Pit**, no matter what's showing. You can play one at any time — they're your get-out-of-jail-free card.

**But here's the twist:** when you play a Time Traveler, you **declare any faction** (color) you want. That's what the next player has to match — either the faction you declared or the rank of the Time Traveler card you played.

> **Example:** The Pit shows a 🔴 Red 8. You play a 🟣 Purple 3 and declare "Green!" The next player must now play either a 🟢 Green card or any 3.

This also matters later when you can't play *anything* from your hand... but we're getting ahead of ourselves. Keep reading — you're going to love Scavenging. Mostly.

### Moving On

If you *can* play a card — you *must* play a card. Pick one, toss it on the Pit, and your turn is done (unless your card triggers a Faction Power — we'll get to those in a minute).

---

## Scavenging (When You Can't Play)

Sometimes you look at your hand and nothing matches. No matching faction. No matching rank. You're stuck.

When this happens, you don't just draw one card and move on. Oh no. You **Scavenge** — and it can get ugly fast.

### How Scavenging Works

**Step 1 — Flip.** Turn over the top card of the Stash and **show it to the whole table**. Everyone needs to see this card — its rank sets the stakes.

**Step 2 — Check.**
- **If it matches the Pit** (by faction or rank): Lucky you! Play it immediately. If it's ranked 6–10, its Faction Power triggers just like a normal play. Your turn ends. Breathe easy.
- **If it does NOT match the Pit**: Bad news. Add it to your hand and move to Step 3. Its **rank** is now your penalty number.

**Step 3 — The Penalty Draw.** You must now draw cards from the Stash **one at a time**, up to a number equal to the **rank of the card you flipped** in Step 1. These draws are **secret** — only you see them as you pull them.

But there's a lifeline:

> **The Mercy Clause:** If at any point during your penalty draw you pull a card that **matches the Pit** (by faction or rank), you may **stop immediately** and play that card to the Pit. If it's ranked 6–10, its Faction Power triggers! Your turn ends. You still picked up extra cards, but the bleeding stopped. (Remember — a 🟣 Time Traveler always matches! Declare a faction and get out.)

If no drawn card matches the Pit (or you choose not to play one that does — yes, that's allowed, you sneaky thing), you keep drawing until you've drawn the full penalty amount. All drawn cards go into your hand, hidden from other players.

**What if the Stash runs out mid-penalty?** Reshuffle immediately (just like normal — Pit's top card stays, everything else becomes the new Stash) and keep drawing. If this is the *second* reshuffle, Sugar Crash kicks in right away — which means on your very next turn you'll get to dump two cards. Getting Stuffed might just have set up your comeback.

> **Example:** The Pit shows a 🟢 Green 4. You have nothing that matches, so you Scavenge. You flip the top card of the Stash and show the table: it's a 🔴 Red 9. It doesn't match — so you take it into your hand, and now you owe a **9-card penalty draw.**
>
> You start drawing one at a time, keeping each card secret. The first two cards don't match the Pit. The third card is a 🟢 Green 6 — that matches! You play it to the Pit, stop drawing, and your turn ends. You "only" picked up 3 extra cards instead of 9. Small victories.
>
> If nothing had matched? You'd draw all 9, add them to your hand, and pass the turn with a hand that just exploded. The table is laughing. This is fine.

## Faction Powers (The Fun Part)

Now that you know how to play cards and how Scavenging works, here's where things get spicy.

**When you play a card ranked 6 or higher** (that's 6, 7, 8, 9, or 10), its **Faction Power** triggers. This applies to *any* card you play to the Pit — whether it's a normal play from your hand, the initial Scavenge flip, or a Mercy Clause save. If it lands on the Pit and it's 6+, the power fires. Cards ranked 0 through 5? They're regular plays — no special effects. They do their job and sit down. Respect the role players.

One important safety rule before we dive in:

> **⚠️ The Chain Link Rule:** Cards that get moved, given, swapped, or discarded as a **side effect of a power** never trigger their own powers. For example, if Hot Potato hands someone a Red 8, that card doesn't fire Hot Potato again. Only cards that are **played to the Pit** can trigger powers. This keeps things from spiraling into chaos. (Well, *more* chaos.)

### The Six Powers

---

**🔴 Super-Dupes — "HOT POTATO!"**

*"Here, hold this for me!"*

Give **1 card** from your hand to any opponent. They must add it to their hand. No backsies. Pick the player who's closest to winning and ruin their day (lovingly).

**One catch:** If you had only 1 card left in your hand when you played your Super-Dupe, Hot Potato **does not trigger** — you have nothing left to give! (Nice try, though. You can only win by playing your last card *to the Pit*, not by handing it off.)

---

**🟠 Finders-Keepers — "DIB IT!"**

*"Dibs on not doing that."*

Choose **any other player**. That player's next turn is completely replaced by a forced Scavenge — they don't get to play a card from their hand, they just flip, check, and deal with whatever happens. Their turn ends after the Scavenge resolves (whether they matched or took a penalty).

After the Scavenge resolves, play continues from **the player who Scavenged** (not the player who played Dib It) in the current play direction.

---

**🟡 Tinkerers — "RE-TINKER!"**

*"Wait, I wasn't done yet!"*

Choose **one** of the following:
- **Reverse** the direction of play (clockwise becomes counter-clockwise, or vice versa), OR
- **Take another turn** immediately.

You can only Re-Tinker once per turn — no chaining extra turns off extra turns. We tried it. It wasn't pretty.

---

**🟢 Prognosticationers — "I FORESAW THIS!"**

*"Called it."*

Look at the **top 3 cards** of the Stash (or as many as remain — don't show anyone). Take **1** into your hand. Put the rest back on top in any order you like. If the Stash is empty when you trigger this power, reshuffle first (Pit's top card stays, everything else becomes the new Stash) — and if that's the second reshuffle, Sugar Crash kicks in.

Then, **play 1 card** from your hand to the Pit that matches the **current top of the Pit** — which is now the Prognosticationers card you just played. So you need something matching its faction or rank. This is a full play — if the card is ranked 6–10, its power triggers! If you can't match anything, you keep the card you took and your turn just ends — no penalty, but no net benefit either.

> **Why it's good:** You see the future *and* shed an extra card. That's card advantage on both ends.

---

**🔵 Magicians — "IS THIS YOURS?"**

*"Nothing up my sleeve..."*

This one works differently depending on the rank:

**Ranks 6–9 — Sleight of Paw:** Fan out your hand face-down. An opponent of your choice blindly picks **1 card** from your hand. Then *you* blindly pick **1 card** from theirs. No peeking. No complaining. You each keep what you grabbed.

**Rank 10 — VANISH!** You *may* swap your **entire hand** with any player. Yes, all of it. Yes, it's as dramatic as it sounds. But you don't *have* to — if you like your hand, just keep it. Nobody's forcing you to pull the trigger on this one.

---

**🟣 Time Travelers — "TIME WARP"**

*"I've already done this a thousand times."*

As you already learned — Time Traveler cards are **Wild**. They always match the Pit, and when you play one, you declare a faction for the next player to match. That's the power. No extra hoops, just pure flexibility exactly when you need it.

---

## The Sugar Crash (Endgame Turbo Mode)

Most games end well before the Stash runs out. But sometimes everyone's hoarding cards and nobody can finish. When that happens, the game kicks into overdrive.

**First Reshuffle:** When the Stash runs out, take all the cards in the Pit except the top card, shuffle them, and flip them face-down as a new Stash. Keep playing normally.

**Second Reshuffle = Sugar Crash!** If you have to reshuffle a *second* time, the Coach has lost patience. New rule kicks in:

> **Sugar Crash Mode:** On your turn, you **must play two cards**. The first card must match the Pit normally and **triggers its power** if ranked 6–10. The second card can be **anything** — no matching required, no powers triggered. It's a free dump. The Coach has stopped caring about the rules.
>
> **Scavenging during Sugar Crash:** If you can't match and have to Scavenge, resolve the Scavenge normally. Once you've played a matching card (either on the initial flip or via the Mercy Clause), dump your free second card. Even if you draw the full penalty without finding a match, you *still* get to play one card for free afterward. Sugar does things to people.
>
> If you only have **1 card left**, just play it normally — and win!

This accelerates the game to a quick finish. Someone *will* go out soon. Probably screaming.

---

## Winning

The game ends **the instant** a player plays their last card.

No power triggers. No swaps. No "wait, actually—"

If your last card *would* normally trigger a power (like a swap or a forced Scavenge), it doesn't. You played it. You're done. You made the cut.

Everyone else is still on the bench.

Shuffle up. Go again. It's only 10 minutes — and you *know* you want revenge.

## Quick Reference

**On Your Turn:**
- Play 1 card matching the Pit by **Faction** or **Rank**
- If you can't play: **Scavenge** (flip → check → penalty if no match)
- If your card is ranked **6–10**: trigger its **Faction Power**

**Faction Powers (Rank 6–10 Only):**

| Faction | Power | Effect |
|---------|-------|--------|
| 🔴 Super-Dupes | Hot Potato! | Give 1 card from your hand to any opponent (no trigger if hand is empty) |
| 🟠 Finders-Keepers | Dib It! | Choose any player — they must Scavenge; play continues from *them* |
| 🟡 Tinkerers | Re-Tinker! | Reverse play direction OR take another turn (pick one) |
| 🟢 Prognosticationers | I Foresaw This! | Peek at top 3 of Stash, take 1; then play 1 matching card to Pit (powers fire) |
| 🔵 Magicians (6–9) | Sleight of Paw | Blind swap 1 card with an opponent |
| 🔵 Magicians (10) | VANISH! | You *may* swap your entire hand with any player |
| 🟣 Time Travelers | Time Warp | Wild — always matches; declare a faction for next player |

**Key Rules:**
- Cards moved by powers **never** trigger their own powers — only cards played to the Pit trigger powers
- **Scavenge penalty** = draw cards equal to the rank of the failed card
- **Mercy Clause**: If a penalty draw matches the Pit, play it and stop
- **Sugar Crash** (2nd reshuffle): Must play 2 cards; first is normal (powers fire), second is free (no powers)
