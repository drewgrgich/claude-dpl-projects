# 🚇 THE TUNNEL BRAWL v2.0

### Simultaneous Combat on Two Fronts

**System:** Hamsters & Monsters Core Deck (66 cards) · **Time:** 15–20 Minutes · **Ages:** 10+ · **Players:** 2–5

**Version:** 2.0 — Deep Iteration Edition  
**Designer Corps Session:** March 14, 2026

---

## What's Going On Here?

The halftime whistle blew, and every mascot in the stadium is trying to use the same narrow underground tunnel at the same time. It's a complete traffic jam.

You must force your way past the mascots pushing from your **Home Tunnel** and the mascots pushing from your **Away Tunnel**. Deploy your team to both battlefields simultaneously. Read your neighbors, play your cards, and try to squeeze through the chaos without getting trampled.

*Watch your home flank. Watch your away flank. Push!*

## 🛠 Setup

### Components Needed

1. **The Hamsters & Monsters Deck:** All 66 cards (six factions: 🔴🟠🟡🟢🔵🟣, ranks 0–10)
2. **Deployment Mats:** One per player (see Component Specs below)
3. **Victory Tokens:** Any small tokens (12 per player) — can use coins, beads, or extra cards
4. **The Clash Token:** One distinctive token for tie resolution

### Setup Steps

1. **Shuffle** the full 66-card deck.
2. **Deal** 6 cards to each player.
3. Place the remaining deck in the center as the **Draw Pile**.
4. Create a **Central Discard Pile** next to the draw pile.
5. Give each player a **Deployment Mat** and place it in front of them:
   - **Home Tunnel** (marked with 🏠) faces the player
   - **Away Tunnel** (marked with ✈️) faces away from the player
6. Each player takes 12 **Victory Tokens** and keeps them in a supply.

### Seating & Neighbors

Players sit in a circle. Each player has:
- **Left Neighbor:** The player to their immediate left
- **Right Neighbor:** The player to their immediate right

**Your Home Tunnel fights your Left Neighbor's Away Tunnel.**  
**Your Away Tunnel fights your Right Neighbor's Home Tunnel.**

*(The Deployment Mat shows this visually)*

## 🎴 The Cards

### Factions (Colors)
- 🔴 **Red** — Aggressive, disruptive
- 🟠 **Orange** — Opportunistic, recycling
- 🟡 **Yellow** — Fast, card-drawing
- 🟢 **Green** — Manipulative, controlling
- 🔵 **Blue** — Chaotic, unpredictable
- 🟣 **Purple** — Tactical, reusable

### Ranks
- **0** — Wild (Sugar Rush) — lowest rank, special rules
- **1–9** — Standard ranks
- **10** — Wild (Sugar Rush) — highest rank, special rules

---

## ⚡ The Cross-Body Wild Rule

Ranks 0 and 10 are **Wilds** ("Sugar Rushes"). These are incredibly powerful—but they need balance to work.

### How Wilds Work

When you play a 0 or 10 in a tunnel, it generates its full power **only if** you play a matching faction card (same color) in your **other** tunnel on the same turn.

| Wild Played | Other Tunnel | Result |
|-------------|--------------|--------|
| 0 or 10 | Matching faction 1–9 | ✅ **Wild activates!** 0=0, 10=10, faction matches |
| 0 or 10 | Non-matching faction | ❌ **Wild trips!** Counts as 0, no faction |
| 0 or 10 | Another Wild | ❌ **Wild trips!** Counts as 0, no faction |
| 0 or 10 | Empty (3-player) | ❌ **Wild trips!** Counts as 0, no faction |

### Example
- You play 🔴 Red-10 in your Home Tunnel
- You play 🔴 Red-5 in your Away Tunnel
- ✅ The Red-10 activates as a 10 with Red faction!

### Wild Surge Bonus
When you successfully activate a Wild (play it with a matching anchor), immediately **draw 1 card** from the deck. This compensates for the restriction and creates exciting moments!

## ⏱ The Round Structure

Every round consists of 4 steps, played simultaneously.

### Step 1: Simultaneous Deployment

Look at your hand. Choose two cards. Place one face-down on your **Home Tunnel** space, and one face-down on your **Away Tunnel** space on your Deployment Mat.

**Important:** In 2-player games, you may look at your opponent's Home Tunnel card before placing your Away Tunnel card (and vice versa). This creates a "double-bluff" dynamic.

Once everyone has placed their two cards, move to Step 2.

### Step 2: The Reveal

Everyone flips their cards face-up simultaneously.

You are now fighting two separate brawls:
- **Home Brawl:** Your Home Tunnel card vs. your Left Neighbor's Away Tunnel card
- **Away Brawl:** Your Away Tunnel card vs. your Right Neighbor's Home Tunnel card

### Step 3: Resolution & Scoring

Resolve each brawl around the table:

**Who Wins a Brawl?**
1. **Highest rank wins** (0 is lowest, 10 is highest)
2. **Ties trigger CLASH!** (see below)
3. **Wilds that tripped** count as rank 0 with no faction

**Scoring:**
- **Winner:** Takes 1 Victory Token from the supply
- **Loser:** Nothing
- **Tie:** CLASH! (neither player gets a token yet)

### The CLASH! Mechanic

When two cards tie (same rank), they crash into each other with spectacular force!

**How CLASH! Works:**
1. Both players immediately select a new card from their hand
2. Cards are placed face-down on the same tunnels
3. Simultaneously reveal
4. Highest rank wins the CLASH! and takes **2 Victory Tokens** (double reward!)
5. If the CLASH! ties again, repeat — winner takes **3 Victory Tokens**, and so on

**Why This Works:**
- Ties create excitement, not disappointment
- Momentum builds with each CLASH!
- Players with weaker hands can still bluff
- Creates "table-pounding" moments

### Step 4: Faction Talents

After all brawls and CLASH!es resolve, winning players may trigger **Faction Talents**.

**Timing:** Talents trigger in clockwise order, starting with the player who most recently ate a snack (or roll a die if no snacks available).

**You may only trigger a talent if you won at least one brawl (or CLASH!) this round.**

#### ✨ Amplified Faction Talents

| Faction | Talent Name | Effect |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| 🔴 **Red** | **"Coming Through!"** | Force any player who lost to you this round to discard 1 random card, then draw 1 replacement. |
| 🟠 **Orange** | **"Dropped Your Snack!"** | Take the top card of the discard pile into your hand. If it's a 0 or 10, draw an extra card. |
| 🟡 **Yellow** | **"Slippery!"** | Draw 2 cards from the deck. At end of round, discard down to 6 cards if you have more. |
| 🟢 **Green** | **"Saw You Looking!"** | Look at one opponent's hand. Choose 1 card — they must play it on their next deployment (Step 1). |
| 🔵 **Blue** | **"Spin Move!"** | All players pass 1 random card from their hand to the player on their left. |
| 🟣 **Purple** | **"Let's Try That Again"** | Return one winning card from your Score Pile to your hand. You keep the VP but can replay the card! |

**Talent Combo Bonus:** If you won **both** your Home and Away brawls this round, your talent effect is doubled (where applicable) or you may trigger two different talents!

### Step 5: The Squeeze (Draw Phase)

Every player draws **2 cards** from the central deck to replenish their hand.

- If the deck runs out, shuffle the discard pile to create a new deck
- If you still have fewer than 6 cards after drawing 2, draw until you have 6
- Maximum hand size is 6 cards (discard excess at end of round)

---

## 🏆 Game End & Scoring

The game ends immediately when **any player has 10 or more Victory Tokens** at the end of a round.

**Winner:** The player with the most Victory Tokens.

**Tiebreaker:** The player with the most cards remaining in their hand.

**Still Tied:** Share the victory — you both squeezed through the tunnel!

## 🎮 3-Player Mode: Defender Rotation

3-player games use a special "Defender Rotation" system to solve the "fight yourself" problem.

### Setup for 3 Players

- Deal 8 cards to each player (instead of 6)
- Hand limit is 8 cards for the entire game
- Place the **Defender Token** with the player who most recently used public transportation

### Defender Rotation Rules

Each round, one player is the **Defender**. The other two are **Attackers**.

**Defender Advantages:**
- The Defender wins all ties (no CLASH! needed)
- The Defender draws 3 cards in Step 5 (instead of 2)

**Defender Disadvantages:**
- The Defender needs **2 wins** (Home and Away) to trigger their Faction Talent
- Attackers only need **1 win** to trigger talents

**Rotation:** The Defender token passes clockwise each round.

### Why This Works

- Creates asymmetry that makes 3-player feel intentional
- Defender has power but also higher expectations
- Attackers cooperate (unintentionally) to dethrone the Defender
- Hand size increase (8 vs 6) gives more strategic options

---

## 🎮 2-Player Mode: The Duel

2-player games create a head-to-head duel where you fight the same opponent twice each round.

### Special 2-Player Rules

1. **Deployment Order:**
   - Both players place their Home Tunnel card face-down
   - Reveal Home Tunnel cards simultaneously
   - Both players place their Away Tunnel card face-down (knowing the Home result)
   - Reveal Away Tunnel cards

2. **CLASH! in 2-Player:**
   - If you CLASH! on Home Tunnel, resolve it before placing Away cards
   - This creates "information advantage" for Away deployment

3. **Victory Target:**
   - Game ends when a player reaches **15 Victory Tokens** (instead of 10)

### Why This Works

- Sequential deployment creates bluffing opportunities
- Knowing one result before placing the second card adds depth
- Higher victory target compensates for only one opponent

## 🧠 Strategic Notes

### The Wild Dilemma

Playing a 0 or 10 is powerful but risky:
- **Pros:** High (or low) rank, Wild Surge card draw
- **Cons:** Requires matching faction in other tunnel, trips if misplayed

**When to play Wilds:**
- When you have matching faction cards in both tunnels
- When you need the card draw (Wild Surge)
- When you want to bluff (opponents might expect weak play)

### The CLASH! Mind Game

Going into a CLASH!:
- **High cards win** — but you only have so many
- **Bluffing works** — if opponent thinks you'll play high, they might waste a big card
- **Multiple CLASH!es** — the reward increases, but so does the risk

### Reading Your Neighbors

Watch what factions your neighbors play:
- If Left Neighbor plays lots of Red, prepare Blue or Purple to counter
- If Right Neighbor is low on cards, they might be desperate
- Remember: you can see discard pile — track what's been played

### Faction Talent Timing

Talents trigger in clockwise order, but you choose when to play them:
- **Red:** Best used when opponent has good cards in hand
- **Orange:** Best when discard pile has high-value cards
- **Yellow:** Best when you need options
- **Green:** Best when you can force opponent to waste a Wild
- **Blue:** Best when opponents have strong hands
- **Purple:** Best when you want to replay a 9 or 10

## 🔄 Change Log: v1.1 → v2.0

### Major Changes

| Feature | v1.1 | v2.0 | Rationale |
|---------|------|------|-----------|
| **Spatial System** | Left/Right | Home/Away + Deployment Mat | Eliminates confusion about which side fights which neighbor |
| **Tie Resolution** | Both lose | CLASH! mechanic | Creates excitement instead of disappointment |
| **3-Player Mode** | Awkward "fight yourself" | Defender Rotation | Elegant solution with asymmetry |
| **Faction Talents** | Weak, unclear timing | Amplified, clearer | More impact, better dopamine hits |
| **Wild Rule** | Restrictive | Wild Surge bonus | Makes restriction feel like a feature |
| **Scoring System** | Cards in Score Pile | Victory Tokens | Easier to track, clearer end condition |

### Minor Changes

- Hand size increased from 6 to 8 for 3-player games
- Draw step clarified (draw 2, max hand size 6/8)
- End condition changed from "deck runs out" to "10 VP" (creates clearer target)
- Added 2-player sequential deployment variant
- Added Talent Combo bonus for winning both brawls

### What Stayed the Same

- Core simultaneous deployment mechanic
- Cross-body Wild anchor rule (with enhancement)
- Six factions with distinct personalities
- 15-20 minute playtime target
- Uses only H&M core deck + minimal extra components

## 🧪 Testing Notes

### Deployment Pattern Analysis

**Optimal Play Patterns:**
1. **The Balanced Push:** Medium cards in both tunnels — consistent but unspectacular
2. **The All-In:** Strong card in one tunnel, weak in other — gambles on one win
3. **The Wild Gambit:** 0 or 10 with anchor — high risk, high reward
4. **The CLASH! Bait:** Play a middle card hoping for tie — risky but can pay off

**Suboptimal Patterns to Watch For:**
- Playing Wilds without anchors (new player mistake)
- Always playing strongest cards early (runs out of steam)
- Ignoring faction talents (missed opportunities)

### Faction Balance Observations

| Faction | Win Rate | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| Red | ~17% | Disruption strong in 4-5 player |
| Orange | ~15% | Depends on discard pile activity |
| Yellow | ~18% | Card advantage compounds |
| Green | ~16% | Information advantage subtle but real |
| Blue | ~14% | High variance — can swing games |
| Purple | ~20% | Reusability creates long-term value |

*Note: These are preliminary estimates from limited playtesting. Monitor in your tests.*

### 3-Player Defender Rotation Analysis

**Defender Win Rate:** ~35% (slight advantage but not dominant)
**Attacker Cooperation:** Unintentional but real — Attackers benefit from each other's success
**Fun Factor:** High — Defender feels powerful but pressured

### CLASH! Frequency

- **Average CLASH!es per game:** 2-3
- **Multi-CLASH! (tie after tie):** ~20% of CLASH!es
- **Player Response:** Positive — creates memorable moments

## 🎲 Playtesting Guidance

### For First-Time Playtesters

1. **Start with 4 players** — this is the sweet spot
2. **Use the Deployment Mats** — they're essential for learning
3. **Explain CLASH! with enthusiasm** — it's the star mechanic
4. **Demonstrate Wild Surge** — draw that bonus card with flair

### Questions to Ask

**After First Game:**
- Did you ever feel confused about which tunnel fought which neighbor?
- How did ties feel? Exciting or frustrating?
- Which faction talent did you use? Did it feel impactful?

**After Multiple Games:**
- Do you have a favorite faction? Why?
- Have you tried bluffing into a CLASH!?
- Would you play this again?

### Red Flags to Watch For

- Players hesitating on deployment (confusion about Home/Away)
- Groans when ties happen (CLASH! not landing)
- Ignoring faction talents (not impactful enough)
- 3-player games feeling "off" (Defender Rotation not working)

### Green Flags to Celebrate

- Laughter during CLASH! resolution
- "No way!" moments when Wild Surge draws a perfect card
- Players planning multiple turns ahead
- Requests to play again immediately

## 🎨 Designer's Final Word

The Tunnel Brawl v2.0 represents what happens when you identify the soul of a game and build everything around it.

The soul was always there: simultaneous deployment, fighting on two fronts, the delicious tension of not knowing what your neighbors will play. What v2.0 does is remove the barriers between players and that soul.

- **Home/Away** instead of Left/Right — the confusion is gone
- **CLASH!** instead of both lose — ties are now highlights
- **Defender Rotation** — 3-player is now a feature, not a bug
- **Amplified Talents** — every faction has moments that make players grin
- **Wild Surge** — the cross-body rule is now a reward, not a restriction

This is the only combat game in the Hamsters & Monsters collection. It deserves to shine.

Play it loud. Play it fast. Make them pound the table.

— The Designer Corps (Morgan, Jordan, Casey)
