# [Working Title] — Rules v0.1.5

*2–5 players | Ages 10+ | 15–20 minutes*

---

## Overview

Compete to control four colored zones over four rounds. Each round, pass cards to reshape your hand, reveal a Condition Card that changes the rules, then simultaneously place cards face-down at zones on your player board. Reveal at once — the strongest presence at each zone wins Victory Points. The player with the most VP after four rounds wins.

| Players | Cards Dealt | Pass/Round | Min Play/Round |
|---------|-------------|------------|----------------|
| 2       | 24          | 4 (setup only) | 3 |
| 3       | 16          | 2          | 3 |
| 4       | 12          | 2          | 3 |
| 5       | 9           | 1          | 2 |

---

## Components

**56-Card Main Deck**

- **44 Number Cards** — 4 colors (Red, Yellow, Green, Blue) × 11 cards each. Ranks per color: 0, 0, 1, 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 9, 10, 10
- **4 Mascots** — one per color. Doubles the rank of your best card at that zone.
- **4 Action Cards** — one per color, each with a unique effect. Default set: Shield (Red), Heist (Yellow), Contaminate (Green), Bounty (Blue).
- **4 Duds** — one per color. Share the same card back as Action Cards (opponents can't tell them apart). Play as rank 5 of their color.

**Additional Components**

- **12 Condition Cards** — modify the rules for one round each
- **4 Zone Cards** — placed in the center of the table (Red, Yellow, Green, Blue)
- **Player Boards** — one per player, with 4 zone slots for face-down deployment
- **Score Pad** or VP tokens

---

## Setup

1. **Place the 4 Zone Cards** face-up in a row in the center of the table: Red, Yellow, Green, Blue.
2. **Shuffle the 56-card main deck** and deal each player their starting hand (see table above). Place remaining cards face-down as a draw pile — these are not used.
3. **Shuffle the 12 Condition Cards** face-down in a pile near the zones.
4. Each player takes a **Player Board** and places it in front of them.
5. Choose a starting player (youngest, random, etc.). Play proceeds clockwise.

---

## Round Structure

The game is played over **4 rounds**. Each round has four phases:

### Phase 1 — Pass

Each player selects cards from their hand and passes them **face-down to the player on their left**. Pick up the cards passed to you. This happens **every round**, not just at setup.

| Players | Cards Passed Per Round |
|---------|----------------------|
| 2       | 4 (setup only — no per-round passing at 2P) |
| 3–4     | 2 |
| 5       | 1 |

*The pass happens before the condition is revealed. You're passing blind — you can't game the pass based on the round's condition.*

### Phase 2 — Condition

Flip the top **Condition Card** and read it aloud. This condition applies to all players for the entire round. (For your first game, skip the Condition Card in Round 1 so new players learn the base rules first.)

### Phase 3 — Deploy

All players simultaneously choose cards from their hand and place them **face-down** in the zone slots on their Player Board. You may place any number of cards at any zone, subject to any restrictions from the active Condition Card.

**Minimum play:** You must play at least **3 cards per round** (2 at 5 players), or all remaining cards if you have fewer. You are not required to play all your cards — any cards you keep will be available in future rounds.

Once all players are ready, say **"Reveal!"**

### Phase 4 — Reveal, Resolve & Score

All players flip their cards face-up at the same time. Resolve action cards, calculate strength, and score each zone.

---

## Strength & Scoring

> **Strength = Best Card Rank + 2 per Extra Card + 3 Home Field Bonus**

**Best Card Rank:** The highest rank among your number cards (and duds) at that zone.

**Extra Card Bonus:** +2 for each additional card beyond the first. The Mascot does not count as an extra card.

**Home Field Bonus:** +3 if at least one **natural card** (number card or dud) in your stack matches the zone's color. Mascots and Action Cards cannot anchor Home Field. A Dud of the matching color does anchor Home Field.

**Mascot:** Doubles the rank of your best card. Does not add +2 as an extra card and cannot anchor Home Field.

### Scoring

- **Zone Winner:** Highest strength wins **5 VP**. Ties split VP rounded down.
- **2nd Place:** At each zone with a clear winner (no tie for 1st), the runner-up scores **1 VP**. Multiple 2nd-place ties each get 1 VP. No 2nd-place award on tied zones.

### Strength Examples

**Simple play:** You play a 7 and a 3 at the Blue zone.
> Best: 7. Extra cards: 1 (+2). Home Field: neither is Blue (+0). **Strength = 9**

**Home Field:** You play a Red 10 and a Red 3 at the Red zone.
> Best: 10. Extra: 1 (+2). HF: Red matches Red (+3). **Strength = 15**

**Mascot combo:** You play a Green Mascot and a Green 9 at the Green zone.
> Best: 9, doubled = 18. Extra: 0 (Mascot doesn't count). HF: Green 9 matches (+3). **Strength = 21**

**Dud at matching zone:** You play a Yellow Dud at the Yellow zone.
> Best: 5 (dud = rank 5). HF: Yellow Dud matches Yellow (+3). **Strength = 8**

## Action Cards

Action Cards do not have a rank. They do not contribute to Strength and do not count as an extra card for the +2 bonus. They activate during the Resolve phase in a fixed order.

### Default Set of 4 (resolve in this order)

| Order | Card | Color | Effect |
|-------|------|-------|--------|
| 1st | Shield | Red | Protects your stack from Heist. If you lose this zone, score 2 VP consolation. |
| 2nd | Heist | Yellow | Steal the highest-ranked card from the strongest unshielded opponent at this zone. Add it to your stack before scoring. |
| 3rd | Contaminate | Green | This zone scores inverted: lowest Strength wins instead of highest. |
| 4th | Bounty | Blue | Double VP if you win this zone. 0 VP if you lose (overrides Shield consolation). |

### Variant Cards (Spice Option — see Variants section)

| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| Bomb | Destroys the highest-ranked card at this zone (from any unshielded player). Resolves after Heist. |
| Swap | Exchange your top card here with your top card at any other zone you occupy. Resolves after Bomb. |
| Ambush | +5 Strength if you are the only player at this zone. Resolves last. |

**Duds** share the same card back as Action Cards — opponents can't tell them apart until revealed. Duds play as a rank 5 card of their color. They contribute to Strength normally and can anchor Home Field.

---

## Condition Cards

One Condition Card is flipped at the start of each round (after passing). It applies to all players equally for that round. The deck contains 12 cards; you will see 4 of the 12 each game.

### Home Field Disruptors

These conditions change or remove the Home Field Bonus. Roughly 1 in 4 rounds will feature one of these.

| Condition | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Inversion | Lowest Strength wins each zone this round. (Home Field's +3 now hurts you — lower is better.) |
| Neutral Ground | The Home Field Bonus (+3) does not apply this round. |
| Color Tax | Home Field is a **-2 penalty** instead of +3 this round. If any natural card in your stack matches the zone color, you get -2. |

### Rank Disruptors

| Condition | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Ceiling | All card ranks are capped at 5 this round. (A 10 counts as 5, a 9 counts as 5, etc. Cards ranked 5 or below are unaffected.) |
| Mirror | Every card's rank becomes 10 minus its printed rank. (A 10 becomes 0, a 0 becomes 10, a 7 becomes 3, etc.) |

### Deployment Constraints

| Condition | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Spread Out | You must play cards at 2 or more different zones this round. |
| Lone Wolf | Maximum 1 card per zone this round. (You can still play at multiple zones — just 1 card each.) |

### Scoring Twists

| Condition | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Double Stakes | Each zone is worth **10 VP** instead of 5 this round. |
| Sudden Death | If two or more players tie at a zone, **nobody scores** that zone. |
| Diminishing Returns | VP per zone won this round: 1st zone = 7, 2nd = 5, 3rd = 3, 4th = 1. (Rewards focusing on fewer zones.) |

### Momentum & Interaction

| Condition | Effect |
|-----------|--------|
| Grudge Match | The player(s) who won the fewest zones last round get **+3 Strength at every zone** this round. (Does not apply in Round 1.) |
| Second Wave | After all players reveal, each player may deploy **1 additional card** from their hand to any zone where they already have cards. (This is done simultaneously, face-up.) |

---

## Game End

After **4 rounds**, the player with the most VP wins.

**Tiebreaker:** Most total zones won. If still tied, the players share the victory.

---

## Variants

### Spice Option (Action Card Pool)

Instead of using the default 4 action cards, randomly select 4 from the full pool of 7 (Shield, Heist, Contaminate, Bounty, Bomb, Swap, Ambush) at the start of each game. Replace the corresponding action cards in the deck. This adds game-to-game variety.

**Full resolution order:** Shield (1st), Heist (2nd), Bomb (3rd), Swap (4th), Contaminate (5th), Bounty (6th), Ambush (7th).

### First Game Mode

For new players: skip the Condition Card in Round 1, and use only the default 4 action cards. This lets everyone learn the core strength formula and deployment before adding variety.

## Strategy Tips

- **Home Field is king — until it isn't.** The +3 bonus makes matching colors almost always correct. A Red 7 at Red zone (Str 10) beats a wild 9 (Str 9). But watch for Inversion, Color Tax, and Neutral Ground — about 1 in 4 rounds will punish autopiloting on color.
- **The draft is everything.** Use the pass phase to build a hand of colors you want to fight with, and pass away colors you don't need. Since you pass every round, your hand reshapes constantly.
- **Pace yourself.** You have 4 rounds but a fixed hand. Dumping everything early leaves you empty later. The minimum play rule (3 cards/round) prevents pure hoarding, but smart pacing still matters.
- **0s have their uses.** Under Inversion or Mirror, low cards become powerhouses. As zone padding, a 0 adds +2 to your best card. Perfect to pass away in the draft.
- **Fight for 2nd.** Even losing a zone earns 1 VP for the runner-up. A single card tossed at a zone you won't win can pay off over four rounds.
- **Read the Condition.** Lone Wolf rewards going where others won't. Sudden Death punishes going where everyone does. Double Stakes makes one round worth twice as much. Adapt every round.
- **Watch the card backs.** Action cards and Duds share the same back. When an opponent plays one, it might be a Heist — or it might be a harmless rank 5. The uncertainty is part of the game.

## Quick Reference

> **Strength = Best Rank (+doubled by Mascot) + 2 per extra card + 3 Home Field**

- **Round order:** Pass, Condition, Deploy, Reveal/Score
- **Action resolution:** Shield, Heist, Contaminate, Bounty
- **VP per zone:** 5 (winner) / 1 (2nd place)
- **Ties:** Split VP rounded down. No 2nd-place award on tied zones.
- **Home Field:** Need a natural card matching the zone color
- **Mascot:** Doubles best rank. Not an extra card. Can't anchor HF.
- **Hand sizes:** 2P=24 | 3P=16 | 4P=12 | 5P=9
- **Pass/round:** 2P=setup only | 3-4P=2 | 5P=1
- **Min play/round:** 3 cards (2 at 5P)
- **Ranks per color:** 0, 0, 1, 1, 3, 5, 7, 9, 9, 10, 10

## Print & Play Guide

Here's everything you need to build a playable prototype. Standard poker-size cards (2.5" × 3.5") work best. You can use blank cards, index cards cut to size, or print onto cardstock.

### 1. Number Cards (44 cards)

Make 11 cards in each of the 4 colors (Red, Yellow, Green, Blue). Write the rank prominently on the face along with a color indicator.

| Rank | Per Color | Total | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|-------|
| 0    | 2         | 8     | Common low card |
| 1    | 2         | 8     | Common low card |
| 3    | 1         | 4     | Scarce mid |
| 5    | 1         | 4     | Scarce mid |
| 7    | 1         | 4     | Scarce mid |
| 9    | 2         | 8     | Common high card |
| 10   | 2         | 8     | Common high card |

*Tip: Use colored markers, stickers, or print on colored paper. The color needs to be instantly readable at a glance.*

### 2. Mascots (4 cards)

One per color. Mark with a star or "M" symbol. These should have the same card back as number cards (not the action card back). Write the color clearly. No rank on the face — just the mascot indicator and color.

### 3. Action Cards (4 cards, or 7 for Spice Option)

These need a **distinct card back** — different from number cards. This is important because Duds share this same back, creating bluffing opportunities. Use a different color back, a bold border, or a stamp.

| Card | Color | Face Text |
|------|-------|-----------|
| Shield | Red | SHIELD — Protected. 2 VP consolation if you lose. |
| Heist | Yellow | HEIST — Steal opponent's best card here. |
| Contaminate | Green | CONTAMINATE — Lowest strength wins this zone. |
| Bounty | Blue | BOUNTY — Double VP if you win. 0 VP if you lose. |
| Bomb* | Any | BOMB — Destroy the highest card here. |
| Swap* | Any | SWAP — Exchange your top card with another zone. |
| Ambush* | Any | AMBUSH — +5 Strength if alone at this zone. |

*\*Variant cards — only needed if using the Spice Option.*

### 4. Duds (4 cards)

One per color. **Must share the same card back as Action Cards** — this is the core bluff. On the face, write "DUD" and the color. They play as rank 5 of their color.

### 5. Condition Cards (12 cards)

These should have their own distinct back (different from both main deck backs). Write the condition name and effect on the face. Use index cards or a different card color.

| # | Name | Short Text for Card Face |
|---|------|--------------------------|
| 1 | Inversion | Lowest Strength wins each zone. |
| 2 | Neutral Ground | No Home Field Bonus this round. |
| 3 | Color Tax | Home Field is -2 penalty (not +3). |
| 4 | Ceiling | All card ranks capped at 5. |
| 5 | Mirror | Rank = 10 minus printed rank. |
| 6 | Spread Out | Must play at 2+ zones. |
| 7 | Lone Wolf | Max 1 card per zone. |
| 8 | Double Stakes | Zones worth 10 VP. |
| 9 | Sudden Death | Ties score 0. |
| 10 | Dim. Returns | VP per zone: 7 / 5 / 3 / 1. |
| 11 | Grudge Match | +3 Strength to last round's loser(s). (Not Round 1.) |
| 12 | Second Wave | After reveal, deploy 1 more card (face-up). |

### 6. Zone Cards (4 cards)

Four large, clearly colored cards: Red, Yellow, Green, Blue. These sit in the center of the table. Index cards work fine — just color them solidly so the zone color is unmistakable.

### 7. Player Boards (1 per player)

Each player needs a board with 4 labeled zone slots (Red, Yellow, Green, Blue) to place cards face-down during deployment. Options:

- **Simple:** A sheet of paper divided into 4 labeled columns.
- **Better:** A folded piece of cardstock with labeled slots and a lip to hide cards from opponents.
- **Quickest:** Skip boards entirely — just have players place cards face-down in front of each Zone Card (works fine for casual play).

### 8. Score Tracking

Use a notepad, a phone, or small tokens (coins, poker chips, etc.) to track VP. Typical final scores are 20–40 VP, so plan accordingly.

### Card Count Summary

| Component | Count | Card Back |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Number Cards | 44 | Standard (same for all) |
| Mascots | 4 | Standard (same as number cards) |
| Action Cards | 4 (or 7 for Spice) | Action back (distinct from standard) |
| Duds | 4 | Action back (same as action cards) |
| Condition Cards | 12 | Condition back (third distinct back) |
| Zone Cards | 4 | N/A (face-up on table) |

**Total cards to make:** 68 (56 main deck + 12 conditions), plus 4 zone cards and player boards. With the Spice Option variant cards, 71 main + 12 conditions = 83 total.

## Designer Notes

This is **v0.1.5** — a working draft for playtesting. The game is currently themed "Tailgate Turf War" (sports/tailgate zone control). Art and a finalized title will come after the mechanics are validated.

### What's New in v0.1.5

- **Per-round passing:** You now pass cards every round, not just at setup. This means your hand reshapes each round, creating a fresh puzzle every time.
- **New action cards:** Heist and Contaminate replace Bomb and Swap as the default set. They create more interesting two-way decisions. Bomb, Swap, and Ambush are available as variant cards via the Spice Option.
- **Condition card overhaul:** 12 new conditions designed to shake up each round. Three HF disruptors ensure you can't autopilot on color matching. Rank disruptors, deployment constraints, and scoring twists create genuine variety.

### Playtesting Feedback Requested

- Does each round feel like a different puzzle?
- Are any condition cards confusing or unfun?
- Does the per-round passing feel good, or is it too much shuffling?
- How do Heist and Contaminate feel compared to Bomb and Swap?
- At 3 players: does sniper strategy (dumping everything at one zone) dominate?
