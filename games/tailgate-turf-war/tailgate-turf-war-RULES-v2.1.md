# TAILGATE TURF WAR v2.1
### A Game of Parking Lot Dominance and Strategic Commitment

**System:** Hamsters & Monsters Core Deck (66 cards) · **Time:** 15 Minutes · **Ages:** 10+ · **Players:** 2–5
**Version:** 2.1 — Balance Patch (March 15, 2026)

---

## THE PITCH

The stadium doors for the *Contests of Chaos* haven't opened yet, and the parking lot is a warzone of grills, coolers, and extreme face-painting. Six distinct tailgating zones are up for grabs. You must deploy your crew to dominate the pre-game party.

But here's the catch: **A massive squad is just dead weight.** A single, highly focused partier will routinely outscore a bloated, disorganized group of four. But show up alone with weak energy and the zone won't even notice you.

*Commit wisely. Survive the mishaps. Dominate the tailgate.*

---

## WHAT'S NEW IN v2.1

After running 3,000+ AI-vs-AI simulations across all player counts and five distinct play styles, we identified four balance issues and applied targeted fixes. Every change below is backed by before/after data.

- **Player Board** — Personal playmat with six zone slots eliminates card mix-ups and tracking confusion
- **Crew Bonus +3** — Pairing cards at a zone is now a real decision, not a trap
- **2-card multiplier removed** — No penalty for bringing a buddy; the math now rewards combos
- **Sweep threshold raised to 4** — Earning a Sweep means dominating the lot, not just showing up everywhere
- **Die-Hard Fan reduced to 3 VP** — Saving cards is still smart, but not the dominant strategy
- **Purple mishap reworked** — "The Extra Lap" now has an upside, making all six zones worth contesting

**Design goal:** Restore the core tension between *concentrating strength* and *spreading thin* that v2.0's math accidentally collapsed. And make it playable without a PhD in card organization.

## 1. PRE-GAME SETUP

### Step 1: Build the Parking Lot
Place one card of each faction color (Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Purple) face-up in a row. These are the six **Tailgate Zones**.

### Step 2: Deal Starting Hands
Shuffle the remaining 60 cards. Deal based on player count:

| Players | Cards Each | Game Feel |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| 2 | 18 cards | High tension, card-counting duel |
| 3 | 15 cards | The mathematical sweet spot |
| 4 | 13 cards | Tight economy, highly competitive |
| 5 | 11 cards | Pure slapstick chaos |

**The Brutal Truth:** You do *not* draw more cards. This single hand must last all three rounds.

### Step 3: Give Each Player
- 1 Player Board (place in front of you — this is where you'll play cards)
- 1 Hype Dial (set to ×1.0)
- 6 Crew Markers in their color
- Place their score marker on 0 of the shared Score Tracker

### Step 4: Place Hype Tokens
Create a supply of Hype tokens near the play area.

---

## 2. HOW TO PLAY (3 Rounds)

Each round has five phases. Play all three rounds to complete the game.

---

### PHASE 1: DEPLOYMENT (Simultaneous)

All players decide where to send their crew:

1. **Select cards** from your hand
2. **Place them face-down** in the matching zone slot on **your Player Board**
3. You may play:
   - Multiple cards to one zone
   - Cards to multiple zones
   - Zero cards (pass and save your hand)

**The Green Zone Exception:** If you play a Green card to the Green Zone, you may reveal it face-up immediately as a **taunt**. If you win that zone, gain +1 bonus VP.

Once everyone has placed cards, confirm readiness. No changes allowed after confirmation.

> **Why the Player Board matters:** Each player's cards stay on their own board throughout the round. No reaching across the table, no mixing up whose cards are whose. After the reveal, Headliner and Crew markers go directly onto the cards on your board. At the end of the round, sweep all played cards off your board into a discard pile — your remaining hand stays in your hand.

---

### PHASE 2: THE REVEAL

Flip all cards face-up simultaneously.

---

### PHASE 3: TAILGATE MISHAPS

If you played a **Natural Card (Ranks 1–9)** matching the Zone's color, it triggers a **Mishap**.

> **ANCHOR RULE:** Wild cards (Rank 0 and 10) **DO NOT** trigger Mishaps unless accompanied by a matching natural card (1–9) played by *you* at that same zone.

**Resolve Mishaps in ROYGBP order:**

| Zone | Mishap Name | Effect |
|------|-------------|--------|
| **RED** | Aggressive Grilling | Your Red natural cards here gain +2 to their base value |
| **ORANGE** | Cooler Scavenger | Claim the lowest-value opponent card here and add it to your squad as Crew |
| **YELLOW** | Premature Face-Painting | Your Yellow Crew cards here provide +4 Hype instead of +3 |
| **GREEN** | Weather Report | (Already resolved in Phase 1 if you revealed face-up) |
| **BLUE** | Sleight of Paw | Swap your Blue card's base value with one opponent's natural card (1-9) here |
| **PURPLE** | The Extra Lap | Return this Purple card to your hand. Gain **+2 VP** immediately. **Must** play it face-up next round. |

**How to Resolve:**
1. Start with Red Zone
2. All players with Red Mishaps resolve simultaneously
3. Move to Orange, then Yellow, etc.
4. If a Mishap adds cards to your squad (Orange), place a Crew Marker on them immediately

---

### PHASE 4: PARTY CALCULATION (The Headliner System)

Follow these steps at each zone where you played cards:

#### Step 1: Identify Your Headliner
Find your **single highest-value card** (after Mishap bonuses). This is your **Headliner**.

> **Place your Headliner marker** on this card to identify it visually.

#### Step 2: Count Your Crew
Every other card you played to this zone is **Crew**.

> **Place Crew markers** on these cards.

#### Step 3: Set Your Hype Dial
Count your total cards at this zone (Headliner + Crew). Set your Hype Dial:

| Cards | Dial Setting | Multiplier |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| 1 | SOLO | ×1.0 |
| 2 | DUO | ×1.0 |
| 3 | SCATTERED | ×0.6 |
| 4+ | CHAOS | ×0.5 |

> **v2.1 Change:** Two-card plays are no longer penalized. Bringing a buddy to the tailgate is now just as efficient as going solo — and the extra Crew bonus can put you over the top.

#### Step 4: Calculate Hype

**Base Hype = Headliner value + Crew bonuses**

| Card Type | Hype Value |
|-----------|------------|
| Headliner (ranks 1-9) | Face value |
| Headliner (rank 10) | 12 (10 + 2 Superstar bonus) |
| Crew (normal) | **+3 each** |
| Crew (Yellow at Yellow zone, mishap active) | +4 each |
| 0 (Mascot) as Crew | +3 |

**Then apply your Hype Dial multiplier:**

| Dial | Calculation | Example (Headliner 8, 1 Crew) |
|------|-------------|-------------------------------|
| SOLO (×1.0) | Base Hype × 1.0 | 8 = **8 Hype** |
| DUO (×1.0) | Base Hype × 1.0 | 8 + 3 = **11 Hype** |
| SCATTERED (×0.6) | Base Hype × 0.6, round up | (8 + 6) × 0.6 = 8.4 → **9 Hype** |
| CHAOS (×0.5) | Base Hype × 0.5, round up | (8 + 9) × 0.5 = 8.5 → **9 Hype** |

> **The v2.0 Fix (still applies):** Instead of doing math in your head, use **Hype Tokens**:
> - Take tokens equal to your Base Hype
> - Remove/replace tokens based on your dial setting
> - The tokens in front of you = your final Hype

**Special Card Rules:**

| Card | Effect |
|------|--------|
| **0 (Mascot)** | Doubles your natural Headliner (ranks 1-9). If Headliner is 10, 0 acts as normal Crew (+3) |
| **10 (Superstar)** | Automatically becomes Headliner. Worth 12 Hype (10 + 2 bonus) before multiplier |

**Example Calculations:**

| Cards Played | Headliner | Crew | Base | Dial | Final Hype |
|--------------|-----------|------|------|------|------------|
| 8 | 8 | 0 | 8 | SOLO | **8** |
| 10 | 10 | 0 | 12 | SOLO | **12** |
| 0, 8 | 8×2=16 | 0 | 16 | DUO | **16** |
| 8, 4 | 8 | +3 | 11 | DUO | **11** |
| 8, 4, 1 | 8 | +6 | 14 | SCATTERED | **9** (14×0.6) |
| 10, 4 | 10 | +3 | 15 | DUO | **15** |
| 0, 10, 3 | 10 | +6 | 18 | SCATTERED | **11** (18×0.6) |
| 5, 3, 2, 1 | 5 | +9 | 14 | CHAOS | **7** (14×0.5) |

> **Key v2.1 insight:** Notice how the Mascot + Natural combo at DUO (×1.0) is devastating: 0+8 = **16 Hype**. This is the strongest possible play per card. But you're committing two cards to one zone — is it worth leaving another zone uncontested?

---

### PHASE 5: SCORING THE ROUND

**Award Zone Control:**

| Result | VP Awarded |
|--------|------------|
| Highest Hype at zone | Full zone VP |
| Tie for highest | Split VP (round up) |
| No cards played | 0 VP |

**Zone VP by Round:**
- **Round 1:** 3 VP per zone
- **Round 2:** 5 VP per zone
- **Round 3:** 7 VP per zone

**Bonus VP:**

| Bonus | Condition | VP |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| **Underdog** | Win with exactly 1 card vs opponent's 2+ cards | +2 VP |
| **Sweep** | Win **4+ zones** in a single round | +3 VP |

> **v2.1 Change:** Sweep now requires winning 4 or more zones (was 3). In v2.0, the Sweep bonus fired too easily — it rewarded the strategy that was already dominant (spreading thin) instead of creating tension. At 4 zones, earning a Sweep means you truly dominated the round.

**Move score markers on the Score Tracker.**

---

## 3. GAME END & FINAL SCORING

After Round 3, the stadium doors open:

### The Die-Hard Fan Bonus
Reveal remaining cards in hand. The player with the **highest total value of unused cards** gains **+3 VP**.

> **v2.1 Change:** Reduced from +5 VP. Saving cards is still a valid strategy, but it no longer dominates. In simulation, the old +5 VP bonus let a "hoarder" strategy win 28% of 5-player games (expected: 20%). At +3 VP, all strategies compete on more equal footing.

### Final Tally
The player with the most VP wins the Tailgate Turf War.

**Tiebreaker:** Most zones won across all rounds → Highest single-card Hype achieved → Shared victory.

## 5. CHANGE LOG

### v2.0 → v2.1 (Balance Patch)

All changes backed by 3,000+ game simulations. Full data in `simulator/BALANCE_REPORT.md`.

#### 1. Crew Bonus: +2 → +3 (Major)
**Before:** Each Crew card added +2 Hype. Combined with the ×0.8 multiplier for 2 cards, a pair was almost never better than two singles at different zones.
**After:** Each Crew card adds +3 Hype. Combined with the removed 2-card penalty, pairing is now genuinely competitive with splitting.

**Why:** In v2.0, 88% of zone plays were single cards. The "concentrate vs. spread" tension — the game's core decision — had collapsed. Players always spread because the math never rewarded concentration. Now a rank-8 paired with a rank-3 gives 11 Hype at one zone (8 + 3 Crew bonus), which beats either card alone and makes you harder to dethrone.

#### 2. Hype Multiplier: ×0.8 removed for 2 cards (Major)
**Before:** 1 card = ×1.0, 2 cards = ×0.8, 3 cards = ×0.6, 4+ = ×0.5
**After:** 1 card = ×1.0, **2 cards = ×1.0**, 3 cards = ×0.6, 4+ = ×0.5

**Why:** The 2-card penalty meant that adding a Crew member to your Headliner was almost never worth it. A rank-8 + rank-4 gave (8 + 2) × 0.8 = 8 Hype — the same as the rank-8 alone. Now, 8 + 3 = 11 Hype at ×1.0 = 11. The DUO dial setting is a real "sweet spot" that rewards smart pairing without being overpowered (you're still spending an extra card).

**Impact on Mascot combos:** Mascot + high natural is now devastating: 0 + 9 = 18 × 1.0 = **18 Hype**. This was the game's signature big play and it was being suppressed by the ×0.8 penalty.

#### 3. Sweep Threshold: 3 zones → 4 zones (Medium)
**Before:** Win 3+ zones in a round for +3 VP.
**After:** Win **4+ zones** in a round for +3 VP.

**Why:** At 3 zones, Sweep triggered 2.2 times per game at 3 players and 3.2 times at 2 players. The dominant spread strategy naturally achieved Sweep without even trying — the bonus rewarded the strategy that was already best. At 4 zones, Sweep drops to 0.8 per game: it still happens (and still feels great), but it requires genuine board dominance rather than just spreading one card to each zone.

**Simulation data (3-player):**

| Threshold | Frequency | Score Spread |
|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| 3 zones (v2.0) | 2.21/game | 29.7 VP |
| **4 zones (v2.1)** | **0.81/game** | **26.2 VP** |

#### 4. Die-Hard Fan Bonus: 5 VP → 3 VP (Medium)
**Before:** Highest total value of unused cards = +5 VP
**After:** Highest total value of unused cards = **+3 VP**

**Why:** The "hoarder" strategy — deliberately saving high-value cards for the Die-Hard bonus — won 28.4% of 5-player games (expected: 20%). Saving two 9s or 10s for a guaranteed +5 VP was more efficient than actually playing those cards at zones. At +3 VP, saving is still rewarded (you're wisely managing your hand) but it's not the dominant path to victory.

**Simulation data (5-player style matchup):**

| Die-Hard VP | Hoarder Win % | Balanced Win % |
|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| 5 (v2.0) | 28.4% | 20.0% |
| **3 (v2.1)** | **25.8%** | **21.1%** |

#### 5. Purple Mishap Rework (Medium)
**Before:** "Return Purple card to hand. Must play face-up next round." (Purely negative — only mishap with no upside.)
**After:** "Return Purple card to hand. **Gain +2 VP immediately.** Must play face-up next round."

**Why:** In v2.0, Purple Zone averaged 0–1.3 wins per game depending on player count, versus 2.5–3.8 for other zones. Every other mishap has an upside (Red: +2 value, Orange: steal a card, Yellow: enhanced crew, Blue: value swap). Purple's was the only purely negative one, so rational players avoided triggering it — which meant avoiding Purple entirely. Adding +2 VP to the mishap creates a genuine trade-off: you lose zone presence this round but pocket guaranteed points. Players now have a reason to contest the Purple zone with Purple cards.

#### 6. Yellow Mishap Updated (Minor)
**Before:** Yellow Crew cards provide +4 Hype instead of +2.
**After:** Yellow Crew cards provide +4 Hype instead of +3.

**Why:** With the base Crew bonus now at +3, the Yellow mishap enhancement needed to adjust. At +4 (a +1 boost over the new base), Yellow's mishap remains a meaningful but modest bonus rather than a dramatic spike.

### What Stayed the Same
- Core Headliner + Crew mechanic
- All Mishaps except Purple and Yellow's bonus (resolution order unchanged)
- Round structure (3 rounds, 5 phases)
- VP progression (3/5/7)
- Underdog bonus (+2 VP for winning with 1 card vs 2+)
- Card quantities and hand sizes
- Mascot doubling and Superstar mechanics
- Deployment rules, reveal, and Green taunt

### v1.1 → v2.0 Changes (for reference)
#### 7. Player Board Added (Major — Usability)
**Before:** Players placed cards face-down directly beneath the shared Zone cards in the center of the table. At 4-5 players, cards from different players overlapped and got mixed up. After the reveal, identifying whose cards were whose — and which was Headliner vs. Crew — was a nightmare.
**After:** Each player gets a personal Player Board with six labeled zone slots. Cards stay on your board the entire round.

**Why:** This is a manufacturing addition, not a rule change. But it's the single biggest improvement to playability. The board eliminates every card-tracking confusion point that real players would hit. It also doubles as a quick reference (multiplier table and scoring printed on the bottom strip), replacing the separate pocket reference card.

**Cost impact:** One 11" × 8.5" cardboard sheet per player. Minimal manufacturing cost. Fits in standard game boxes.

See `tailgate-turf-war-RULES-v2.0.md` for the full v2.0 changelog. Key additions were the Hype Dial, Hype Tokens, Crew Markers, and streamlined teaching flow.

## 6. TESTING NOTES

### Simulation Results (v2.1)

We ran 500 games per configuration using AI-vs-AI heuristic simulation with five distinct play styles (balanced, aggressive, sniper, hoarder, spread).

**Seat balance (3-player):**

| Metric | v2.0 | v2.1 |
|--------|------|------|
| Max seat deviation from fair | 2.2% | 4.0% |
| Avg score spread | 29.7 VP | 26.2 VP |
| Sweep frequency | 2.21/game | 0.81/game |

**Strategy balance (5-player, one of each style):**

| Style | v2.0 Win % | v2.1 Win % | Fair: 20% |
|-------|------------|------------|-----------|
| Balanced | 20.0% | 22.4% | — |
| Aggressive | 18.6% | 14.9% | — |
| Sniper | 21.3% | 26.5% | — |
| Hoarder | **28.4%** | **25.8%** | — |
| Spread | 11.7% | 8.4% | — |

**Reading these results:** The Sniper style (Mascot + high natural combos) benefits most from the DUO multiplier change, as intended. Hoarder is still above-average but no longer dominant. The Spread and Aggressive styles remain the weakest — this is by design, since the game should reward *thoughtful* card allocation over brute-force volume.

### Hype Value Distribution

| Stat | v2.0 | v2.1 |
|------|------|------|
| Mean Hype | 6.7 | 7.1 |
| Max Hype | 18 | 22 |
| Median | 6 | 6 |

The slightly higher mean and max reflect the Crew +3 and DUO ×1.0 changes making strong plays stronger. The 0+9 Mascot combo now produces 18 Hype (up from 15 in v2.0), making it the game's most exciting moment.

### Cognitive Load (Unchanged from v2.0)

The v2.1 changes are all number tweaks — they don't add complexity. The DUO ×1.0 actually *reduces* cognitive load since players don't need to multiply for 2-card plays.

| Cards | v2.0 | v2.1 |
|-------|------|------|
| 1 | No math | No math |
| 2 | Multiply by 0.8 | **No math** |

## 7. PLAYTESTING GUIDANCE

### What to Watch For (Updated)

| Question | Good Sign | Warning Sign |
|----------|-----------|--------------|
| Are players making DUO plays? | Players debate pairing vs. splitting | Players still only play singles |
| Does Sweep feel earned? | "I got a Sweep!" is a big moment | Sweeps happen every round for someone |
| Is Purple Zone contested? | Players consider the +2 VP trade-off | Purple still ignored |
| Are Mascot combos exciting? | Players hunt for 0+9 or 0+8 plays | Mascots get thrown away solo |
| Is hand management engaging? | Players debate saving vs. spending | Players dump everything Round 1 |
| Does Die-Hard feel balanced? | Winner varies between savers and spenders | Same player always hoards and wins |

### Recommended A/B Tests for v2.2

**Test 1: Die-Hard by Card Count**
Instead of highest total value, award 1 VP per unused card. Prevents cherry-picking 9s and 10s.

**Test 2: Catch-Up Mechanism**
Player with lowest score at start of Round 2 and 3 draws 1 extra card from unused pool. Tightens score spread.

**Test 3: Scattered Multiplier**
Try ×0.7 instead of ×0.6 for 3-card plays. Gently makes 3-card plays more viable.

## DESIGNER NOTES

### Why v2.1 Exists

v2.0 nailed the presentation — Hype Dials, tokens, and streamlined teaching made the game accessible. But simulation revealed a hidden problem: **the math discouraged the game's most exciting plays.**

When pairing two cards is never better than splitting them, players converge on one strategy: spread single cards everywhere. The Mascot combo (the game's signature "wow" moment) was being penalized by the ×0.8 multiplier. The Sweep bonus rewarded the already-dominant spread strategy. And the Die-Hard Fan bonus let passive hoarding beat active engagement.

v2.1 fixes the incentives so that every strategy has a real shot:
- **Snipers** who hunt for Mascot combos at DUO can dominate zones
- **Spreaders** can still contest many zones, but need 4 wins for the Sweep
- **Hoarders** can save for Die-Hard, but the payoff is fairer
- **Balanced players** can mix and match each round

The core tension — *where do I commit my limited cards?* — now has multiple correct answers depending on your hand and the round.

### The DUO Sweet Spot

The most important change is the DUO multiplier. At ×1.0, a two-card play is exactly as efficient as two solo plays in terms of per-card Hype. But it concentrates that Hype at one zone, making you harder to beat there. This is the intended design: concentration isn't *more efficient*, but it's *more powerful* at the point of contest.

The real magic happens with Mascot combos. A Mascot + rank-9 at DUO = 18 Hype. That's an entire zone locked down with two cards, freeing the rest of your hand for other zones. This was always supposed to be the game's power play — now the math agrees.

### Target Audience (Unchanged)

- **Casual gamers** who want quick, fun decisions
- **Families** who need simple rules with hidden depth
- **H&M fans** looking for a different experience
- **Gateway gamers** ready for a step up
