# TAILGATE TURF WAR v3.0
### The Streamlined Edition

**System:** Hamsters & Monsters Core Deck (66 cards) · **Time:** 10–15 Minutes · **Ages:** 8+ · **Players:** 2–5
**Version:** 3.0 — Streamlined Edition (March 15, 2026)

---

## THE PITCH

Six tailgate zones. One parking lot. You and your rivals are deploying fans to claim territory before the stadium doors open. Play cards secretly, reveal simultaneously, and see who dominates.

**Three sentences, and you can play:**

> Place cards face-down at zones. Reveal everything at once. Highest strength wins each zone.

---

## COMPONENTS

- 66-card H&M deck (6 factions × ranks 0–10)
- 6 Zone cards (one per faction color)
- 1 Player Board per player (6 zone slots + reference strip)
- Score pad or shared score track

That's it. No tokens, no dials, no markers.

---

## SETUP

1. **Lay out the Lot.** Place the 6 Zone cards (Red, Orange, Yellow, Green, Blue, Purple) face-up in a row.
2. **Deal hands.** Shuffle the remaining 60 cards and deal:

| Players | Cards Each |
|---------|-----------|
| 2 | 18 |
| 3 | 15 |
| 4 | 13 |
| 5 | 11 |

3. **This hand must last all three rounds.** You do not draw more cards.
4. Give each player a Player Board. Set scores to 0.

---

## HOW TO PLAY

The game lasts **3 rounds**. Each round has three steps.

### Step 1: DEPLOY

All players simultaneously choose cards from their hand and place them **face-down** in the matching zone slots on their Player Board.

- You may play any number of cards to any number of zones.
- You may skip zones entirely.
- You may save cards for future rounds.
- Once everyone is ready, lock in. No changes.

### Step 2: REVEAL

Flip all cards face-up on all boards.

### Step 3: SCORE

Compare strength at each zone. The player with the highest strength wins that zone's VP.

**Zone VP:** Each zone is worth **5 VP** every round.

Ties split VP (round up). Uncontested zones (only one player present) still award VP to that player.

Record scores. Sweep all played cards into a shared discard pile. Keep your remaining hand for the next round.

---

## CALCULATING STRENGTH

At each zone where you played cards:

**Strength = Highest card's rank + 2 per additional card + Home Field bonus**

That's the whole formula. Here's each piece:

### 1. Your Best Card
Your highest-rank card at that zone sets your base strength. (If you played only one card, that's your strength before bonuses.)

### 2. Extra Cards: +2 Each
Every card beyond the first adds +2, regardless of its rank. A rank-1 backup adds the same +2 as a rank-9.

### 3. Home Field Advantage: +3 (The Anchor Rule)
If at least one **Natural card (ranks 1–9)** in your stack matches the zone's color, add +3. Mascots (0) and Superstars (10) are wild cards — they don't count as a matching natural for Home Field. You need a real faction member anchoring the play.

### Examples

| Zone | Cards Played | Calculation | Strength |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Red | Red 8 | 8 + 3 (home field) | **11** |
| Red | Blue 9 | 9 | **9** |
| Red | Red 7, Green 3 | 7 + 2 + 3 (home field) | **12** |
| Blue | Blue 6, Blue 4 | 6 + 2 + 3 (home field) | **11** |
| Green | Orange 10 | 10 | **10** |
| Red | Red 10 | 10 (no home field — 10 is not natural) | **10** |
| Red | Red 10, Red 5 | 10 + 2 + 3 (Red 5 anchors home field) | **15** |
| Yellow | Yellow 5, Red 2, Blue 1 | 5 + 2 + 2 + 3 (home field) | **12** |

Notice: a Red 8 at the Red zone (strength 11) beats a Red 10 there (strength 10) because the 8 anchors Home Field and the 10 can't. This gives natural cards a distinct tactical role.

## THE MASCOT (Rank 0)

The rank-0 card in each faction is the **Mascot** — a wild, game-changing fan.

**Rule:** If the Mascot is paired with at least one other card at the same zone, it **doubles** the highest other card's rank before adding anything else.

| Zone | Cards Played | Calculation | Strength |
|------|-------------|-------------|----------|
| Red | Red 0, Red 8 | (8 × 2) + 3 (Red 8 anchors home field) | **19** |
| Blue | Red 0, Red 8 | (8 × 2) | **16** |
| Red | Red 0, Red 10 | (10 × 2) — no home field (10 is not natural) | **20** |
| Green | Blue 0, Green 6, Red 2 | (6 × 2) + 2 + 3 (Green 6 anchors home field) | **17** |
| Any | Red 0 alone | 0 | **0** |

**Key details:**
- A Mascot played alone is worth 0. It's a huge risk.
- The Mascot's contribution is the doubling — it doesn't also add +2 as an "extra card." Any cards beyond the Mascot and the doubled card do add +2 each.
- Home Field requires a **natural card (1–9)** matching the zone in your stack. A Mascot or Superstar alone doesn't anchor Home Field — but if you also played a matching natural, you get the +3.

**Mascot math:**
- Mascot + one card = card × 2. Then check Home Field.
- Mascot + two cards = (best card × 2) + 2 for the third card. Then check Home Field.

---

## GAME END

After Round 3, total up all VP. Highest score wins.

**Tiebreaker:** Most zones won across all rounds → highest single strength achieved → shared victory.

---

## STRATEGY TIPS

**For new players** (read after your first game):

- **Pace yourself.** Every round is worth the same VP, so spread your cards across all three rounds. Play too heavy early and you'll have nothing left.
- **Home Field is powerful.** A rank-7 at its home zone (10 strength) beats an off-color rank-9. But a rank-10 can't anchor Home Field on its own — you need a natural (1–9) in that color. Look at what colors you were dealt.
- **The Mascot is a sniper rifle.** Mascot + 9 = 18 strength. That's almost unbeatable. But you spent two cards on one zone — was it worth skipping another?
- **Spreading thin wins zones.** A single rank-5 (strength 5) beats an empty zone for free VP. But you're using a card for just 3-5 points.
- **Concentration wins contested zones.** Two cards at one zone can overpower a single card. But you're leaving another zone open.
- **Watch opponent hand sizes.** If someone has lots of cards left, they're saving for Round 3. If someone played heavy, the late zones might be free.

## WHAT WE CUT (Designer Notes)

v3.0 is a ground-up simplification of v2.1. Here's what changed and why:

### Removed: Six Unique Mishaps → One Rule (Home Field Advantage)
v2.1 had six faction-specific effects triggered when you played a matching natural card to its home zone (Red: +2 value, Orange: steal a card, Yellow: +4 crew, Green: taunt for +1 VP, Blue: swap values, Purple: return card for +2 VP). Each was different, resolved in ROYGBP order, had edge cases, and required memorization. Home Field Advantage (+3 if you have a natural card matching the zone color) captures the same core idea — "playing at home is better" — in one sentence, with The Anchor Rule ensuring only real faction cards (ranks 1–9) count.

### Removed: Headliner/Crew/Multiplier System → Simple Addition
v2.1's hype calculation was a 4-step process: identify Headliner, count Crew, set Hype Dial, multiply. The multiplier table (×1.0/×1.0/×0.6/×0.5) was the single most-cited point of confusion. Now: highest card + 2 per extra card. Done. The "concentrate vs. spread" tension survives because +2 per extra card is always less than that card's face value at another zone — but concentration makes you harder to beat at the point of contest.

### Removed: Bonus VP Conditions
Underdog (+2 for winning with 1 card vs 2+), Sweep (+3 for winning 4+ zones), Die-Hard Fan (+3 for best saved hand), Green taunt (+1 VP for face-up reveal) — all cut. Each added a conditional check that players forgot mid-game.

### Changed: Flat VP (5/5/5) replaces Escalating VP (3/5/7)
v2.1's escalating VP (3→5→7 per zone by round) created a degenerate strategy: skip Rounds 1 and 2 entirely, dump all 15 cards in Round 3, and win 65.6% of games. The VP forfeited in early rounds gets split among opponents, so no one builds a decisive lead — then the hoarder dominates Round 3 with overwhelming card advantage. Flat 5 VP per zone per round eliminates this exploit (hoarder drops to 18% win rate) and simplifies the teach by one more sentence.

### Removed: Superstar Special Rule
Rank 10 was worth 12 (10 + 2 bonus) and was always the Headliner. Now it's just 10. It's still the best card. It doesn't need a special rule to feel special.

### Kept: The Mascot (Rank 0)
The doubling combo is the game's signature moment and it's one sentence to explain. Mascot + 9 = 18 is the kind of play people remember and talk about. It creates genuine risk/reward tension (a Mascot alone is worth 0) and rewards reading the board state.

### Kept: Player Boards
Individual boards with zone slots were v2.1's best usability addition. They're even more valuable in v3.0 since they're now the only physical component beyond the cards themselves.

### Kept: Core Identity
Simultaneous deployment, zone control, limited hand across 3 rounds, 6 factions. The game's fundamental tension — "where do I commit my limited cards?" — is unchanged. We just removed the layers of math and conditional rules sitting on top of it.

## THE 3-MINUTE TEACH

Use this script:

> "Here are six tailgate zones. You got dealt cards for the whole game — three rounds, no re-draws.
>
> Each round: pick cards from your hand, put them face-down on your board at whatever zones you want, then we all flip at once.
>
> Your strength at a zone is your best card, plus 2 for each extra card there, plus 3 if any of your cards is a natural — ranks 1 through 9 — matching the zone's color. Highest strength wins that zone — 5 points.
>
> One special card: the Mascot is rank 0. Alone it's worthless. But pair it with another card and it doubles that card's rank. Mascot plus a 9 is 18.
>
> Most points after three rounds wins. Ready?"

That's under 30 seconds to read aloud. The rest, players learn by playing.
