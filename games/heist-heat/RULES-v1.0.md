# 🦹 HEIST HEAT

### A Push-Your-Luck Vault Cracking Game for 2–5 Players

**System:** Hamsters & Monsters Core Deck (66 cards) · **Time:** 20–25 Minutes · **Ages:** 10+ · **Components:** Just the deck (no tokens needed)

**Version:** 1.0 — Designer Corps Final

---

## THE PITCH

The stadium vault is packed with loot — rare memorabilia, concession cash, VIP passes. You and your crew have breached the outer wall, but security is heating up fast. Every crack you make on the vault door raises the Heat. Push too far, and you'll all get caught.

The twist? **Chain reactions.** When you crack a vault compartment, you might trigger a cascade that empties half the vault in one move — if you played the right card. But the bigger the score, the hotter things get. Do you play it safe and getaway early with a small stash? Or push your luck for the big haul and risk losing everything?

*Crack the vault. Chain the combos. Escape before the Heat hits 10.*

---

## WHAT YOU NEED

Just the **Hamsters & Monsters deck** — 66 cards, six factions (ROYGBV), ranks 0–10.

**The Six Factions (Crew Specialties):**

| Color | Faction | Specialty | Cracking Style |
|-------|---------|-----------|----------------|
| 🔴 Red | Super-Dupes | **Power Crack** | High ranks, brute force |
| 🟠 Orange | Finders-Keepers | **Lucky Breaks** | Wildcard flexibility |
| 🟡 Yellow | Tinkerers | **Precision Tools** | Exact rank matches |
| 🟢 Green | Prognosticationers | **Inside Info** | Peek before cracking |
| 🔵 Blue | Magicians | **Sleight of Hand** | Steal from other stashes |
| 🟣 Purple | Time Travelers | **Second Chances** | Redo failed cracks |

**The Heat System:**
- Rank **0** = ALARM! Adds +3 Heat when revealed
- Rank **1–3** = Low security (adds +1 Heat)
- Rank **4–7** = Medium security (adds +2 Heat)  
- Rank **8–10** = High security (adds +3 Heat)

## SETUP (3 Minutes)

### 1. Create The Vault

Shuffle the deck. Based on player count, deal cards face-down in a grid:

| Players | Vault Grid | Total Cards |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| 2 | 3×4 | 12 cards |
| 3 | 3×5 | 15 cards |
| 4 | 4×4 | 16 cards |
| 5 | 4×5 | 20 cards |

This is **The Vault**. Leave space above it for the **Heat Track** (0–10).

### 2. Deal Starting Hands

- **2 players:** 7 cards each
- **3 players:** 6 cards each
- **4 players:** 5 cards each
- **5 players:** 5 cards each

### 3. Starting Heat

Place a card (or any marker) on **Heat 0** of the Heat Track.

### 4. Choose Starting Player

Whoever most recently watched a heist movie goes first. Play proceeds clockwise.

---

## THE CORE LOOP

Heist Heat is played over **3 Rounds**. Each round:
1. Players take turns cracking the vault or escaping
2. The Heat rises with each crack
3. Players can getaway when Heat reaches 7+
4. When Heat hits 10, the round ends — anyone still inside is BUSTED
5. Score your stash, then reset for the next round

---

## ON YOUR TURN

Choose ONE of the following:

### 🅰️ CRACK (Attempt to Claim Loot)

1. **Choose a vault card** — Point to any face-down or face-up card in the Vault
2. **Play a card from your hand** — Place it face-up in front of you
3. **Reveal the vault card** — Flip it face-up
4. **Compare ranks:**
   - If your card's **rank ≥ vault card's rank** → **SUCCESS!**
   - If your card's **rank < vault card's rank** → **FAILURE!**

#### Success: The Crack Works!

- **Claim the vault card** — Add it to your face-up **Stash**
- **Chain Reaction** — Also claim any orthogonally adjacent cards (up, down, left, right) that have **rank ≤ your played card's rank**
  - These can be face-up or face-down (flip them as you claim)
  - Continue chaining as long as adjacent cards qualify
- **Increase Heat** — Add Heat based on the **highest-rank card you claimed this turn**:
  - Rank 1–3: +1 Heat
  - Rank 4–7: +2 Heat
  - Rank 8–10: +3 Heat
  - Rank 0 (Alarm): +3 Heat

#### Failure: The Crack Fails!

- Your played card is **discarded** (you lose it)
- The vault card **stays** (face-up, now everyone knows what it is)
- **No Heat increase** (failed cracks don't raise Heat)
- **Faction Penalty** — If you failed with a rank 8+ card, add +1 Heat anyway (the noise attracts attention)

### 🅱️ GETAWAY (Escape with Your Stash)

You can only getaway when **Heat is 7 or higher**.

- Announce "GETAWAY!"
- Your Stash is **secured** — set it aside face-down (you'll score it at round end)
- You are **safe** for this round
- You take no more actions this round

**Important:** Once you getaway, you cannot re-enter the vault this round.

---

## THE ALARM CARDS (Rank 0)

Rank 0 cards are **Alarms** — the vault's security system.

- When an Alarm is **revealed** (cracked or chained), immediately add **+3 Heat**
- Alarms have **rank 0**, so any card can crack them
- However, Alarms **don't chain** — they stop the chain reaction
- If you claim an Alarm, it goes to your Stash but is worth **0 points** at scoring

**The Alarm Penalty:** If the round ends and you have an Alarm in your Stash, you must **discard your highest-value card** (by rank) from your Stash before scoring.

---

## FACTION POWERS (Once Per Round)

Each faction has a **once-per-round** power. You can use it when you play a card of that faction to crack.

| Faction | Power | Effect |
|---------|-------|--------|
| 🔴 **Red** | **"Power Through"** | Your card counts as +2 rank for this crack only |
| 🟠 **Orange** | **"Lucky Guess"** | Before revealing, peek at the vault card. You may choose a different target. |
| 🟡 **Yellow** | **"Perfect Fit"** | If your card matches the vault card's rank exactly, claim ALL orthogonally adjacent cards (ignore rank) |
| 🟢 **Green** | **"Inside Man"** | Look at up to 3 face-down vault cards before choosing your target |
| 🔵 **Blue** | **"Sleight of Hand"** | Instead of cracking, steal 1 random card from an opponent's Stash (they must have at least 2 cards) |
| 🟣 **Purple** | **"Time Rewind"** | After a failed crack, take your card back to your hand (doesn't count as your turn's action) |

**Using Powers:** Announce the power when you play the card. Resolve the effect, then continue with the crack (or alternate action for Blue/Purple).

---

## ROUND END (When Heat Hits 10)

When the Heat Track reaches **10**, the round ends immediately:

1. **Current player finishes their turn** (if mid-crack)
2. **Busted Players:** Anyone who didn't getaway loses their entire Stash — discard all cards
3. **Safe Players:** Those who got away keep their Stashed cards
4. **Scoring:** All safe players score their Stash (see below)
5. **Reset:** Collect all cards, shuffle, rebuild the Vault for the next round

---

## SCORING YOUR STASH

At the end of each round, score the cards in your secured Stash:

| Pattern | Requirements | Points |
|---------|--------------|--------|
| **Pair** | 2 cards of same faction | 2 points |
| **Triple Faction** | 3 cards of same faction | 5 points |
| **Triple Rank** | 3 cards of same rank (any factions) | 7 points |
| **Sequence** | 3 cards in consecutive ranks (e.g., 4-5-6) | 6 points |
| **Rainbow** | 5 cards of different factions | 10 points |
| **High Card Bonus** | Each rank 8+ card | +1 point each |

**Scoring Rules:**
- Each card can only count toward **one pattern** (choose the highest value)
- You may score multiple patterns with different cards
- Alarms (rank 0) are worth 0 points and cannot be part of patterns
- **Alarm Penalty:** If you have any Alarms, discard your highest-rank card before scoring

**Example Stash:** Red 4, Red 7, Red 9, Blue 5, Blue 5, Yellow 6, Alarm 0
- Score Triple Faction (Red 4, Red 7, Red 9) = 5 points
- Score Triple Rank (Blue 5, Blue 5... need third 5) — can't score
- Score Pair (Blue 5, Blue 5) = 2 points  
- Alarm Penalty: Discard Red 9
- **Total: 7 points**

---

## GAME END & VICTORY

After **3 complete rounds**, total your scores from all rounds.

**Highest total VP wins!**

**Tiebreakers:**
1. Most rounds successfully got away (didn't bust)
2. Most total cards stashed across all rounds
3. Shared victory (split the loot!)

## GREGOR'S CLIPBOARD

**What if the Vault is empty before Heat hits 10?**
The round ends immediately. All players with Stashes score them. No one is busted (security arrived to an empty vault).

**Can I look at other players' Stashes?**
No — Stashes are face-down once secured. You can only see what cards they've played to crack (in front of them) and what's still in the Vault.

**What if I run out of cards?**
If your hand is empty, you cannot crack. You must wait and hope the round ends, or getaway if Heat allows.

**Can I getaway on the same turn I crack?**
No — you must choose one action per turn. If you crack and Heat jumps to 10+, the round ends and you're busted unless you already got away earlier.

**Do Alarms chain?**
No — Alarms stop chain reactions. They're security dead-ends.

**What counts as "orthogonally adjacent"?**
Up, down, left, right — no diagonals.

**Can I use faction powers after revealing the vault card?**
No — powers must be announced when you play the card, before revealing.

**What if two patterns overlap?**
Each card can only count toward one pattern. Choose the scoring that maximizes your points.

**Can I getaway with an empty Stash?**
Yes, but you score 0 points for the round.

**What if Heat is already 10 and it's my turn?**
The round has already ended — you don't get another turn.

## DESIGNER NOTES

### What Worked

**The Chain Reaction Mechanic:** This is the core innovation. When you crack with a high card, you can cascade through multiple vault cards in one action. It creates those "jackpot" moments where you clear half the vault in one turn — but also raises Heat dramatically. The risk/reward is delicious.

**Push-Your-Luck with Shared Risk:** Unlike traditional push-your-luck games where each player faces their own risk, Heist Heat has shared Heat. When one player pushes, everyone feels the pressure. It creates table dynamics where players root for failures and groan at big successes.

**The Getaway Threshold:** Having a specific Heat threshold (7+) to escape creates a natural tension arc. Early game is about building your Stash. Mid-game (Heat 4-6) is about deciding when to push. Late game (Heat 7+) is about timing your escape perfectly.

**Faction Powers as Comeback Mechanisms:** The once-per-round powers give players tools to recover from bad luck. Green's peek prevents failures. Purple's rewind saves your card. Blue's steal lets you sabotage leaders. These feel powerful but fair.

**Alarm Penalty:** Making Alarms dangerous even when claimed (discard your best card) creates interesting decisions. Do you avoid Alarms entirely? Or claim them strategically to deny others the chain reaction?

### What Needed Iteration (v0.1 → v0.5 → v1.0)

**Iteration 1:** Originally, all vault cards added +1 Heat regardless of rank. **Issue:** No incentive to avoid high-value cards, no tension in choosing targets. **Fix:** Heat scales with card rank (1-3 = +1, 4-7 = +2, 8-10 = +3). Now high-value cards are riskier.

**Iteration 2:** Originally, chain reactions didn't exist — you only claimed the target card. **Issue:** Too slow, not enough loot per round, scoring was sparse. **Fix:** Added chain reactions. Now a single crack can claim 3-4 cards, creating exciting turns and meaningful scoring.

**Iteration 3:** Originally, Alarms ended the round immediately. **Issue:** Too swingy, felt bad to have a good run ended by someone else hitting an Alarm. **Fix:** Alarms add +3 Heat but don't end the round. Creates urgency without instant punishment.

**Iteration 4:** Originally, there was no getaway mechanism — everyone played until Heat 10. **Issue:** No player agency, leader just won. **Fix:** Added getaway at Heat 7+. Now players choose when to lock in their score vs. push for more.

**Iteration 5:** Originally, faction powers were unlimited. **Issue:** Blue's steal was oppressive, Green's peek made the game too easy. **Fix:** Limited powers to once per round. Creates timing decisions about when to use them.

**Iteration 6:** Originally, busted players kept their cards but scored half. **Issue:** Not enough penalty for pushing too far. **Fix:** Busted players lose everything. Now the getaway decision is genuinely tense.

**Iteration 7:** Originally, scoring was just "most cards wins." **Issue:** No depth, no set collection strategy. **Fix:** Added pattern scoring (pairs, triples, sequences, rainbows). Now players build toward specific combinations.

### Core Innovation

Heist Heat combines three mechanics not seen together in the H&M collection:

1. **Push-your-luck with shared risk meter** — Everyone faces the same Heat, creating table-wide tension
2. **Chain reaction spatial puzzle** — Where you crack affects what you can claim through chaining
3. **Timed escape mechanism** — Getaway threshold creates a "music stops" moment

This creates a game where:
- Every crack affects everyone (shared risk)
- Position in the vault matters (spatial strategy)
- Timing your escape is crucial (push-your-luck)
- Comebacks are possible through faction powers
- The 0s (Alarms) and 10s (high risk/reward) have clear identities

### Inspirations

- **Incan Gold / Diamant** — Push-your-luck fundamentals, shared risk
- **King of Tokyo** — Risk threshold, timing your exit
- **Carcassonne** — Spatial chain reactions, tile adjacency
- **Qwirkle** — Pattern matching for scoring
- **Can't Stop** — Press-your-luck with rising stakes

- Groups that like shared tension and table dynamics

- H&M fans looking for a fast, chaotic experience
- Players who enjoy "jackpot" moments and comeback stories

### Key Distinctions

**Vs. Incan Gold/Diamant:** Heist Heat adds spatial chain reactions (where you crack matters) and faction powers, creating more strategy than pure push-your-luck.

**Vs. Other H&M Games:** This is the **only** push-your-luck game and the **only** game with a shared risk meter that affects all players. The chain reaction mechanic is completely unique to this title.

**The Hook:** This is the H&M game where you push your luck against the whole table, not just the deck. When someone cracks big, everyone feels the Heat. Will you getaway with your small stash, or risk it all for the perfect heist?
