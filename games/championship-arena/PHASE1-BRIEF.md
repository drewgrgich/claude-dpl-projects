# PHASE 1 — DESIGN BRIEF
## Championship Arena by Designer Corps (Morgan, Jordan, Casey)

---

## 1. WHAT THE GAME DOES WELL

**Strengths of v1.0:**

- **Six genuinely asymmetric Ring powers.** Each Ring plays a different scoring "game" — not just加减 numbers but fundamentally different logic. Purple's "lowest wins" inversion is the standout, but all six create real strategic variety.
- **Simultaneous commit with open dice placement.** The tension of committing blind while seeing where others are competing is the engine of the game. It creates real decisions without a long planning phase.
- **The Stunt Double.** A face-down trump card you can move post-reveal is elegant. It adds a poker-style bluff layer without complicating the rule set.
- **Sweep incentive (6 FP for all 3 Rings).** The nonlinear scoring (1/3/6) rewards ambition and creates dramatic swings. A single round can change everything.
- **The Stunt Pool market.** Paying exact die value to draft is a neat constraint — it ties your market access to your luck, creating interesting tension.
- **Quick runtime (20–30 min).** The game doesn't overstay its welcome. Dense decisions in a short window.

---

## 2. GAPS vs. THE H&M CATALOG

Championship Arena is a functional reskin of Court of Shadows. As an H&M game, it's missing several elements that define the brand identity:

| H&M Mechanic | Found In | In Championship Arena? |
|---|---|---|
| **Run tracking** (cards played in sequence matter) | Contests of Chaos | ❌ No runs |
| **Faction identity** (cards trigger differently by faction) | All three games | ❌ Factions are cosmetic |
| **Combo chains** (sequence cards for amplified effects) | Contests of Chaos | ❌ No combos |
| **Shared deck memory** (the deck has state players track) | Contests of Chaos, Summit Scramble | ❌ No deck state |
| **Chaos/spectator element** (external forces disrupt plans) | Contests of Chaos | ❌ No external disruption |
| **Shedding pressure** (must dump cards or suffer) | Get Stuffed | ❌ No pressure to discard |
| **Climbing/tiered structure** | Summit Scramble | ❌ Flat structure |
| **The Signature Moment** (the "wow" thing you tell people about) | All three | ❌ None identified |

**Key gap:** The game doesn't feel like it *needs* the H&M deck. Swap the hamster art for generic art and the game plays identically. The deck's identity — its factions, its rank structure, the shared state it creates — is completely inert.

## 3. FIVE FRESH CONCEPT IDEAS

### Concept A — SPECTATOR MAYHEM
**Core idea:** The deck generates Spectator cards. Spectators are chaotic forces — they boost a random player, curse a random Ring, or demand a challenge from the crowd. The arena is alive.

**Mechanic sketch:**
- During setup, deal 3 "Spectator" cards face-up beside the arena (not in the Stunt Pool — separate).
- At the START of each round, draw 1 Spectator card. It triggers immediately.
- Spectator effects: "The Crowd loves the Purple Ring — Double Points there!" / "Challenge: highest single die at Red Ring wins a bonus FP." / "Rivalry: two named players must each commit +1 die to Yellow Ring." / "Wild Card: spectators dump 3 random cards into the Stunt Pool."
- After triggering, discard Spectator cards. Draw a new one next round.
- **Why it's H&M:** Chaos, whimsy, external forces disrupting plans. This is what Contests of Chaos players love — the game has a mind of its own.

---

### Concept B — FACTION RIVALRY PROTOCOL
**Core idea:** Your Faction matters. When you play cards matching your faction's Ring, you get bonus effects. Play too many off-faction cards and the crowd turns on you.

**Mechanic sketch:**
- Each player secretly notes their "home faction" at game start (or it defaults to your Talent's faction).
- When you play 2+ cards of your home faction at a single Ring → you get a Faction Bonus (e.g., draw a card, add +3 to that Ring's score, or activate your Talent for free).
- When you play NO cards of your home faction all round → the Crowd turns on you. Lose 1 FP (if you have any) or your next Ring win is voided.
- Each Ring is permanently linked to a faction (as currently mapped: Red = Super-Dupes, Orange = Finders, etc.). When you play at your faction's Ring, you're "representing."
- **Why it's H&M:** Creates faction identity. Makes you care which cards you're holding. Creates a push-pull: play your strong faction cards where they matter, or spread them to win multiple Rings.

---

### Concept C — THE RUN TRACKER
**Core idea:** The deck has memory. Track which ranks have been played. Complete "runs" (consecutive ranks) to trigger bonuses. The deck is a shared resource, not just a card pool.

**Mechanic sketch:**
- A shared run tracker on the table: a strip of numbers 0–10.
- Whenever a card is revealed at any Ring, mark that rank as "played" on the tracker.
- If you complete a run of 3+ consecutive played ranks in a single round → you get a Run Bonus (e.g., draw 2 cards, claim any Ring at +5, etc.)
- If all 11 ranks (0–10) have been played → trigger "Deck Crisis" — shuffle all Trophy cards and face-up discards back into the deck, reset the tracker. It's a reset valve AND a dramatic moment.
- **Why it's H&M:** This is the engine of Contests of Chaos. The shared deck state creates emergent strategy — you start tracking what's "used up" and what's still in play.

---

### Concept D — THE SHOWSTOPPER CHAIN
**Core idea:** Chain cards in sequence for amplified effects. The crowd rewards spectacle.

**Mechanic sketch:**
- At the start of the game, flip the top 3 cards of the deck face-up. These are the "Show Cards" — they define bonus conditions for the round.
- Example: "Rank 5 = Wild" / "Adjacent ranks played at the same Ring = +4 bonus" / "Three of a kind at one Ring = claim any Ring free."
- The Show Cards change each round (flip 3 new ones).
- When you trigger a Show Card condition, you execute the bonus and discard that Show Card. Someone chasing a Show Card changes their strategy mid-commit.
- **Why it's H&M:** Creates "the moment." The showstopper is the climactic turn where everything clicks and the crowd goes wild.

---

### Concept E — THE CROWD FAVORITE TWIST
**Core idea:** The Crowd is a 5th player — and it's active. The Crowd Favorite (a rotating role) gets a special action each round. But the Crowd is fickle: you can win its favor or lose it based on your play.

**Mechanic sketch:**
- At the start of each round, the player with the most Trophy cards is the **Crowd Favorite**.
- The Crowd Favorite gets ONE action: reveal the Stunt Double of any opponent, swap one of your dice with an opponent's die at any Ring, or declare a Ring "Wild" for the round.
- At the END of each round, if the Crowd Favorite didn't win a Ring this round, they lose the title. The next round, the player with the most Trophy cards claims it.
- **Why it's H&M:** The "fickle crowd" is a classic show-business trope. It adds a jockeying for status element that creates interesting secondary objectives.

## 4. RECOMMENDATION

### **PURSUE: CONCEPT A — SPECTATOR MAYHEM**

**Rationale:**

1. **It solves the "signature moment" problem.** The Spectator cards create moments that are *specific to this game*. A random Spectator effect can turn a losing position into a winning one, or force a player to pivot dramatically. These are the stories players tell after the game.

2. **It preserves the existing structure.** All six Rings, all Talents, the Stunt Double, the Stunt Pool, the 1/3/6 scoring — everything stays. We add Spectator cards as a layer on top. No rewriting, just expansion.

3. **It makes the game feel like a SHOW.** Right now, Championship Arena feels like "dice and cards on a table." Spectator cards make it feel like a *televised competition*. The whimsy and chaos of H&M lives in the Spectator mechanic.

4. **It's mechanically clean.** Spectator cards are simple: draw 1, trigger effect, discard. Players don't need to track complex state. The chaos is external, not self-referential.

5. **It differentiates Championship Arena from its parent.** Court of Shadows has no spectator element. This is the first thing that makes Championship Arena *not just a reskin*.

**Runner-up: Concept B (Faction Rivalry)** — would also work well and could be combined with A in a future version. For v1.1, we do A only to keep scope manageable.

---

## 5. WHAT CHAMPIONSHIP ARENA'S SIGNATURE MOMENT SHOULD BE

**The Spectator Twist.** When a Spectator card drops and suddenly everyone's best-laid plans get scrambled. Maybe the Purple Ring — where lowest wins — suddenly becomes Double Points, and a player who was deliberately losing to win Purple suddenly has to recalculate everything. Or the Crowd demands a challenge at Red Ring between the two players who both went all-in on dice, and only the highest single die wins the bonus. These moments are unscripted, dramatic, and *funny* — which is exactly H&M's brand.
