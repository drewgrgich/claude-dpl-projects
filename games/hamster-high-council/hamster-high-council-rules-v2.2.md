# 🏛️ HAMSTER HIGH COUNCIL v2.2
### The Shifting Alliance Trick-Taking Game for 4 Players

**System:** Hamsters & Monsters Core Deck (66 cards) · **Time:** 25–35 Minutes · **Ages:** 10+

---

## What's Going On Here?

The High Council runs the Stadium — but alliances shift after every single trick. One moment you're partners with the player across from you. The next trick, you're rivals. The trick after that? Partners again.

This isn't traditional trick-taking. This is **dynamic partnership** — where reading the table matters as much as reading your hand. Where a low card can be more valuable than a high card. Where the player in last place can force a comeback by manipulating the alliance structure.

**Win tricks. Shift alliances. Score points.**

First to **60 Victory Points** claims the gavel.

---

## What Makes This Different

| Traditional Trick-Taking | Hamster High Council |
|-------------------------|----------------------|
| Fixed partnerships (2v2) | Alliances shift every trick |
| Highest card always wins | Lowest card wins at one dial position |
| Score at round end | Score immediately when you win a trick |
| Comebacks are rare | Alliance manipulation creates swing turns |
| Partners share a score | Partners cooperate but score individually |

## Setup

1. **Shuffle** all 66 cards. This is the **Snack Vault** (draw pile).
2. Designate a face-up **Discard Crate** (discard pile).
3. Place the **Alliance Dial** in the center, set to **CROSS**.
4. **Deal 8 cards** to each player.
5. Determine a starting **Dealer** randomly. Dealer passes left each round.
6. Run **Council Balance** to establish Trump and Elite factions (see below).
7. Player to the left of the Dealer leads the first trick.

---

## The Alliance System

This is the heart of the game.

### Alliance Positions

The Alliance Dial has three positions. Each position defines two things: who your **partner** is, and how the current trick **resolves**.

| Position | Your Partner | Trick Rule | Scoring Multiplier |
|----------|-------------|------------|-------------------|
| **CROSS** ➕ | Player Across | Highest wins | ×1 |
| **LEFT** ↶ | Player to Your Left | Highest wins | ×1 |
| **RIGHT** ↷ | Player to Your Right | **Lowest wins** | **×2** (see VP Compression) |

### How Alliances Shift

**When you win a trick, rotate the Alliance Dial one position clockwise:** CROSS → LEFT → RIGHT → CROSS.

Your partner is determined by the dial position **at the moment a trick is played**, not at the end of the round. Every trick exists in its own alliance context.

### What "Partner" Means

Your partner does not score points from your wins. Partnership is about **cooperation during play**: your partner throws you favorable cards, avoids trumping your lead, sacrifices tricks so you can win at a better dial position. The alliance system drives *how you play* — not how you divide points.

## Phase 1: Council Balance

Before tricks begin, establish Trump and Elite factions.

1. Reveal cards one at a time from the Snack Vault until **two different factions** appear.
2. **Seat 1 — THE BIG CHEESE (Trump):** The first faction revealed becomes Trump. Trump cards beat all other factions.
3. **Seat 2 — THE FAVORITES (Elite):** The second *different* faction revealed becomes Elite. Elite cards break ties.
4. **Discard all revealed cards** to the Crate. They are out of play for this round.
5. **Safety cap:** If 5 cards are revealed without a second faction appearing, discard all revealed cards and re-deal the Council Balance from scratch.

Keep the Trump and Elite factions visible (use the revealed cards as reminders or announce them clearly).

---

## Phase 1.5: Castle's Blessing (Catch-Up)

**Skip this step in Round 1** (no scores to compare yet).

Starting in Round 2, after dealing cards but before the first trick, trailing players get to improve their hands:

1. **Last place** draws **3 extra cards** from the Snack Vault, looks at all 11 cards, keeps the best 8, and shuffles the remaining 3 back into the Vault.
2. **Second-to-last** draws **2 extra cards** from the Snack Vault, looks at all 10 cards, keeps the best 8, and shuffles the remaining 2 back into the Vault.
3. All other players keep their dealt hands as-is.

**Ties:** If multiple players are tied for last, all tied players receive the full last-place blessing (draw 3, keep 8). If multiple players are tied for second-to-last, all receive the second-place blessing (draw 2, keep 8).

**Why this exists:** Trick-taking rewards good hands, and early leads compound. Castle's Blessing gives trailing players better cards — not a free ride, but sharper tools. You still have to play them well.

## Phase 2: Tricks

### Who Leads?

- **First trick of the round:** Player to the left of the Dealer.
- **Every trick after:** The winner of the previous trick.

### Playing a Card

1. **Lead:** The leader plays any single card from their hand face-up. Its faction is the **led faction** for this trick.
2. **Follow:** In clockwise order, each other player must play a card of the led faction if they have one.
3. **Off-Suit:** If you cannot follow the led faction, you may play any card from your hand.

### Resolving the Trick

Resolve in this exact order. Stop at the first step that produces a winner.

**Step 1 — Trump Check.** Were any Trump cards played? If yes, the **highest-ranked Trump card** wins. (Trump cards beat everything, even if they didn't follow the led faction.)

**Step 2 — Alliance Resolution.** No Trump was played. Check the Alliance Dial:
- **CROSS or LEFT:** The **highest-ranked card** of the led faction wins.
- **RIGHT:** The **lowest-ranked card** of the led faction wins.

Cards that are off-suit and non-Trump cannot win the trick.

**Step 3 — Tiebreak.** If two or more cards of the led faction are tied for the winning rank:
- If one of the tied cards is **Elite**, it wins.
- If still tied (multiple Elite, or no Elite among tied cards), all cards in the trick go to the Crate. No one wins the trick. No VP is scored. No dial rotation occurs. The same player leads again.

### After Winning a Trick

Perform these steps in order:

1. **Score VP.** Count the total number of cards in the trick (normally 4). Multiply by the current Alliance Dial position: CROSS or LEFT = ×1, RIGHT = ×2. The **winner alone** scores that many VP. *(Exception: VP Compression — see Scoring below.)*
2. **Discard the trick.** All cards from the trick go face-up to the Crate. No one keeps won cards.
3. **Rotate the Alliance Dial** one position clockwise (CROSS → LEFT → RIGHT → CROSS).
4. **Faction Talent** (optional). If the card you won with has a Faction Talent, you may activate it now. See Faction Talents below.
5. **Lead the next trick.**

---

## Special Rules

### 🐣 The Intern (Rank 0)

When you **play** a Rank 0 card — win or lose — immediately draw 1 card from the Snack Vault (if any cards remain). This happens the moment you play the card, before the trick resolves.

Interns are weak but they keep your hand full.

### End of Round

The round ends when **any player plays their last card from hand.** Finish resolving the current trick (including scoring VP if someone wins it), then:

1. All remaining cards in all players' hands are discarded to the Crate.
2. The round is over.

There is no bonus trick. The round ends cleanly.

### New Round

If no player has reached 60 VP:

1. Shuffle everything — Crate and Snack Vault — into a fresh 66-card deck.
2. Reset the Alliance Dial to **CROSS**.
3. The player with the **lowest total VP** becomes the new Dealer. (Tied? Previous Dealer passes left as normal.)
4. Deal 8 cards to each player.
5. Run a new **Council Balance**.
6. Run **Castle's Blessing** (trailing players improve their hands).
7. Play.

## ✨ Faction Talents

Talents trigger **when you win a trick** with a card of that faction (any rank 0–10). All talents are **optional** — you may decline to use them.

Talents resolve **after** VP is scored, after the trick is discarded to the Crate, and after the Alliance Dial is rotated.

---

### 🔴 Red — Rally the Dupes

**Effect:** Rotate the Alliance Dial **back** one position (counter-clockwise). This reverses the rotation that just happened from winning the trick.

**Net result:** The dial stays where it was before you won. You've frozen the alliance structure.

**Advanced use:** If you win a trick and the forward rotation moved the dial *off* a position you wanted, Rally undoes that. If the forward rotation moved the dial *to* RIGHT and you don't want the next trick resolved as low-wins, Rally pulls it back.

---

### 🟠 Orange — Finders Keepers

**Effect:** Look through the Crate. Swap 1 card from your hand with any 1 card in the Crate. Return the Crate to its face-up pile.

You may look through the entire Crate. Take your time — your opponents can see what you chose.

---

### 🟡 Yellow — Quick Fix

**Effect:** When you lead the next trick, you play **two cards simultaneously** instead of one. Both cards must be the **same faction** (the led faction). Both cards are "in the trick" — opponents follow the led faction as normal.

**Resolution:** Only the card that *would win* among your two counts for trick resolution. (At CROSS/LEFT, your higher card competes. At RIGHT, your lower card competes.) The other card is simply part of the trick's total card count.

**Scoring impact:** The trick now contains 5 cards instead of 4 (your two + three opponents' cards). If you win, you score 5 × the dial multiplier.

**Hand cost:** You've played two cards from hand. You will run out of cards sooner. That's the tradeoff — power now, fewer options later.

**Requirements:** You must have at least 2 cards of the same faction in hand to use Quick Fix. If you don't, the talent fizzles (no effect).

**Intern interaction:** If either of your two cards is a Rank 0, you draw from the Snack Vault as normal for each 0 played.

---

### 🟢 Green — Saw It Coming

**Effect:** Look at the top 3 cards of the Snack Vault. Keep 1, return the other 2 to the top of the Vault in any order you choose.

If the Vault has fewer than 3 cards, look at whatever remains. If the Vault is empty, this talent has no effect.

---

### 🔵 Blue — Sleight of Paw

**Effect:** Choose 2 opponents. Each takes 1 random card from their hand, and they swap those cards with each other.

**Procedure:** Each chosen opponent shuffles their hand face-down, and the other chosen opponent draws 1 card at random. Both drawn cards are swapped simultaneously.

If any chosen opponent has no cards in hand, the talent fizzles.

---

### 🟣 Purple — Pocket the Past

**Effect:** Bank 1 card from your hand face-down in front of you — it scores **1 VP** immediately. Then draw 1 card from the Snack Vault. Then discard 1 card from your hand to the Crate.

**Net result:** You trade a hand card for 1 VP and cycle your hand (draw 1, discard 1). Your hand size stays the same.

**Why this is powerful:** It's guaranteed VP — no trick required. Over a game, repeatedly activating Pocket the Past adds up. It also lets you dump low-value cards while fishing for better ones.

**Why this is balanced:** 1 VP is modest compared to winning a trick (4–10 VP). You're paying a card and a discard for incremental progress. The real value is consistency — Purple wins give you both trick VP and a small bonus, but neither half is overwhelming on its own.

**Requirements:** You must have at least 2 cards in hand (1 to bank, 1 to discard after drawing). If you have fewer than 2 cards, the talent fizzles.

**Banked cards:** The banked card is out of the game for the remainder of the round. It goes to the Crate at round end when everything is reshuffled.

## 🏆 Scoring

VP is scored **immediately when a trick is won.** There is no end-of-round scoring.

| Dial Position | VP per Trick |
|--------------|-------------|
| **CROSS** | Cards in trick × 1 |
| **LEFT** | Cards in trick × 1 |
| **RIGHT** | Cards in trick × **2** |

A standard trick has 4 cards (one per player). A Quick Fix trick has 5 cards.

### VP Compression

**If the trick winner currently has the highest VP among all players and the dial is on RIGHT, they score ×1 instead of ×2.**

This prevents the leader from snowballing via the most lucrative trick position. The ×2 multiplier still applies for all other players — only the current VP leader is compressed.

**Ties:** If multiple players share the highest VP, none of them are considered "the leader" — VP compression does not apply to any of them.

**Why this exists:** Without compression, the player who takes an early lead disproportionately benefits from RIGHT tricks, creating a snowball effect. Compression keeps RIGHT dramatic for trailing players while gently capping the leader's acceleration. Simulation shows this reduces the Round 1 leader's win rate from ~43% to ~37% — still an advantage for playing well early, but not a lock.

**Example turn sequence:**

> Dial is on LEFT (×1). Alice leads Tinkerer-7. Bob follows with Tinkerer-3, Carol plays Tinkerer-9, Dave plays off-suit. Carol wins (highest Tinkerer). Carol scores 4 VP. Cards go to Crate. Dial rotates to RIGHT.
>
> Now at RIGHT (×2, low wins). Carol has 22 VP (leader). Alice has 18, Bob has 15, Dave has 12. Carol leads Super-Dupe-6. Alice plays Super-Dupe-2, Bob plays off-suit, Dave plays Super-Dupe-8. Alice wins (lowest Super-Dupe). Alice scores 4 × 2 = 8 VP (not the leader — full multiplier).
>
> Next RIGHT trick: Alice now has 26 VP (leader). She wins again — but scores 4 × 1 = 4 VP (VP compression applies). Cards go to Crate. Dial rotates to CROSS.

### Victory

**First player to reach 60 VP wins immediately** — even mid-round. (In practice, this will happen when a trick is scored, or when Purple's Pocket the Past triggers.)

**Tiebreaker:** If two players are tied at exactly 60 VP at the same moment (extraordinarily rare — only one player wins each trick), play one additional round. Highest VP after that round wins.

## Quick Reference Card

1. Trump? → Highest Trump wins

3. Otherwise → Highest of led faction wins

1. Score VP (cards in trick × multiplier; leader scores ×1 on RIGHT)

3. Rotate dial clockwise
4. Use Faction Talent (optional)
5. Lead next trick

- CROSS/LEFT → ×1, high wins
- RIGHT → ×2, **low wins** (×1 for VP leader)
- Win a trick → Rotate clockwise
- Red talent → Rotate back

| Faction | Talent | Effect |

## VP Economy Reference (For Playtesting)

These numbers are derived from 1,000 Monte Carlo simulated games using the v2.2 hybrid ruleset.

**Per round (confirmed by simulation):**
- ~7 tricks per round (32 cards dealt ÷ 4 per trick, minus occasional ties)
- RIGHT appears on roughly every 3rd trick (dial cycles through 3 positions)
- Average VP per trick: ~5.3 (accounting for ×2 on ~33% of tricks, reduced by VP compression on leaders)
- Average VP per player per round: **~9**

**Game length (confirmed):**
- Median: 5 rounds
- Estimated play time: 25–35 minutes

**RIGHT VP share:** ~42.6% of total VP comes from RIGHT-position tricks. VP compression reduces the leader's share of RIGHT VP without reducing drama for other players.

**Tuning levers if playtesting reveals issues:**

| If This Happens | Try This |
|----------------|----------|
| Games too short (3–4 rounds consistently) | Raise VP target to 70 |
| Games too long (7+ rounds) | Lower VP target to 50 |
| ×2 too swingy / feels "rigged" | Change RIGHT to ×1.5 (round up). This equalizes game-ending position to exactly 33% each. |
| ×2 not dramatic enough | Change to ×3 (dangerous but testable) |
| Dial feels too predictable | Test variable rotation: win with rank 0–5 = rotate 1, win with 6–10 = rotate 2 |
| Players forget to rotate | Add physical "ROTATE" token passed to trick winner |
| Talents rarely used | Make them automatic (not optional) |
| Talents too dominant | Restrict to ranks 6–10 only |
| Yellow talent confusing | Simplify to "draw 1 card from Vault" |
| VP compression too harsh on leader | Remove compression; increase VP target to 70 instead |
| VP compression not enough | Compress at CROSS too (leader always scores ×1) |

## 3-Player Variant: The Rotating Council

- No fixed partnerships. Free-for-all.
- Alliance Dial has 3 positions: **YOU, LEFT, RIGHT**
- When you win a trick, rotate to next position.
- **Scoring:** At RIGHT position, trick winner scores ×2. At YOU and LEFT, ×1.
- **Trick resolution:** RIGHT position = lowest of led faction wins. Otherwise, highest wins.
- **VP Compression** applies as in the 4-player game.
- **Castle's Blessing:** Last place draws 2 extra, keeps best 8.

## 5-Player Variant: The Unstable Coalition

- **2v2v1** format (two partnerships + one solo "Wild Card").
- Alliance Dial cycles through 4 positions: **CROSS / LEFT / RIGHT / SOLO**
- At SOLO: The trick winner has no partner. Score ×1.
- At RIGHT: ×2, low wins (as in standard game).
- Deal 8 cards each (40 cards dealt, 26 in Vault).
- **VP Compression** applies as in the 4-player game.
- **Castle's Blessing:** Last place draws 3, second-to-last draws 2 (same as 4p).

*Both variants are secondary designs. The 4-player game is the primary experience.*

## Designer's Note

Hamster High Council answers one question: *What if trick-taking had shifting alliances?*

The Alliance Dial isn't a gimmick — it's the game. Every trick, four questions:
- Who's my partner right now?
- Where will the dial be after this trick?
- Do I want to win and rotate toward RIGHT?
- Do I want to lose and keep the dial where it is?

Per-trick scoring means every alliance shift has immediate consequences. There's no waiting for round end to find out if the dial landed favorably. You score now. The dial moves now. Your partner changes now.

The ×2 multiplier on RIGHT creates genuine drama. Winning a 4-card trick at RIGHT scores 8 VP — double a normal trick. But RIGHT means *low wins*, so your 10-value powerhouse is suddenly your worst card. That inversion — where strength becomes weakness and weakness becomes strength — is the moment players remember.

VP compression ensures the leader can't ride RIGHT to a runaway victory. Everyone else still gets the full ×2 — only the player in first place is capped at ×1 on RIGHT. It's a light touch: the leader still wins RIGHT tricks, still rotates the dial, still gains points. They just gain them at the same rate as CROSS and LEFT. The drama of RIGHT stays intact for the players chasing the leader — and that's exactly where you want the drama.

Games disproportionately end on RIGHT (~42% of the time). That's not a bug. It means the game tends to conclude on its most dramatic trick type — the one where low wins and points double. The finale *earns* its tension.

Castle's Blessing keeps trailing players in the fight. It doesn't guarantee a comeback — you still need to play your cards well — but it ensures the player in last place has sharper tools heading into each round. The Council throws the underdog a bone. What the underdog does with it is up to them.

The trick-taking market is crowded. The dynamic partnership market is wide open.

Play hard. Watch the dial. Try not to eat the gavel.
