# Summit Scramble — V2 Rules Comparison

Three changes tested against 1,000 games per player count:

1. **Ability-finish rule** — if an ability empties your hand, you reach The Summit
2. **Cannon interrupt removed** — Confetti Cannons are now lead/follow only (Trip-Up is the sole interrupt)
3. **Revelation unrestricted** — Blue's ability targets any other player (no "fewer cards" gate)

---

## Revelation: From Weakest to Competitive

The single biggest improvement. Removing the "fewer cards" gate brought Blue in line with every other faction.

| Player Count | Revelation Before | Revelation After | Change |
|-------------|-------------------|------------------|--------|
| 3P          | 0.8/game          | **1.3/game**     | +63%   |
| 4P          | 0.9/game          | **1.5/game**     | +67%   |
| 5P          | 0.9/game          | **1.6/game**     | +78%   |

Revelation went from dead last to the **most-triggered ability at 5P** (tied with Rotation). The ability now fires in the endgame when it matters most — stealing a card from a near-winner is a legitimate defensive tool.

### 3-Player Win Rates

The spread narrowed from **5.6%** to **1.7%** — essentially balanced. The Revelation change likely helped P0 because the first player, who leads (spending cards), now has access to a disruption tool that works regardless of relative hand size.

## Cannon De-Interruption: Clean Removal

Confetti Cannons continue to appear as **regular formations** at healthy rates:

| Player Count | Cannon Formations (Before) | Cannon Formations (After) |
|-------------|---------------------------|--------------------------|
| 3P          | 4.3% of plays (759)       | 3.2% of plays (575)      |
| 4P          | 2.6% of plays (626)       | 1.8% of plays (425)      |
| 5P          | 1.4% of plays (400)       | 0.8% of plays (235)      |

Cannons dropped ~25-40% without the interrupt mechanic, which makes sense — they're no longer opportunistic "fire from anywhere" plays, they require winning initiative first. They're still present, still shed 4 cards, still trigger abilities. The play pattern shifted from "surprise interrupt" to "power lead" — a cleaner, more readable mechanic.

**Trip-Up frequency was unaffected** (the sole remaining interrupt):

| Player Count | Trip-Ups Before | Trip-Ups After |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------|
| 3P          | 0.81/game (60%) | 0.78/game (57%)|
| 4P          | 1.44/game (82%) | 1.41/game (81%)|
| 5P          | 2.02/game (92%) | 2.07/game (92%)|

Trip-Up is now the singular dramatic interrupt moment — which is exactly what you want.

## Stagnation: Still Zero

No stagnation detected in any configuration. The ability-finish rule eliminated the deadlock bug completely, and no new stalling patterns emerged.

---

## Summary

All three changes are net positives with no downsides detected:

| Change | Impact |
|--------|--------|
| Ability-finish rule | Eliminates deadlocks. Rare but essential. |
| Cannon de-interruption | Simpler rules, one fewer exception. Cannons still viable as formations. |
| Revelation unrestricted | Blue goes from weakest to competitive. 3P balance improves as a bonus. |

The game is tighter, simpler, and more balanced after these changes.
