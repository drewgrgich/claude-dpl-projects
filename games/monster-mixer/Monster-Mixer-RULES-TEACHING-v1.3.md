# Monster Mixer
### The Teaching Edition — v1.3 (The Best Version)

*One deck. One lineup. Endless "should I or shouldn't I?" moments.*

---

## WHAT YOU NEED

- **66 cards** (the Hamsters & Monsters deck)
- **Paper and pen** for scoring
- **2 to 5 players** (works best with 3–4)

---

## THE ONE-SENTENCE VERSION

Play cards into a shared row; builds runs of 3 to score big, but completing a run costs you — so trick opponents into finishing your runs for you.

---

## SETUP (30 seconds)

1. **Shuffle** the deck and place it face-down in the center.
2. **Deal 5 cards** to each player. Keep them secret.
3. Leave the rest as the **draw pile**.
4. **Create the lineup:** an empty row in the center where all cards go.
5. **Score track:** Draw a line on paper, mark 0 for everyone.

*That's it. No tokens, no modifiers, no special zones.*

---

## THE CARD — WHAT IT DOES

Each card has a **suit** (Fire 🔥, Shadow 🌑, Frost ❄️, or Storm ⚡) and a **rank** (1–11). Only the suit matters for runs.

---

## YOUR TURN — THREE STEPS

On your turn, do these in order:

**Step 1 — Draw.** Draw 1 card from the draw pile.

**Step 2 — Place.** Choose one card from your hand and place it **anywhere** in the lineup — left end, right end, or between any two cards. The lineup is always read left-to-right.

**Step 3 — Score.** Your placed card scores immediately:

| It lands... | You score |
|---|---|
| No same-suit neighbors (isolated) | **3 points** |
| Next to 1 same-suit card (run of 2) | **7 points** |
| Next to 2 same-suit cards (run of 3) | **12 points** |

That's your whole turn. Pass to the left.

---

## THE BOUNCER — THE twist THAT MAKES IT FUN

Here's the thing: **completing a run of 3 has a cost.**

When your card creates a run of exactly 3:

1. You **score 12 points** ✓
2. Your card is **removed** from the lineup
3. You **lose 3 points** (penalty)
4. Net: **you keep 9 points.** The two remaining cards stay as a run of 2.

**The run of 2 is now exposed** — anyone who extends it will trigger the Bouncer again.

### The Bouncer Budget

Each player can only trigger the Bouncer **once per round**. After that, you *cannot* play a card that would complete a run of 3 — even if you try, the game won't let you. (You'll have to play something else.)

This creates the core tension: *"I want to score this big, but I don't want to be the one who set it up for my opponent."*

---

## WHAT DOES "AROUND" LOOK LIKE? — EXAMPLES

The lineup is a row of cards, face-up. You can insert between any two, or at either end.

```
Example A — Empty lineup
[  ]

You play a Fire card to the right end:
[ Fire 7 ]   → Isolated  → 3 points ✓
```

```
Example B — Existing lineup
[ Fire 7 | Shadow 3 | Frost 9 | Storm 2 ]

You play Fire 5 to the LEFT of the Fire 7:
[ Fire 5 | Fire 7 | Shadow 3 | Frost 9 | Storm 2 ]
  Creates a run of 2  →  7 points ✓
```

```
Example C — Completing a run of 3 (Bouncer!)
[ Fire 5 | Fire 7 | Shadow 3 | Fire 2 | Frost 9 ]

You play Fire 9 at the RIGHT end of the Fire run:
[ Fire 5 | Fire 7 | Fire 9 | Shadow 3 | Frost 9 ]
  Run of 3!  →  +12 pts, card discarded, -3 pts
  Lineup now: [ Fire 5 | Fire 7 | Shadow 3 | Frost 9 ]
  Net for you: +9 points. The run resets to 2 cards.
```

---

## ROUNDS — HOW THE GAME ENDS

A **round** = everyone plays all 5 cards from their hand.

After all hands are empty, check the draw pile:

- If there are **enough cards to deal everyone 5 more**, a new round begins. (Everyone gets 5 new cards, Bouncer budgets reset.)
- If not, **the game ends.** Highest score wins.

---

## QUICK REFERENCE

```
SCORING (per card you play):
  Isolated          →  3 pts
  Run of 2          →  7 pts
  Run of 3          → 12 pts, then card discarded and -3 penalty
                      (net +9 pts)

BOUNCER BUDGET: 1 per player per round
  Once you've triggered the Bouncer, you can't trigger it again until the next round.

SCORING AT END: Most points wins.
```

---

## TWO-PLAYER VARIANT

Deal **7 cards each**. Play one round (14 total turns). No refill — this is the whole game. Each player gets **2 Bouncers** instead of 1.

*Faster, meaner, more tactical.*

---

## WHY IS THIS FUN?

The lineup is **shared** — every card you place affects what everyone else can do. You can:

- **Wall off** a run you don't want others to complete
- **Extend** an opponent's run (gives them 7 points safely, but you're setting them up to be Bouncer'd)
- **Split** an existing run by inserting a different suit between two cards
- **Save your Bouncer** for a moment when completing a run gives you a strategic advantage

The game rewards **reading the table** and **timing** as much as card luck.

---

## COMMON QUESTIONS

**Q: Can I place a card between two cards of the same suit?**  
A: Yes. If it breaks a run, that's allowed. You're splitting their run.

**Q: What if all my cards would complete a run of 3, but I've already used my Bouncer?**  
A: You must play the card that hurts you the least. (Usually this means playing it isolated or as part of a run of 2, even if that's not ideal.)

**Q: Does the discarded card go to my hand, the draw pile, or somewhere else?**  
A: It's out of play for the rest of the game. Gone.

**Q: Can I look at the lineup whenever I want?**  
A: Yes — it's always face-up and public. Studying it is half the game.

---

## DESIGNER NOTES

Monster Mixer was designed to solve a specific problem: most shared-lineup games feel like multiplayer solitaire. Either you have the right card or you don't. The Bouncer budget transforms this — even when you *have* the right card, you have to choose whether this is the right moment. That micro-decision, multiplied across 5–15 turns per player, creates genuine strategy.

**What makes a good Monster Mixer player:**
- Watching what suits others are collecting
- Knowing when to be the Bouncer victim (net +9 is still good)
- Saving your Bouncer for a situation where completing a run also disrupts an opponent's plan
