# Monster Mixer v1.3 — Game Rules
**Designers:** Morgan, Jordan, Casey (Designer Corps)
**Iteration:** v1.3 (anywhere insertion + bouncer cap)
**Playtime:** 15–20 minutes
**Players:** 2–5
**Deck:** 66-card Hamsters & Monsters deck

---

## ONE SENTENCE

Place cards anywhere in a shared lineup to build runs; completing a run of 3 scores big but triggers the Bouncer — so spend your one Bouncer per round wisely and force opponents to finish what you started.

---

## SUMMARY

**Monster Mixer** is a shared-lineup combo game. Every card you play goes into a public center row at a position of your choosing. Your card scores instantly: isolated = 3 pts, in a run of 2 = 7 pts, in a run of 3 = 12 pts. But completing a run of 3 triggers the **Bouncer** — you discard the card, pay a -3 penalty, and net +9. Each round, you can trigger the Bouncer at most once. Build opponents' runs to score safely; trap them into using their Bouncer budget on your terms.

---

## SETUP

1. **Shuffle** the deck to create the face-down **draw pile** in the center.
2. **Deal 5 cards** face-down to each player — your **hand** (private).
3. Create the **lineup**: a single face-up row in the center, empty.
4. **Score track**: shared paper or shared device. All players start at **0**.

---

## THE LINEUP

The lineup is a **shared, public row** of face-up cards, read left-to-right. Cards can be inserted **anywhere** — left end, right end, or between any two cards. The only way cards leave the lineup is via the **Bouncer rule**.

---

## YOUR TURN

On your turn, play **1 card** from your hand to any position in the lineup. That's your entire action.

---

## SCORING (INSTANTANEOUS)

When you place your card, it scores **immediately** based on the run it now belongs to. A **run** = two or more consecutive cards of the same suit. An **isolated** card has no same-suit neighbors.

| Your placed card creates a run of... | It scores... |
|---|---|
| 1 (isolated) | **3 pts** |
| 2 | **7 pts** |
| 3 | **12 pts** |

Only your **newly placed card** scores. Cards already in the lineup do not retroactively change.

---

## THE BOUNCER RULE

If your placed card creates a **run of exactly 3**, the Bouncer activates for **you**:

1. **Score 12 points** ✓ (run of 3 = 12 pts)
2. **Discard that card** from the lineup.
3. **Pay a -3 penalty** (subtract 3 from your total).
4. **Net result: +9 points.** The run resets to 2 cards.

**Bouncer budget:** Each player can trigger the Bouncer **once per round**. Once you've used your budget, placing a card that would complete a run of 3 is not allowed — the AI will automatically choose a different play. (If all your cards would complete a run of 3, you must play the one that minimises damage.)

---

## ROUND STRUCTURE

A **round** = all players play all 5 cards from their hands. Each player's Bouncer budget resets at the start of each round.

**Phase 1 — Play:** Clockwise from the dealer, each player plays 1 card per turn. Continue until all hands are empty.

**Phase 2 — Refresh:** Count the remaining cards in the draw pile.
- If **5×N cards remain** (N = number of players), each player draws 5 new cards and a new round begins.
- Otherwise, the game ends after scoring this final round.

---

## WINNING

After the final round, the player with the **most points** wins. Tie: most cards placed across all rounds (rewards activity; if still tied, co-winners).

---

## 2-PLAYER VARIANT

Deal **7 cards each** (14 total). Play one round of 14 turns. No refresh — this is the entire game. Faster, more tactical, higher Bouncer pressure (shorter lineup means runs are rarer and more valuable). Each player has a budget of **2 Bouncers** for the single round.

---

## SCORING QUICK REFERENCE

```
When you place your card and it creates a run of N same-suit cards:
  N = 1 (isolated)    →   3 pts
  N = 2               →   7 pts
  N = 3               →  12 pts, card discarded, -3 penalty  (net +9)
```

---

## WHY V1.3 WORKS (VS V1.1)

**v1.1 problem:** Append-only placement meant most turns were structurally isolated (~48% forced). The Bouncer was a threat that rarely materialized because runs were too hard to build.

**v1.3 fix:**
- **Anywhere insertion** means runs form naturally — forced-iso drops from 48% to 0%
- **Bouncer cap** prevents bouncer chains (18.5% bouncer rate vs 85% in unconstrained anywhere) and creates a strategic budget — "do I complete this run now or save my Bouncer for something bigger?"
- The classic "don't play red!" dynamic is preserved and enhanced — now it also means "I'm choosing not to use my Bouncer on your run"
