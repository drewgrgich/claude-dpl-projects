# Monster Mixer — Game Rules v1.0
**Designers:** Morgan, Jordan, Casey (Designer Corps)
**Playtime:** 18–22 minutes
**Players:** 2–5
**Deck:** 66-card Hamsters & Monsters deck

---

## ONE SENTENCE

Place cards into a shared lineup; runs score big but if YOU complete a run of 3, the bouncers fine you — so build others' runs and force them to finish what you started.

---

## SUMMARY

**Monster Mixer** is a shared-tableau combo game. Every card you play goes into a public center row. Your card scores instantly: isolated = 3 pts, in a run of 2 = 7 pts, in a run of 3 = 12 pts. But if your card creates a run of 3, the bouncers fine you — you keep the 12 pts but discard that card and lose 5 pts. The run resets to 2. Build your opponents' runs to score points safely; trap them into completing your runs and wiping your mistakes.

---

## SETUP

1. **Shuffle** the deck to create the face-down **draw pile** in the center.
2. **Deal 5 cards** face-down to each player — your **hand** (private).
3. Create the **lineup**: a single face-up row in the center, empty.
4. **Score track**: shared paper or shared device. All players start at **0**.

---

## THE LINEUP

The lineup is a **shared, public row** of face-up cards, read left-to-right. Every card you play goes to the **rightmost end**. Cards are never rearranged. The only way cards leave the lineup is via the **bouncer rule** (see below).

---

## YOUR TURN

On your turn, play **1 card** from your hand to the right end of the lineup. That's your entire action.

---

## SCORING (INSTANTANEOUS)

When you place your card, it scores **immediately** based on the run it now belongs to. A **run** = two or more consecutive cards of the same suit. An **isolated** card has no same-suit neighbors.

| Your placed card creates a run of... | It scores... |
|---|---|
| 1 (isolated) | **3 pts** |
| 2 | **7 pts** |
| 3 or more | **12 pts** |

Only your **newly placed card** scores. Cards already in the lineup do not retroactively change.

**Quick reference:**
- Isolated = 3 pts
- Run of 2 = 7 pts
- Run of 3+ = 12 pts (capped; run of 3 and run of 4 score the same)

---

## THE BOUNCER RULE

If your placed card creates a **run of exactly 3** (it becomes the third consecutive card of the same suit):

1. **You score 12 points** ✓ (run of 3 = 12 pts)
2. **Discard that card** from the lineup.
3. **Pay a -5 penalty** (subtract 5 from your total).
4. The remaining cards — now a run of 2 — **stay in the lineup**.

**Net result of triggering bouncer: +7 points.** Not a disaster, but not the big payday you hoped for. The run resets to 2 cards, and whoever extends it next earns 7 pts.

**Key insight:** Bouncers always trigger at run of 3 — a run can never grow past 3. Every run of 2 in the lineup is one card away from bouncer. This creates relentless pressure to extend runs (for points) while avoiding being the one who extends them (reset + penalty).

**Strategic summary:**
- Build your opponents' runs to score 7 pts safely
- Extend your own runs to 2 only, then force someone else to complete it
- If forced to complete a run to 3: you net +7 (12 pts - 5 penalty) and your card is gone — the run resets and the next extender scores 7

---

## ROUND STRUCTURE

A **round** = all players play all 5 cards from their hands.

**Phase 1 — Play:** Clockwise from the dealer, each player plays 1 card per turn. Continue until all hands are empty.

**Phase 2 — Refresh:** Count the remaining cards in the draw pile.
- If **5×N cards remain** (N = number of players), each player draws 5 new cards and a new round begins.
- Otherwise, the game ends after scoring this final round.

**Deck depletion examples:**
- **5 players:** 2 full rounds (50 cards dealt) + 16 leftover → game ends after Round 2.
- **4 players:** 3 full rounds (60 cards dealt) + 6 leftover → game ends after Round 3.
- **3 players:** 4 full rounds (60 cards dealt) + 6 leftover → game ends after Round 4.

---

## WINNING

After the final round, the player with the **most points** wins. Tie: most cards placed across all rounds (rewards activity; if still tied, co-winners).

---

## 2-PLAYER VARIANT

Deal **7 cards each** (14 total). Play one round of 14 turns. No refresh — this is the entire game. Faster, more tactical, higher bouncer pressure (shorter lineup means runs are rarer and more valuable).

---

## SCORING QUICK REFERENCE

```
When you place your card and it creates a run of N same-suit cards:
  N = 1 (isolated)    →   3 pts
  N = 2               →   7 pts
  N = 3               →  12 pts, card discarded, -5 penalty  (net +7)
  N = 4+              → impossible by bouncer rule
```

## DESIGNER NOTES

### The Core Tension
Every placement is a micro-decision: do I play it safe (isolated = 3) or extend a run (7 pts, but I might be forced to complete it next turn)? The lineup is public, so you can see what others are building toward. The question is never "can I build this run?" — it's "who will be the one to finish it?"

### Why It Works at the Table
- The lineup is **instantly readable** — no reference lookup, no ambiguous states
- Scoring is **immediate and visible** — you see the points appear on the track when you place
- The bouncer rule is **visually dramatic** — when it triggers, a card physically leaves the lineup
- "Don't play red!" is a natural table conversation that requires no rules explanation
- No one is ever "out of the game" — scores stay close because triggering bouncer doesn't cascade

### Inspirations
- **No Thanks!** — the "don't take the third in a row" tension
- **Coloretto** — building toward sets while knowing when to stop
- **Sushi Go!** — combo scoring from card adjacency
- **Seasons** — the shared pool that everyone influences

### Intended Audience
Players who enjoy light strategy games with readable, shared board states. Groups who like emergent table talk ("Are you going to play red? I have a red I NEED you to take..."). Works best at 3–4 players; still fun at 2 and 5 but the dynamics shift.

### Manufacturing Notes
- **66-card deck only** — no accessories, no tokens required
- Score pad: any lined paper works (columns for player names, running total)
- Box: standard H&M tuck box dimensions
- Optional: small bouncer reminder card (the scoring table fits on an index card)

## 2P–5P SCALING

| Players | Hand Size | Rounds (approx.) | Lineup Length | Bouncer Pressure |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 2 | 7 (no refill) | 1 | 14 cards | Very high |
| 3 | 5 | 4 | ~20 cards | Medium-high |
| 4 | 5 | 3 | ~20 cards | Medium |
| 5 | 5 | 2 | ~25 cards | Medium (fast rounds) |

**2-player:** Strategic, tight, every card matters. 14-card lineup means runs are rare and valuable. No comeback mechanic needed — it's just 14 decisions.

**3-player:** Most chaotic and social. Lineup builds fast. Highest replay variety.

**4-player:** Cleanest flow. Natural round structure, good balance of runs and isolated cards.

**5-player:** Fast rounds. Bouncer triggers frequently. Good party-game energy.

## COMPARISON TO EXISTING H&M TITLES

| | Bid Brawl | Rampage Run | The Burrow | Monster Mixer |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Core mechanic** | Blind bidding + feeding | Press-your-luck brinkmanship | Wheel drafting + personal grid | Shared combo tableau |
| **Hidden info** | Bids (yes) | Cards (yes) | Draft positions (no) | Hands (yes) |
| **Shared state** | No (cards feed forward) | Partial (shared rampage pile) | No (personal grid) | Yes (lineup affects all) |
| **Player elimination** | No | No | No | No |
| **Setup time** | Medium | Low | High | Low |
| **Luck/skill split** | 60/40 | 70/30 | 50/50 | 55/45 |
| **Table talk type** | Strategic bargaining | Betting tension | Quiet optimization | "Don't play red!" |
| **Catch-up mechanic** | Underdog push | None (broken) | None | Implicit (short rounds) |

Monster Mixer is the only H&M game where the **board state is fully shared and simultaneously affects all players' decisions**. In Bid Brawl, your bid only affects the feeding. In Rampage Run, you're betting against the pile. In The Burrow, your grid is yours alone. Monster Mixer's lineup is the first true **shared public resource** in the H&M catalog.
