## Privacy Rules — MUST FOLLOW

These are hard boundaries. No exceptions without Drew's explicit approval.

- **Danny's deal terms:** No specific dollar amounts, royalty percentages, or profit-share thresholds. Use ratios or ranges ("roughly a third of pre-launch budget")
- **Danny's name:** Only after contract is signed AND Danny agrees to be named publicly
- **Budget specifics:** Ranges are fine ("low five figures"). Exact line items are not.
- **Manufacturer quotes:** Share the process and decision framework, not exact numbers
- **Drew's personal finances:** Off limits entirely
- **Game name:** Use "Subject 42" until the reveal (target: around Week 10-15)

**What IS okay to share:**
- The structure of decisions (flat fee vs. royalty as concepts)
- Conversion rates, email list sizes, ad performance metrics
- General budget categories and proportions
- Playtest feedback and game design insights
- The emotional reality of the process

## Platform Strategy

- **Substack** — primary home. Publish here first. These subscribers feed the Kickstarter email funnel.
- **Medium** — cross-post within 24-48 hours. Better SEO, 3x traffic, AI search visibility.
- **Tags for Medium:** #kickstarter #gamedesign #AI #indiegames #tabletopgames

## Voice

Read `voice-guide.md` before drafting any article. Key traits:
- Conversational authority — confident but loose
- Parenthetical humor — asides that undercut just enough
- Fragments as punctuation — short beats after longer sentences
- Self-aware honesty — failures are content, not embarrassments
- Metaphors that land and leave — one image, move on
- Direct address to reader — pull them into the room

## Writing Process

1. Check `series-outline.md` for the current week's topic and content type
2. Read `voice-guide.md` to calibrate tone
3. Draft in `drafts/week-XX-slug.md`
4. Drew edits, revises, makes it his own
5. After publishing, move final version to `published/`
6. Update `series-outline.md` status
